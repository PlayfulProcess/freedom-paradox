# Social Working: An Analeptic Deck

*Status: Grammar imported. Not yet drafted as prose.*

**Working thesis:** "The mainstream has never run clean." Social work as a profession has been complicit in the colonial project from its founding. But social working — the verb, the practice of communities caring for each other — predates and exceeds the profession.

**Structure:** 76 cards across 8 suits:
1. **The Standard Account** — Canonical figures (Addams, Richmond, Pratt)
2. **Erased Voices** — Marginalized practitioners (Ida B. Wells, Du Bois, Maggie Walker)
3. **Sanitized Icons** — Figures stripped of complexity
4. **The Cracks** — Indigenous/spiritual/everyday roles
5. **Contemporary Witnesses** — Alternative practitioners (Akomolafe, Andreotti)
6. **The Wicked History** — Oz canon deconstructed
7. **The Rest of the Worlds** — More-than-human social working
8. **Places of Care** — Historical sites of mutual aid

**Connection:** The MSW professional companion. The critical examination of the professional field.
