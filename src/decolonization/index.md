# Decolonization: Body, Mind, Spirit, Collective, Planet

*Status: Grammar imported, two source grammars. Not yet drafted as prose.*

**Working thesis:** Modernity is dying and needs hospicing — not with despair or triumph, but wisdom (Andreotti). Decolonization is not a destination but an ongoing practice.

**Structure:**
- **Part I: The Tree of Coloniality** — Roots (six separations), Trunk (five addictions), Canopy (five domains), Rotten Fruits, Seeds
- **Part II: Five Domains** — Body (9), Mind (10), Spirit (10), Collective (10), Planet (8)
- **Supplementary: Decolonizing Childhood** — Thresholds, Learning, Sexual Education, Somatic/DBT

**Connection:** The decolonial companion to Grammars of the Living World. Where Grammars diagnoses the colonial reflex abstractly, this book applies it concretely across every domain of life.
