The Freedom Paradox Project

Four books about power, responsibility, and the stories that hold them together
PlayfulProcess

This is an open-source book project about the gap between human capacity and human responsibility. The gap has been widening for thirteen thousand years. It is now approaching the boundary conditions of the game board.

The books trace the problem from four angles: the political economy of open source and AI, the biology of cooperation, the practical architecture of storytelling, and the narrative traditions that encode adaptive wisdom. Each book works alone. Together they form the full argument.

Book 1: The Freedom Paradox

16 chapters + epilogue · ~85,000 words · Complete first draft
Open source — a forty-year freedom movement — confronts genuine civilizational risk when applied to AI. The diagnosis: power without responsibility.

Book 2: The Species That Got Fire

9 chapters · ~21,000 words · Draft in progress
Extraction wins single rounds. Cooperation wins iterated games. Grammars are the encoding mechanism for cooperative strategy. The framework.

Book 3: Working Architecture

12 chapters · ~20,000 words · Complete first draft
A practical manual about stories that work. Seven tellable stories, sixteen book recommendations, ten games, four seasonal rituals. For anyone who tells stories to anyone else.

Book 4: The Species That Tells Stories

3 of 7 chapters · ~13,000 words · Draft in progress
We are the species that tells stories to its children. Love is the container. The container is disappearing. The narrative heart.
