What does "free" mean when the software costs $300 million to run? Click each rung to expand.

The Book's Argument

Open source freed code when code was the scarce resource. AI shifted scarcity to compute. "Open weights" gives you the recipe but not the kitchen. The freedom to fork is meaningless without the resources to run what you've forked. The compute ladder shows who can actually exercise the Four Freedoms in the AI era — and who cannot.

The gap between the bottom rung ($1,600) and the top ($125 billion/year) is not narrowing. It is widening exponentially. When four companies spend more on AI infrastructure than the GDP of all but ~20 countries, "openness" becomes a question of physics, not philosophy.
