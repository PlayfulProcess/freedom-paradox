The Cost of "Open"

Estimated training cost for AI models over time — the new barrier to entry

Book argument (Ch. 2, 9, 12): Open source freed code when code was the scarce resource. AI shifted scarcity to compute. "Open weights" gives you the recipe but not the kitchen. Retraining Llama 3 405B costs ~$30M. The freedom to fork is meaningless without the resources to run what you've forked.

Sources: Press reports, analyst estimates. All figures approximate. [VERIFY]

Who Can Afford to Play

Annual AI compute spending by company — the new concentration of power

The $380B question: Four companies will spend more on AI infrastructure in 2025 than the GDP of most countries. Approaching $700B in 2026. Even "open" AI (Meta's Llama) is produced by organizations with $72B compute budgets. The commons requires a cathedral to enter.

The Self-Hosting Ladder

Hardware cost to run "free" open-weight AI models yourself

The access gradient: An 8B model on a $1,600 GPU is genuinely accessible. A 405B model requires $150K+. Retraining a frontier model requires $300M+. "Open" exists on a spectrum from "hobbyist accessible" to "nation-state only."

The Surveillance Cost Curve

Amodei's estimated cost trajectory for AI-powered mass domestic surveillance

Book argument (Ch. 1, 10, 12): When surveillance costs drop below $1B, open-weight models become a governance crisis. The Dwarkesh Problem meets the cost curve: if capable models are freely available AND the compute to run them is affordable, Anthropic's ethical commitments become irrelevant. Anyone can strip guardrails.

The Paid vs. Unpaid Divide

Critical infrastructure gets funded. Everything else is volunteer labor.

Book argument (Ch. 5, 13): The "bazaar" (Raymond) was always more like a "stadium" (Eghbal) — a few maintainers performing for many watchers. When the performers burn out, critical infrastructure fails (Heartbleed, xz-utils, Log4j).

The Gender Bias in Code Review

PR acceptance rates for outsiders: gender-neutral vs. identifiable profiles

Corrected data from Terrell et al. 2017: For outside contributors, women's PRs are accepted at similar rates to men's when profiles are gender-neutral (71.8% vs 73.8%). When gender is identifiable, women's rate drops 9 points to 62.5% while men's stays flat. The bias emerges at the identity layer, not the code layer.

Source: Terrell et al. 2017, PeerJ CS. 3M+ PRs, 330K+ users.

Governance Models

How power is distributed in major open-source projects

Book argument (Ch. 14–15): Ostrom's principles for governing commons included boundaries and sanctions. Open source resisted both. The result: corporate governance dressed in community language. Ghost and PostgreSQL show alternatives exist.

Methodological Honesty

This dashboard uses the best publicly available data, but every metric here carries caveats. Gender inference from names is reductive and Western-biased. Geographic data is self-reported. "Inclusivity" cannot be reduced to a single number. Survey data is self-selected toward English-speaking professional developers.

The point is not precision but pattern: open source creates enormous value, distributes it asymmetrically, reproduces existing demographic inequalities, and concentrates governance power despite its rhetoric of openness. These patterns are robust even when individual numbers are approximate.
