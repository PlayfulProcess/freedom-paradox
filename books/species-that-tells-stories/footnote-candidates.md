## Summary: Highest-Priority Moves

The chapters with the most footnote candidates (and therefore the most reading-flow improvement available):

1. **ch01** (Biological Hypothesis) -- 18+ candidates. The heaviest. Every researcher gets a full institutional bio, every study gets sample sizes, every theory gets its formal name. The Feldman paragraph (line 77), the Schore passage (line 95), and the polyvagal critique section (lines 111-137) are the biggest wins.

2. **ch03** (What the Old Stories Knew) -- 17+ candidates. The second heaviest. The songlines dating studies (line 85), the Esalen/Roth extraction genealogy (lines 211-213), and the six-tradition rapid-fire passage (line 213) are the biggest wins.

3. **ch04** (The First Fire) -- 16+ candidates. The acacia/kudu numbers (line 47), the five feedback loops section (lines 63-71, which is five statistics in five paragraphs), and the cultural prisoner's dilemma framework dump (lines 83-97) are the biggest wins.

4. **ch02** (Darkness Is the Medicine) -- 10+ candidates. The Foa/Kozak attribution (line 17), the Pennebaker protocol details (line 63), the Cohen's d (line 67), and the five-framework summary sentence (line 107) are the biggest wins.

5. **intro** -- 7 candidates. The La Brea study details (line 9) and the CO2 figures (line 21) are the biggest wins.

The cleanest chapters are **ch05** (Prometheus Problem), **ch07** (What Grammars Are), and **ch10** (Adaptive Wager) -- these already read fluidly because they work through narrative and argument rather than citation.
