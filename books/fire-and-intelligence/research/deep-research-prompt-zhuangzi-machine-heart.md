# Deep Research Prompt: Zhuangzi's "Machine Heart" (Ji Xin) and AI

Deep research on the Daoist concept of ji xin (機心, "machine heart") from the Zhuangzi:

- The original passage (Chapter 12, "Heaven and Earth"): the old farmer who refuses a well-sweep because "Where there are machines, there are bound to be machine worries; where there are machine worries, there are bound to be machine hearts"
- Scholarly translations and commentary (A.C. Graham, Burton Watson, Brook Ziporyn)
- The concept: technology doesn't merely change what we do; it changes what we ARE
- Connection to Heidegger's Enframing (Gestell) — both argue technology transforms the practitioner's consciousness
- Connection to Ellul's autonomous technique
- Application to AI: does using AI tools produce "machine hearts"? Does prompt engineering create ji xin?
- The Daoist alternative: wu wei (non-action), ziran (naturalness), the "uncarved block" as counter to machine-mind
