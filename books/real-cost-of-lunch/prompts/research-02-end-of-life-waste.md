# Research Prompt 02: End of Life — Food Waste, Composting, and Packaging Reality

Deep research on what happens to food and food packaging AFTER consumption:

1. **Food waste scale and location** — UNEP Food Waste Index 2024. How much food is wasted globally? At which stage (farm, processing, retail, household)? How does this vary by country and income level?

2. **Landfill methane from food waste** — EPA LMOP data. How much methane does decomposing food produce in anaerobic landfill conditions? Compared to other methane sources? Paul Hawken/Drawdown ranking food waste as #1 climate solution — is that accurate?

3. **Composting science** — home composting vs. municipal/industrial composting vs. anaerobic digestion vs. vermicomposting. What actually happens chemically? Carbon sequestration potential of compost. What can and can't be composted? How much does composting reduce emissions vs. landfill?

4. **"Compostable" packaging reality** — BPI certification, EN 13432 standard. What does "compostable" actually mean? Does compostable packaging decompose in home compost? In landfill? How many cities have industrial composting infrastructure? The gap between the label and reality.

5. **Packaging life cycle analysis** — glass vs. aluminum vs. plastic (PET, HDPE, LDPE, PP) vs. paper/cardboard vs. multi-layer (TetraPak, chip bags, pouches). Actual recycling rates by material and by country. Which packaging types are truly circular? Which are recycling theater?

6. **Deposit-return systems** — Germany's Pfand, Scandinavian systems, Oregon's bottle bill. What recycling rates do they achieve vs. curbside? Cost-effectiveness?

7. **Microplastics in food contact materials** — 2024-2026 research on microplastic shedding from food packaging, water bottles, takeout containers. Health implications?

8. **"Best before" vs. "use by"** — how date labeling drives food waste. Policy experiments to change labeling (UK, EU, US). Impact on waste reduction?
