# Research Prompt 04: Supply Chain Deep Dives — Cocoa, Coffee, and Palm Oil

Deep research on the three most ethically complex commodity supply chains in the food system:

## Cocoa / Chocolate
- Scale of child labor in West African cocoa: Tulane University monitoring reports (latest available). How many children? What kind of work? Has it improved or worsened?
- The Harkin-Engel Protocol (2001) — industry voluntary commitment to end child labor. 25 years later, what happened?
- Agroforestry vs. monoculture cocoa: environmental and social outcomes. Which companies/cooperatives use agroforestry?
- Fair trade cocoa: does the certification actually improve farmer income? Tony's Chocolonely model vs. traditional fair trade vs. direct trade. Critiques of fair trade certification
- Bean-to-bar movement: does small-batch, single-origin chocolate solve the structural problems or just create premium products for wealthy consumers?
- Companies to examine: Cadbury/Mondelez, Mars, Nestle, Tony's Chocolonely, Taza, Dandelion, Alter Eco

## Coffee
- World's second most traded commodity (or is it?). Scale of the industry, number of smallholders
- Shade-grown vs. sun-grown: biodiversity impact, yield trade-offs, bird habitat
- Fair trade vs. direct trade vs. conventional: price premiums, who captures the value?
- The specialty coffee paradox: $6 lattes in Brooklyn, $2/day for the farmer in Ethiopia
- Coffee and deforestation: which origins are driving forest loss?
- Water footprint of coffee (one of the highest per-cup of any beverage)
- Climate change threatening coffee-growing regions — what projections exist?

## Palm Oil
- The "in everything" problem: what percentage of supermarket products contain palm oil?
- Indonesian and Malaysian deforestation: scale, rate, orangutan/biodiversity impact
- RSPO (Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil) certification: does it work? Critiques, compliance rates, audit problems
- Palm oil vs. alternatives: the counterintuitive argument that palm oil is actually the MOST land-efficient vegetable oil — banning it could increase total deforestation
- Smallholder vs. industrial plantation palm oil: different supply chains, different impacts
- The no-palm-oil movement vs. sustainable palm oil — which approach is more effective?
