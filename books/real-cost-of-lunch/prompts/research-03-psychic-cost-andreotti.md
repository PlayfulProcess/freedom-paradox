# Research Prompt 03: The Psychic Cost — Eco-Anxiety, Decision Fatigue, and Hospicing the Ethical Consumer

Deep research on the mental health and psycho-social dimensions of sustainable food choices:

1. **Eco-anxiety as clinical reality** — APA recognition and reports on climate psychology. Hickman et al. (2021) Lancet Planetary Health survey of 10,000 young people across 10 countries (75% said "the future is frightening"). Susan Clayton's research on climate anxiety. Is eco-anxiety adaptive or pathological? Both?

2. **Decision fatigue in food** — Barry Schwartz's *Paradox of Choice* applied to the grocery aisle. Choice overload research. How many food decisions does the average person make per day? (Brian Wansink estimated 200+, though his research is controversial). The cognitive cost of perpetual ethical optimization.

3. **Orthorexia nervosa** — Steven Bratman's original concept. When "clean eating" or "ethical eating" becomes pathological. Prevalence data, especially in sustainability-conscious populations. The overlap between food anxiety and eco-anxiety. Current diagnostic debates.

4. **Vanessa Andreotti's framework** — *Hospicing Modernity* (2021). Key concepts: "soft reform" vs. structural facing. The violence of forcing people to "face the wall" (see the full picture of harm) without the metabolic capacity to process what they see. Complicity without paralysis. The difference between guilt and accountability. How her framework applies to ethical consumption specifically.

5. **Joanna Macy and the Work That Reconnects** — *Active Hope* framework. Grief as mobilizing force vs. guilt as paralyzing force. The spiral: gratitude, honoring pain, seeing with new eyes, going forth. How does this apply to food grief specifically?

6. **Glenn Albrecht** — solastalgia, psychoterratic distress, the emotional vocabulary for ecological loss. His taxonomy of earth emotions. Relevance to food systems (watching your local farmland become subdivisions, watching fisheries collapse).

7. **Moral injury in everyday consumption** — the psychological concept of moral injury (originally military) applied to civilian life. Knowing your coffee involved exploitation, your chocolate involved child labor, and choosing to consume it anyway. The impossible bind of ethical consumption under extractive capitalism.

8. **Bayo Akomolafe** — "the times are urgent, let us slow down." The radical practice of not-solving. How does this apply to food choices? The difference between paralysis and pause.

9. **Burnout of "conscious consumerism"** — research on sustainability fatigue, compassion fatigue applied to consumption. When awareness becomes a burden rather than a resource.

10. **What actually helps** — contemplative practices for holding eco-grief (mindfulness, nature-based practices). Community processing vs. individual burden. Ritual as metabolic capacity builder. The difference between hope and optimism (Macy, Solnit).
