# Research Prompt 01: Food Environmental Impact Data Backbone

Deep research on the foundational datasets for food environmental impact, focusing on the most current and comprehensive sources:

1. **Poore & Nemecek (2018) Science meta-analysis** — 38,700 farms, 119 countries, 40 food products. What are the key findings? Land use, GHG emissions, water use, eutrophication per food product. What are the most impactful foods and why? Any updates or critiques of this dataset since 2018?

2. **Our World in Data / Hannah Ritchie** — her food section data visualizations and her 2024 book *Not the End of the World*. What are her key arguments about food and environment? How does she challenge common assumptions?

3. **Water Footprint Network** — green, blue, and grey water footprints per food. Which foods are most water-intensive? How does context (where it's grown) change the water cost?

4. **IPCC AR6 food systems chapter** — what does the latest IPCC say about food's contribution to climate change? What mitigation strategies does it recommend?

5. **EAT-Lancet Commission planetary health diet** — what is it? What does it recommend? Critiques (cultural bias, feasibility, equity concerns)?

6. **NOVA food classification** — ultra-processed food categorization. How does processing level correlate with environmental impact? The Monteiro group's research.

7. **FAO FAOSTAT** — global food production, trade, and land use trends 2020-2026. What's changing?

8. **Key finding**: for each major food category (beef, chicken, dairy, rice, wheat, soy, palm oil, coffee, chocolate, fruits, vegetables), what are the per-kg carbon, water, and land use figures? How much do they vary by production method and geography?
