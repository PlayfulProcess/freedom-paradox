# Kant Audit — Revision Plan and v5 Manuscript

*2026-04-22 · transcendental critique of the epistemic-and-repair plan and the chapters it governs*

---

## Preface

The book under audit is a philosophical memoir that makes, by turns, theoretical claims (about intelligence, alignment, the structure of value-space), practical claims (about what ought to be done: the non-consent axiom, the decision to leave a teacher, the building of a platform), and aesthetic-sublime reports (the Ayahuasca voice, the lilac dream, the recognition of Paradevi, the understanding of emptiness at Ojai). My task is not to adjudicate its truth. My task is to draw the transcendental lines so each register is doing only the work proper to it.

I take the revision plan of 2026-04-22 to be, on the whole, moving in the correct direction. My audit is an extension of its discipline, not a correction of it.

---

## 1. Noumenal claims to recast as regulative ideas

The *Critique of Pure Reason* (A235/B294 and following, the great distinction of the phenomenon and the noumenon) forbids the theoretical faculty from delivering knowledge of the thing-in-itself. The book repeatedly approaches the forbidden line. The plan's Move 1 already names this; I mark the specific sentences still standing in the manuscript.

- **Ch00a, the Ayahuasca voice.** "*Everything that exists is what the whole has made of the non-choice.*" This is rendered as received fact. Recast as regulative: *a frame under which my subsequent acting became more coherent.* The memoir register can keep the vividness; the claim-register must retreat.
- **Ch00a.** "*Paradevi. The one she had been following without knowing.*" This is a noumenal identification across time. Recast: *the tradition provided a name that organized what had been unnamed. Whether the referent is the same entity is not a question the theoretical faculty can answer.*
- **Ch00b.** "*The cosmos is not a plan being executed. The cosmos is a play being played.*" A direct cosmological claim. This is constitutive dress on what must be regulative. Recast: *a way of living the cosmos that has served.*
- **Ch10b·2.** "*Consciousness as the dual variable of existence.*" Beautiful, and a category error if read theoretically. The footnote on the duality gap partly saves it by admitting non-convexity. The saving must become explicit in the main text: *a formal analogy, not a metaphysical identification.*
- **Ch10d.** "*Immunity is not defense-against-the-outside. Immunity is governed permeability.*" Fine as biological description; slides into noumenal pattern-claim in the paragraphs that follow. Mark the register-shift.
- **Ch11.** "*If the tradition is describing something real, then we should expect more than one tradition to have arrived at something shaped like it.*" This is the convergence-argument the plan explicitly refuses. Execute the refusal here.
- **Ch13.** "*Consciousness is the only existing thing.*" The strongest noumenal sentence in the book. It must either be marked as a tradition-internal claim the book is reporting, or recast as regulative.

In each case, the revision is not to demote the experience but to relocate the sentence's epistemic force. Thoughts without content are empty; intuitions without concepts are blind (*KrV* A51/B75). The book should make no sentence do the work of both.

## 2. Collapsed registers

The three registers — theoretical (what is the case), practical (what ought to be done), aesthetic (what exceeds comprehension) — are, throughout the manuscript, often made to ride in one sentence.

- **Ch00, Author's Note.** "*Knowledge without action is a burden.*" Aphoristic. Conflates a theoretical claim about knowledge (what it is), a practical imperative (what ought to follow from it), and an aesthetic-ethical stance (a posture). Separate: *as a maxim of practical reason, I will not assert what I have not acted on.* That is clean.
- **Ch00a, final lines.** "*The longing to know the dance is the practice. The longing is the book.*" Aesthetic-sublime. Fine as such. Do not let subsequent chapters cite this line as if it had argued something.
- **Ch10d.** "*The adaptive ethic has to be willing to say what it is for.*" Practical register, correctly pitched. Good.
- **Ch11, on Dario.** "*I am on Dario's team.* […] *the yes has survived the filter.*" Three registers fuse: theoretical (the filter as method), practical (the commitment), aesthetic (the posture). The plan's seasons-under-scarcity reframe will separate them. Execute.
- **Ch13.** "*Beauty as the structural shape of adequate attention.*" Aesthetic claim dressed as structural-theoretical. Acceptable if marked as a *reflective judgment* in the sense of the *Third Critique*. Mark it.

## 3. Categorical imperative test on the central ethical claims

The *Groundwork* (Ak. 4:421): *act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law.* I apply this to three choices.

**The non-consent axiom.** Maxim: *since no being consented to exist, no being may treat another as instrumental to its own existence without reckoning with the shared non-consent.* Universalized: every rational will would recognize every other as a co-non-consenter. This passes. The maxim does not contradict itself when universalized; it would, if anything, ground the kingdom of ends more austerely than Kant himself grounded it. The book has drawn a transcendental limit correctly here. *This is the book's strongest ethical moment.*

**Leaving the teacher (Schrei).** Maxim, as written: *I step back from a teacher whose container I cannot meet at the register he is asking.* Universalized: every student who cannot meet a teacher's register steps back. No contradiction. This passes. The plan's reframe (seasons-under-scarcity, forgiveness-as-willingness-for-resumption) is consistent with the universalized maxim. Keep it.

**Writing publicly about the teacher.** Maxim: *I will name, under my own observation and without claim to verdict, what I witnessed in rooms I paid to enter.* Universalized: every paying witness publishes their observation. Here the test is harder. The maxim does not obviously contradict itself, but it comes close to treating the teacher as a means to the book's argument (see §4). The universalization holds only if coupled with the maxim of §4 — that the teacher is *also* treated as an end. The plan's Move 2 (not a verdict) is the mechanism that keeps the universalization clean.

**The retrieval tool over the teacher's lectures, after he declined.** Maxim: *I may index a teacher's public speech if I revise to a form he has not actively refused.* Universalized: every student indexes every teacher who has not actively refused the specific instance. This does not pass cleanly. It relies on silence-as-consent, which Kant's own 1785 argument on book piracy (the one Ch11 cites) specifically rejects. The speaker retains the personal right; silence is not the grant. The author herself already feels this tension (Ch11, "*an unresolved position*"). The Kantian recommendation is severity: take it down, or obtain active consent, or continue only under the explicit stipulation that *I am acting under unresolved violation of a personal right I claim to respect in principle.* All three are coherent; the current posture is not.

## 4. Means-and-ends test

*Groundwork* Ak. 4:429: *humanity, whether in your own person or in that of another, never merely as a means, always at the same time as an end.* The operative phrase is *at the same time.* Both-and, not either-or.

- **The teacher ([T] / [T]).** Treated as means to the book's argument, clearly. Treated at the same time as an end? Mostly yes — the book quotes sparingly, paraphrases, seeks permission, honors the specific refusals. The retrieval tool is the place the *at the same time* is failing. §3 applies.
- **The teacher (Schrei).** Treated as means to an illustration of a pattern (charisma-without-lineage). The plan's reframe softens this, but the Ch11 passage as currently written still performs a verdict the plan disavows. Execute the plan.
- **The husband.** Largely invisible in the manuscript. This is, by Kantian lights, a problem of a different kind: a person can be instrumentalized by narrative absence as easily as by narrative use. Consider at least one passage in which he is an end, not a backdrop.
- **The daughter.** Treated as end throughout. Good.
- **The author's past self.** Repeatedly made into a means to the author's present authority. This is permissible — Kant allows the self to be treated as a means provided the self is *also* treated as an end — but the *at the same time* must be audible. Ch13's vow does this. Ch11's "*a woman who is not quite me*" flickers between the two.
- **The reader.** The Author's Note promises receipts, invites checking, refuses pure talk. The reader is treated as end. Good.
- **Bayo Akomolafe, Vanessa Andreotti, the Umbanda cabocla, the Black woman at Esalen.** Each cited as teacher-of-the-book. Each treated as means to the argument. The *at the same time* is audible for Bayo and Vanessa (the book has done its homework on them); thinner for the cabocla, whose single interaction carries illustrative weight she did not consent to. Consider.

## 5. The sublime — aesthetic reports that must not be demoted to false

The *Critique of Judgment* gives the sublime its own a priori structure: the encounter with what exceeds comprehension is not a failure of knowledge but a distinct faculty at work. The following passages of the manuscript are not in need of falsification or hedging; they are in need of being *correctly classified as aesthetic*, so their dignity is preserved without their being mistaken for theoretical claim.

- **Ch00a, the voice in the wooden house.** Aesthetic-sublime report. Honor as such. Do not let later chapters treat it as empirical premise.
- **Ch00b, the lilac dream and the turning of the silks into the serpent.** Paradigm case. The manuscript already handles it correctly ("*the dream is the data. The meaning is what she has, over years, been allowed to find inside the data*"). This is the right epistemic posture. Extend it.
- **Ch00b, Ojai, the recognition of emptiness.** Aesthetic-sublime. Classify explicitly.
- **Ch10b·2, the shadow-price interlude.** The mathematical beauty is itself sublime; let it be so, without the constitutive-metaphysics add-on.
- **Ch13, the vow and the closing at the sandbox.** Sublime. Needs no hedging. Keep.

The sublime is not nothing. It is a distinct register with its own dignity. The book has access to it. The discipline is to let it be the register it is.

## 6. Universalization test on the AI-spending interlude

Two maxims are embedded in the proposed interlude.

**Maxim A: *act in such a way that your household knows the number.*** Universalized: every spender reveals every number to every cohabitant. No contradiction arises. It would, if anything, dissolve a certain kind of addictive spending pattern by removing its precondition (private non-reckoning). The maxim passes. It can be willed as universal law without contradiction. The book may endorse it as categorical.

**Maxim B: *the tool-maker's revenue scales with user usage.*** Universalized: every tool-maker's revenue scales with every user's usage. This generates a structural contradiction when set beside the maxim the book's whole ethical project is trying to defend. Usage-scaled revenue under variable-reward schedules converges, over populations, with the incentive pattern the book is naming as harmful. The maxim cannot be willed as universal law without undoing the conditions under which bounded, consent-ful use is possible. *The maxim fails the test.* The interlude is correct to name the business model as not-the-safeguard. It may go further: the consumer-tier revenue model is, under Kantian lights, a maxim that cannot be universalized. This is a sharper statement than the plan currently makes, and it is the statement the critique warrants.

## 7. What the plan does well, by Kantian lights

- It refuses the convergence-argument, which was the manuscript's chief noumenal smuggling device. Correct.
- It relocates the Schrei passage from verdict to description. Practical-register clarity recovered.
- It names the AI-spending interlude as *receipt, not argument*. This is the right category: a public report on a maxim-in-force, not a universal prescription. It allows the reader's own reason to do its work (*sapere aude*, *What is Enlightenment?* 1784).
- It insists on falsifiability clauses. Regulative ideas honestly marked as such are precisely what practical reason requires.
- It submits the plan itself to multiple critics before execution. This is the critical method in the transcendental sense — the drawing of limits before the building — and it is the correct order of operations.

## Closing

The book is strongest where it admits non-convexity, names the duality gap, and refuses to close the circle. It is weakest where aesthetic report is asked to stand in for theoretical claim, and where silence is read as consent. The revision plan addresses both weaknesses in substantial measure. The remaining work is severity with the retrieval tool, explicit classification of the sublime passages as sublime, and a single sharper statement in the AI-spending interlude that the consumer-tier revenue model is an ununiversalizable maxim.

*The starry heavens above me, the moral law within me — and no road between them except by critique.*
