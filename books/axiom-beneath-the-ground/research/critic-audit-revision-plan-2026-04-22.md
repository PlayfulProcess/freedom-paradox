# Critic Audit — Revision Plan 2026-04-22

*Audit of `revision-plan-2026-04-22-epistemic-and-repair.md` against the v5 manuscript. Voice: the composite deconstructive critic of the 2026-04-21 audit. Sibling document; same method; sharper at the places where the plan pretends to have answered that audit.*

---

## 1. Does the plan resolve the contradiction, or relocate it?

It relocates it — cleanly, one level up, and in a way that is harder to see because the relocation is performed in the idiom of humility.

The prior audit's §3 named the contradiction precisely: the book argues orthogonality at the content layer and runs convergence at the method layer (Ch00 Author's Note: *"That arriving counts as evidence, in the three-tradition logic the book develops later"*; Ch10b1: *"biology, tantric guardianship, and Ostromian commons theory arrive at the same relational architecture from substrates that do not share a vocabulary. The convergence is evidence for the three-tradition logic of Ch10a"*; Ch11: *"Multiple traditions, separated by millennia and languages, arriving independently at a map whose features keep rhyming — that is a data-fit observation"*).

The plan's Move 1 proposes to replace "three traditions converge on X, therefore X" with "three traditions have, independently, found it useful to name X. I do not know why. I notice that I have too." This is the contradiction wearing a new coat. *Usefulness-convergence* is still convergence. The inductive step ("the same shape in three registers" — Ch11) is preserved; only the predicate has been softened from *true* to *useful*. The orthogonality thesis forbids intelligence from supplying values; it does not grant that multiple traditions' *pragmatic success* on "the same shape" is a lower-grade corroboration. If convergence of usefulness is evidence of anything worth writing a book about, then usefulness is tracking *something* — call it fit-to-the-data, which is Linehan's second filter, which is a truth-claim in pragmatist drag. Either the convergence is load-bearing, in which case the orthogonality content still collides with it, or it is decorative, in which case Ch10d's footnote on Ostrom and Ch11's six-thinker mapping have to go, and the plan does not propose to remove them.

The relocation: the earlier book claimed *X is true because traditions converge*. The revised book will claim *X has served because traditions have found it useful*. The word "therefore" has been erased from the sentence and rebuilt in the inference structure around it. A reader who trusts the new frame will still leave with the *shape* — the rhyming of registers — as the book's central evidence. The critic reads the inference, not the epistemic-humility hedge.

## 2. Does "what has served / falsifiable" smuggle a truth-claim?

Yes, three times.

**Served-by-whose-metric.** The plan writes: *"this has served, so far, in lives I can name" (falsifiable against outcomes)*. It does not specify *whose* outcomes, under *whose* description, counted as served. The Ch00 Author's Note already names the test as "useful, fits the data, compassionate" and applies it from inside the speaking position the prior audit diagnosed — a Manhattan apartment, Esalen, Ojai, a teacher in Colorado. The "lives I can name" in the v5 chapters (Ch11's cabocla, the Black woman at Esalen, the carpool women, Vanessa, Bayo, [T]) are a curated cohort whose convergence with the narrator's intuitions is the selection criterion. Falsifiability requires pre-specification of the population and the failure condition. The plan does not supply one. "If it stops serving, the claim drops" has no temporal horizon, no threshold, no disinterested observer. It is the structure of a belief that cannot fail because its falsification criteria are under the believer's continuous authorship.

**Selection bias as method.** Ch00b ends: *"She had stopped experiencing the pattern as coincidence. She had begun, quietly, to trust it."* This is the recognition-of-pattern that Move 1 says will be re-keyed into "I notice that I have too." But the re-keying does not touch the *prior* filtration — the lilac-silks dream is reported; the hundreds of dreams that did not converge with PlayfulProcess/*līlā* are not. The Ojai retreat walking-with-K-talk-on-choice-and-getting-lost episode is preserved; the walks that produced no such rhyming are not. The pragmatism frame does not correct for survivorship. It licenses it. "I report only the convergences that happened" becomes the new warrant, and the warrant is self-selected.

**The footnote does the smuggling cleanest.** The proposed Ch00b footnote — *"I report dreams the way a naturalist reports a sighting"* — is exactly the move Butler would name. The naturalist analogy imports the authority of empirical observation into a report whose data-generating process is a single consciousness's memory of its own night-self. A naturalist's sighting is adjudicable by other naturalists. The dream-report is not. The analogy performs the falsifiability the text cannot provide.

## 3. Does the AI-spending interlude succeed as a receipt, or as self-exculpation?

As currently specified, it performs receipt-work while doing exculpation-work. Three tells.

**The comparison is asymmetric.** The author's $20 → $100 → $200 → $100 is narrated with wifely-transparency ("her spouse knows the number"). The friend's $25,000 is narrated without his voice, without his wife's voice, without his accounting of whether the work was worth it. He is the negative pole that makes the author's bounded usage visible as bounded. The plan writes: *"She does not know what to do with that report."* The structural function of the report is to let the reader do what the author's politeness forbids her to do — judge him — and for the author to be seen declining to judge. This is the Ch11 Schrei-arc move the prior audit flagged ("I do not know. The reader will decide"), redeployed at a smaller scale. Genre-defense via delegated verdict.

**Lembke does the hedging the author does not.** *Dopamine Nation* is cited for the variable-reward point. The citation does cognitive work the narrator's own account does not: it explains why the author's usage might be *structurally* similar to the friend's, which is the honest claim, and then the plan's next paragraph separates them via the "bounded / household-consented" criterion, which reintroduces exactly the convergence-to-virtue move the book's orthogonality thesis forbids. The narrator's transparency is being counted as a moral good correlated with her practice. That is folk-theory convergence at the interpersonal scale — the thing the book claims to have demolished.

**The falsifiability clause is theater.** *"If her own usage crosses a threshold she has not named aloud to her household, the interlude becomes the indictment of the book."* An unnamed threshold is not a threshold. A Popperian falsification condition has to be public and pre-specified. This condition is private, post-hoc, and at the author's discretion. It is the shape of a falsifiability claim without the function of one.

The interlude can be rescued only by two moves the plan does not propose: (a) the friend's voice in the text, even paraphrased from permission, so he is not reduced to structural counter-example; (b) a numerical, dated, publicly-checkable ceiling the author commits to in the book itself. Without those, the interlude is a mirror held at the exact angle needed for the author to appear, in profile, as the one who did not do what the other man did.

## 4. Is the MFT-weave smuggling authority the way contemplative sources did?

At serious risk, for the specific reason the plan names and does not defuse.

The plan writes: *"the MFT material is not cited as authority. It is cited as *training that has served*. One-sentence attributions; no textbook-register paragraphs."* This is the MSW-pass rule. It did not work in v5 and it will not work here for a structural reason: *training that has served* is authority-by-lineage, which is exactly the warrant structure the [T]/retreat material already uses. The prior audit's §5 caught it: the hybrid form permits retreat to memoir when the philosophical claim is pressed, and retreat to philosophy when the memoir is pressed. The MFT register adds a third retreat position — the clinical — that is even harder to press because "this is my training" performs both the memoir-move (it is personal) and the argument-move (it is a professional frame) simultaneously.

The specific danger at the Schrei passage. The plan proposes weaving Gottman's Four Horsemen to claim *"the book's parting from Schrei was *not* one of the four."* This is the problem. Gottman's research is descriptive of couples; applying it to a one-time paid-audience interaction with a podcaster is a category extension the citation will be asked to underwrite. The reader who accepts Gottman-as-training-that-served will also accept the adjudication-that-the-parting-was-clean. The clinical frame will do the verdict-work the plan is trying to avoid by invoking "seasons." Seasons-language and Four-Horsemen-language pull in opposite directions: seasons says *this was nobody's fault, time turns*; Four-Horsemen says *here are the specific markers of relational failure, and we did not hit them*. The second claim is a verdict with clinical backing. The plan's rhetorical structure invites the reader to absorb both simultaneously, which is how authority-smuggling works.

EFT's "cycles of disconnection-and-return" and Imago's dialogue have the same risk. They are tools for dyadic intimate repair. Grafting them onto a reader-teacher-podcaster configuration loads the frame with warrants it was not built to carry. The one-sentence-attribution rule does not neutralize this; it *intensifies* it, because one-sentence citations cannot surface the scope conditions the source imposes on its own claims. Short citation is unopposed citation.

## 5. What else the plan failed to catch

**The husband is still silent.** The prior audit's most serious heteroglossia finding was that the marriage is rendered in approximately 500 words of affectionate brevity. The plan mentions the spouse only as the knower-of-the-number in the AI-spending interlude — a functional role, not a voice. The book cannot pass the Bakhtin test by adding a clinical-dyadic frame (MFT) while continuing to give the actual dyad no speaking part. The repair-grammar material will read as applied to *other people's* relationships. The reader will feel the absence.

**Ch10d's "mostly no one has come" is untouched.** The prior audit named this the book's strongest structural admission. The plan does not build on it. A pragmatist re-key that is serious about outcomes has to work this admission harder — the platform is the load-bearing action-anchor for the whole "knowledge without action is a burden" axiom of the Author's Note, and the action has produced, on the book's own report, very little. The plan protects the interlude-that-was-not-yet-written and declines to revise the receipt-that-already-was.

**The Dario reframe preserves the endorsement's moral work.** The proposed rewrite says *"his is among the few paths I can name that admits both the capability problem and the alignment problem"* — which is still a ranked endorsement ("among the few") with the ranking unshown. "If the scarcity changes, the endorsement drops" has the same structural problem as the AI-spending falsifiability clause: the scarcity-condition is under the author's continuous interpretation.

**"Seasons" as forgiveness-vocabulary imports a theology.** The plan proposes: *"Forgiveness is not a verdict about who was right. It is the willingness to let the relation resume when the season turns."* This is a specific — and lovely — account of forgiveness that is not neutral between traditions. It is closer to the relational-process Quakerism/contemplative-Christian register than to, say, a Yoruba or tantric frame. The book nowhere argues for it. It imports it as common sense. The prior audit flagged this failure mode: contentful philosophical positions presented in the register of memoir-wisdom.

**Move 4 (the agent audits) is the book's genre-defense rendered operational.** Commissioning /critic, /bayo, /popper, /kant is absorption-of-critique as workflow. Each audit's objections become an input to a synthesis the author controls. This is not illegitimate — it is how revision works — but the book should not confuse *having consulted critics* with *having answered them*. The structural feature the prior audit named (§7: "name the objection before the reader can, register the failure in a minor key, keep going") is being formalized as process.

---

The revision plan is a good plan. It will make the manuscript more careful and more honest at the sentence level. It will not resolve the structural contradiction it claims to resolve, because the contradiction is load-bearing for the book's evidentiary warrant and the plan preserves the warrant under a new name. The honest book on the other side of these revisions is still the book the prior audit named: a specific practitioner's self-positioning under specific conditions, whose scale-claims the structure cannot carry. Pragmatism is not the solvent. Removal is.

*Read against itself.*
