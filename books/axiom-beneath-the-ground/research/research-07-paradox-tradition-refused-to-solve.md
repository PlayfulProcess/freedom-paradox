## Summary

The canonical texts of nondual Śaiva Tantra do not merely contain the tension between "you are already whole" and "practice these methods" — they structurally depend on it. Every major text stages the contradiction. Not one resolves it.

Five conclusions for the book:

1. **The tension is in the texts themselves.** Not a modern imposition. VBT: 23 + 112 + 23 architecture. Pratyabhijñā-hṛdayam: Sūtra 1 vs Sūtras 15-20. Śiva Sūtras: 1.1 vs 55 practice sūtras. Tantrāloka: 37 chapters elaborating what Chapter 1 declares unnecessary.

2. **The commentators managed it without resolving it.** Kṣemarāja: "partial burning" (the fire never goes out). Abhinavagupta: jeweler analogy (expertise erases the need for procedures that produced it). Both used śaktipāta to defer: grace is consciousness choosing to reveal itself to itself.

3. **The circularity was recognized and incorporated.** Lawrence: Utpaladeva and Abhinavagupta knew their central inference made Śakti both reason and conclusion. Abhinavagupta's reading of ŚS 1.2 preserves ambiguity: knowledge IS bondage too.

4. **Parallel traditions confirm this is structural, not accidental.** Dōgen, Longchenpa, Śaṅkara, Eckhart — all stage the same paradox. "An understanding of reality as inherently paradoxical."

5. **The scholarly debate is itself structured by the same undecidability.** Constructivism = axiom. Perennialism = truth. NST's svātantrya suggests the distinction itself dissolves at the level of ultimate consciousness.

## Key Quote for the Book

"The VBT's Devī asks 'who worships? who is worshipped?' and receives not an answer but more practice. The question, having been asked, does not prevent the practice from continuing. It does not need to be answered. It needs to be *inhabited*."

## The Wallis Triangle (confirmed)

Three positions in Wallis's published work:
- Pragmatist: "evaluate thoughts solely by efficacy"
- Realist: "your worth is proven by your very existence"
- Axiomatic: the View must be held BEFORE recognition

These form a triangle that does not fully cohere. The contradiction is probably compassionate — adapted to the student's level, which is why oral traditions and 1-1 containers exist. The book that tries to hold all three positions simultaneously is the book that is being written.
