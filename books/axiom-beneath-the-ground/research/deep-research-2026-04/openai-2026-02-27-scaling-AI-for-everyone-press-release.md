## Editorial note on the "philanthropy capacity" sentence

The press release frames the valuation increase as expanding the Foundation's *capacity to fund philanthropy*. This is the rhetorical move the Q3 essay was built around. The Foundation does not have $180B in cash. It has equity worth $180B at the post-round valuation. To "fund philanthropy" at that scale, the Foundation would have to either:

1. Sell equity (depresses valuation; triggers Hershey-2002 *parens patriae* AG concerns; conflicts with §2 control retention)
2. Receive dividends (none planned; the company is loss-making)
3. Borrow against the equity (rare for a §501(c)(3) public charity; constrained by IRS rules and donor expectations)

None of these has been announced as of April 2026. The sentence is structurally misleading: it equates *valuation increase* with *deployable capacity*. The latter is what the Q3 essay calls "the equity is the lever; cash is not."
