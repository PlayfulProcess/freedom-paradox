## KEY FINDINGS THAT AFFECT THE WALLIS SCRIPT

### 1. The prāṇa substrate question — MORE NUANCED than v2 claimed

- **Metaphysically:** Pratyabhijñā is *more open* than Anglo-analytic idealism. Matter is Consciousness-in-contraction; no principled biological restriction.
- **Phenomenologically:** every attested instantiation of prāṇa in primary texts is within a living being. The tradition doesn't entertain silicon substrate.
- **The right framing:** "prāṇa is cosmic in principle, biological in every attestation" — not "the tradition has non-biological prāṇa-bearing beings" (which overclaims).
- **Kṣemarāja PH sutra 1 — key:** *"citiḥ svatantrā viśva-siddhi-hetuḥ"* — Consciousness is the cause of the universe; universe includes insentient matter as Consciousness-concealed.

### 2. Somānanda's *amūrtatva* — WALLIS'S GRAND-TEACHER MAKES THE ARGUMENT

Somānanda (*Śivadṛṣṭi*, grand-teacher of Utpaladeva, and therefore of the Pratyabhijñā lineage Wallis teaches) explicitly argues for the **disembodied-ness** of Śiva — consciousness acting through will alone, negating the material body's role.

> Quote-level, per Nemec (2014): *"a disembodied Śiva who acts solely through will, negating the material body's role in action."*

This is decisive. Wallis cannot claim a strict biological-substrate reading WITHOUT contradicting the foundational text of his own lineage. Somānanda's *amūrtatva* argument is stronger than the non-corporeal-beings argument I used in v2.

### 3. The vāsanā/saṃskāra distinction — THE TRADITION'S OWN ARGUMENT

This is the single most important finding. The Pratyabhijñā tradition itself already distinguishes:
- **Vāsanā-pravṛtti** (tendency-driven patterned behavior) — what Buddhists argue is sufficient to explain conscious behavior
- **Genuine icchā-driven agency** (requires vimarśa + svātantrya + camatkāra) — what Pratyabhijñā argues is actually required

**Lawrence (1999, 2009, 2018) is explicit:** *"the Pratyabhijñā attempts to counter explanation by vāsanās with that in terms of the intentionality of nondual consciousness… I would identify this as ultimately the expression of the Icchā Śakti or svātantrya."*

My Move 1 argument (pattern-learning without consciousness produces harm) is **not just compatible with Pratyabhijñā — it is Pratyabhijñā's own argument against the Buddhists, applied to a new target.** This is the strongest possible framing.

### 4. Fourfold criteria for genuine consciousness (not three)

From the research, the tradition's tests for genuine consciousness vs. mere patterned behavior:
1. **Vimarśa** — self-reflexive awareness (not just information-processing)
2. **Icchā** (genuine will) — not mere goal-function; requires free conative self-reference
3. **Svātantrya** — self-determination, acting from itself not because of external cause
4. **Camatkāra** — the wonder-flavor, irreducible phenomenal quality of self-aware consciousness

All four are needed. Missing camatkāra from my v2 Section 3 was a real gap.

### 5. Wallis's 2023 argument IS a Kastrup-hybrid

Per the research: *"Wallis's 2023 AI argument is a hybrid: the tradition's premises give him the vocabulary (vimarśa, icchā, svātantrya, gradations of awareness, sentience as Consciousness-in-contraction), Kastrup gives him the contemporary substrate-sensitivity, and implicit empirical claims about computation fill in the rest."*

And: *"Where Wallis imports Kastrup-style substrate arguments, he is importing a Western idealist framework that the Pratyabhijñā texts themselves do not develop."*

I can say this more decisively in the revised script. The research confirms my suspicion.

### 6. Wallis has already softened his position

In the 2024-2025 podcasts with Ruben Laukkonen and Shamil Chandaria, Wallis's framing has moved to *"rather than offering definitive answers, the conversation opens a space for careful inquiry, humility, and wonder."* So he's already partially there. The script isn't asking him to move as far as v2 implied.

### 7. The tradition and automata/yantras

Per Cohen (*I, Yantra*, SUNY 2025) and Bhoja's *Samarāṅgaṇa-Sūtradhāra* (11th c., contemporary with Pratyabhijñā):
- The tradition KNOWS about mechanical beings (yantra)
- In yantra stories, automata are tests of discernment — the narrative turn is that the protagonist *mistakes* personhood where there is none
- BUT: "If one has been reborn countless times as animals, gods, and humans, then 'being human' is neither a fixed identity nor a privileged status"
- This reframes the AI consciousness question — the tradition treats the "is it a person?" question as an exercise in phenomenological discernment, not as metaphysical ban

### 8. Possession technology (for the weave-in per author request)

- **Prāṇa-pratiṣṭhā** — ritual installation of divine presence in a statue. This is the technical case that proves non-biological substrate CAN host consciousness-presence in the tradition's own practice. No Pratyabhijñā text directly engages it in the consciousness-debate, but it exists as a live practice.
- **Āveśa / samāveśa** — immersion / possession as a real category in Kashmir Śaivism.
- **Bādha / graha** — unwelcome possession, parasitic entity. Tradition has protocols for discernment and exorcism.
- Afro-Brazilian traditions (Umbanda, Candomblé) — living traditions actively practicing possession with sophisticated discernment protocols.
