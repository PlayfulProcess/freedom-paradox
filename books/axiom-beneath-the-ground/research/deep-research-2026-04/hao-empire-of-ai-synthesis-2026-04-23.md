## KEY CITATION-DISCIPLINE NOTES FOR THE BOOK

1. **ALWAYS note the December 2025 water correction.** Hao publicly corrected a factor-of-1,000 unit error on p. 288. Citing the original figure invites the exact Anthropic-aligned rebuttal that she is overheated.

2. **Empire of AI is the PRIMARY source for** the bad-actor framing, the empire thesis, the Kenyan labor documentation (Ch. 9), the 11/2023 board-crisis governance failure (Chs. 15–17), and the inevitability-was-a-choice claim.

3. **Empire of AI is NOT the primary source for** addictive-design or household-scale financial harm. For THAT, pair Hao's Democracy Now / TechCrunch interviews (supporting) with: Pataranutaporn & Mahari 2024 "allure of AI companions" (MIT Tech Review); Meetali Jain's litigation; Raine v. OpenAI filings; Common Sense Media 2024 studies.

4. **OpenAI declined to cooperate** with the book and Altman publicly disparaged it (April 2025 tweet, unnamed). Anthropic / Amodei has NOT directly responded to the book as of mid-2026. Amodei's January 2026 "Adolescence of Technology" essay makes adjacent arguments — concentration of power, AI-company governance risk — that overlap with Hao's critique in places; a careful Amodei-aligned defender might actually concede some of it rather than contest.

5. **Verified safe fair-use quotes** (all ≤30 words; all from the Prologue, verifiable via Porchlight's public excerpt):
   - *"Nothing about this form of AI coming to the fore or even existing at all was inevitable; it was the culmination of thousands of subjective choices."*
   - *"The new empires of AI are amassing extraordinary riches across space and time at great expense to everyone else."*
   - *"Workers in Kenya earned starvation wages to filter out violence and hate speech from OpenAI's technologies, including ChatGPT."*
   - *"At this point, AGI is largely rhetorical — a fantastical, all-purpose excuse for OpenAI to continue pushing for ever more wealth and power."*

6. **Her framework question** for readers (Tech Policy Press): *"How are you developing and deploying AI to shift power? Are you ultimately fortifying the empire or dismantling the empire?"*
