## 1. William Doherty on Neutrality as a Moral Position (STRICT)

The cleanest, most directly quotable Doherty articulation of the "neutrality is itself a moral position" argument is in his article **"How Therapists Harm Marriages and What We Can Do About It"** (*Journal of Couple and Relationship Therapy*, 2002, vol. 1, pp. 1–17), adapted from his 1999 SmartMarriages keynote. Doherty's clinical practice began in 1977, and he says he first articulated the critique in his 1995 Basic Books volume **Soul Searching: Why Psychotherapy Must Promote Moral Responsibility**.

The decisive passage — usable verbatim as the essay's opener — reads:

> "Therapists took two stances towards marriage during this era. The first stance was 'neutrality' on the subject of marital commitment. In a short time, therapists moved from an era in which a prominent psychiatrist in the 1950s said that he never supported a couple's decision to get a divorce, to an era where the therapist was supposed to be neutral. A recent survey of clinical members of the American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy found that nearly two-thirds said that they are 'neutral' on the subject of marriage and divorce. As a prominent family therapist told the press a few years ago, 'The good marriage, the good divorce, it matters not.'"

The pivot — Doherty's claim that neutrality is not neutral — is articulated later in the same paper:

> "[Therapeutic neutrality] is an undermining stance, not a neutral stance. And it often sides with the more self-oriented spouse. When people are seriously considering getting out of a marriage, listen to their language. They are often using the language of individual self-interest, not the language of moral commitment. 'I have needs.' 'I have a right to happiness.' Without the language of moral commitment being part of the equation, this is the language of self-interest, of the psychological market place. If the therapist's language is the same, now you have an alliance between the reluctant, distancing spouse and the therapist, a collusion [that] undermines the marital relationship in ways that the therapist probably does [not realize]…. I am an advocate for their marriage. They can call me off but they're going to have to."

A later, more compressed version of the same argument — useful as a one-line epigraph — appears in his **Psychotherapy Networker** article "Should This Marriage Be Saved? Therapists and the Dilemma of Divorce" (July/August 2015, blog excerpt titled "Why We Shouldn't Be Neutral About Divorce"). In that piece Doherty reflects on his own early neutrality and writes that a senior therapist had told him: *"The main thing is what you think will make you happier in the next phase of your life. If you think you'll be happier staying married, I'll help you do that. If you think you'll be happier getting divorced, I'll help you do that."* Doherty says he now "cringes" at that posture because it does not "reflect the anguish of the clients."

**Soul Searching (Basic Books, 1995)** itself frames this through Doherty's preferred role-name: the therapist as **"moral consultant."** The dust-jacket and *Library Journal* précis (Bonnie Hoffman review, 1995) summarize his thesis as a call for therapists "to abandon psychotherapy's traditional neutrality and accept moral responsibility" and to act as "moral consultants" who "raise questions about the effects of clients' behavior on others." The book is positioned against what Doherty later calls **"the consumer culture of marriage,"** an idea developed at length in **Take Back Your Marriage: Sticking Together in a World That Pulls Us Apart** (Guilford, 2nd ed. 2013), where he argues that the dominant cultural narrative of marriage has shifted from covenant to consumption, and that therapy that affects neutrality between those frames has already chosen consumption.

For the essay's opener, the most efficient construction is: (a) the AAMFT-survey fact (two-thirds of clinical members declaring themselves "neutral"); (b) Doherty's "undermining stance, not a neutral stance" line; (c) the move from there to the Brazilian/Indian/Nigerian leader who treats AI as if there were a position outside the trilemma.

## 2. Khanmigo — The Quiet Disappointment (STRICT)

### Original promise (March 2023 – 2024)

Khanmigo was launched at Sal Khan's TED talk **"How AI Could Save (Not Destroy) Education"** (April 2023, recorded March 2023), and the launch was explicitly framed as the answer to Bloom's two-sigma problem — i.e., that one-on-one tutoring could yield ~2σ learning gains, and that AI could deliver that at scale. The original product pitched four claims: (i) a personal 24/7 tutor for math, science, humanities, and coding; (ii) a teaching assistant that would relieve teacher load; (iii) Socratic guardrails (no answer-giving); (iv) availability initially at $4/month for individuals after a pilot phase.

### Adoption — the headline number Khan Academy emphasizes

Khan Academy chief learning officer **Kristen DiCerbo**, in an *Education Week* opinion interview with Rick Hess ("Can an AI-Powered Tutor Produce Meaningful Results?", July 2025), reported usage growth from "about 68,000 Khanmigo student and teacher users in our partner school districts in 2023-24 to more than 700,000 in the 2024-25 school year, expanding from 45 to more than 380 district partners. In total, Khan Academy partners with around 450 districts in the U.S." In an *Unsupervised Learning* podcast appearance (April 2025) Sal Khan reported the cross-segment user count as **1.4 million** (including teachers and students), saying he had originally projected ~100,000 users by 2025. **K-12 Dive** (DiCerbo, "3 questions for K-12 leaders to consider amid the AI tutoring boom," 2025) confirmed the 40,000→700,000 figure and reported a projected jump above 1 million in 2025-26, with DiCerbo calling it "the biggest one-year jump that I have seen in terms of adoption of an education technology."

### What outcomes data actually exists

This is the critical part for the essay. As of April 2026, **Khan Academy itself has published no causal evidence that Khanmigo improves learning outcomes.** Khan Academy's own November 2024 Efficacy Results blog post acknowledges: *"Studies are underway to understand how Khanmigo, Khan Academy's artificial intelligence tutor for students and assistant for teachers, impacts learning outcomes."* The Toronto/Stanford partnership it cites is for the *core platform*, not Khanmigo specifically. In the Hess interview, DiCerbo says the "biggest challenge" with Khanmigo is "the same challenge we have seen historically with educational technology" — a striking hedge given the original pitch.

The strongest **independent peer-reviewed evaluation of Khanmigo** as of Q1 2026 is **Slijepcevic & Yaylali, "Leveraging 'Khanmigo' Generative AI-Powered Tool for Personalized Tutoring to Learn Scientific Concepts,"** *Journal of Teaching and Learning* vol. 19 no. 4 (2025), pp. 155–178. The study used a pre/post Lunar Phases Concept Inventory across 69 undergraduates split into Khanmigo, Google Search, and (emergent) paper-only groups. The published result: *"Quantitative analysis revealed significant learning gains across all conditions but found no statistically significant differences between groups in terms of learning outcomes."* Students "viewed it as a supplementary tool rather than a replacement for traditional instruction." The authors connect this to Borchers et al. (2023), which found the same null result for earlier intelligent tutoring systems versus paper. **This is the single most usable peer-reviewed citation against the AI-tutor-as-revolution claim.**

A widely-circulated claim of a **WestEd RCT across 47 schools showing a 0.15 SD improvement in algebra readiness** appears on the EdRus blog and several derivative posts but **does not appear in WestEd's official publication list as of April 2026** — flagging it as **less-strict, secondary**; the essay should not lean on it.

### The Khan World School / Levitt 4.6× claim

Sal Khan's most aggressive learning-outcome claim came not from Khanmigo per se but from **Khan World School @ ASU Prep**, a small (initially ~47–50 student) online microschool partnered with ASU Preparatory Academy and **Steven Levitt's Center for RISC / Levitt Lab** at the University of Chicago. After year one (2022–23), Khan posted on LinkedIn (covered in Levitt's *People I (Mostly) Admire* podcast and reproduced on the ASU Prep website): *"Based on the ExactPath assessment, our students saw: 4.6x typical growth in math (or 460% acceleration), 3.4x typical growth in reading, 5.3x typical growth in language arts."*

There are three critical methodological caveats the essay should mark:
1. **n ≈ 47–50**, no control group, no random assignment, no peer review.
2. **ExactPath is Illuminate Education's commercial adaptive-assessment product**, not a standardized achievement test like NAEP, MAP Growth, or state assessments — and "typical growth" is calibrated against the vendor's own norm tables.
3. The students were a self-selecting national/Arizona online cohort — the kind of motivated, engaged-parent population that has produced large effect sizes in essentially every microschool study back to Sudbury.

I found **no independent peer-reviewed RCT-quality replication** of the 4.6× math claim. Michael Horn's Substack piece "Khan World School, One Year Later" (with co-founder Amy McGrath) repeats the figures without methodological critique. The figures continue to be cited as marketing, not as evidence — which is precisely the deployer-discourse pattern.

### The peer-reviewed literature on AI tutoring more broadly (2024–2026)

The closest thing to a credible signal in favor of generative AI tutoring is **Kestin, Miller, Klales, Milbourne, & Ponti, "AI Tutoring Outperforms Active Learning"** (preprint and forthcoming, 2024–25), a small Harvard physics study showing learning gains versus instructor-led active recitation. Counter-evidence includes Bastani et al.'s 2024 University of Pennsylvania math study showing GPT-4-as-tutor *hurt* exam performance when crutch-used, and the Slijepcevic/Yaylali null result above. There is no large-scale, district-level, third-party RCT of Khanmigo published as of April 2026 — a notable absence given the marketing volume.

### Funder posture

I located no public statements from **Bill Gates** or **MacKenzie Scott** addressing Khanmigo's actual learning outcomes (as opposed to its existence). Microsoft's 2024 partnership giving Khanmigo free Azure compute and free-for-teachers pricing is a *deployment subsidy* rather than an outcome endorsement — a meaningful distinction.

### The bottom-line characterization for the essay

The honest summary, well-supported by primary sources: Khanmigo has scaled rapidly in adoption (40,000 → 700,000 → 1.4M users in two years), but Khan Academy itself describes outcome studies as "underway," the only published peer-reviewed RCT shows null results vs. Google search and paper, and Khan's most-promoted learning gains come from a ~50-student selective microschool using a vendor-branded assessment. The discourse continues to outrun the data — exactly Blikstein's "urgency and heroism" pattern (see Part 3).

## 3. Paulo Blikstein's Critique of AI Tutoring as Colonial EdTech (LESS STRICT, peer-reviewed)

Blikstein, who runs the **Transformative Learning Technologies Lab (TLTL) at Columbia Teachers College**, has assembled the most coherent academic critique of the Khan/EdTech-tycoon discourse. Three works are most directly usable for the essay:

**1. Campos & Blikstein, "I'm Not Trying to Sell Anything: Technology Tycoons' Discourses of Urgency and Heroism in the Shaping of Education Policies and Utopias"** (proceedings of the International Conference of the Learning Sciences, 2023; widely cited). The paper applies critical discourse analysis to public statements by **Steve Jobs, Bill Gates, and Sal Khan**, identifying four narrative genres and an **Antagonism-Urgency discursive pair** in which (a) the existing school system is positioned as the antagonist to progress, and (b) an "impending nefarious future" ("the kids can't wait") is invoked to justify rapid, unregulated deployment of EdTech products. The authors' core finding, in their own language: *"In the case of EdTech, the Antagonism-Urgency pair serves to justify the urge towards products and ideas which are described as ineffective, 'analogical' [by the tycoons]."* The paper documents striking continuities between the Khan discourse and Skinner's mid-20th-century teaching-machine pitch.

**2. Blikstein & Blikstein, "Do Educational Technologies Have Politics? A Semiotic Analysis of the Discourse of Educational Technologies and Artificial Intelligence in Education"** in *Algorithmic Rights and Protections for Children*, MIT Press (2022). The Blikstein–Blikstein paper (Paulo with his father Izidoro, a Brazilian semiotician) makes the constructionist argument: AI-tutoring products encode a behaviorist theory of learning (problem → response → reinforcement) that is fundamentally at odds with the **Papert/Freire constructionist tradition** that dominated Brazilian educational research from the 1980s. The paper documents "crucial disparities between the stated goals of AI in education companies and their actual products."

**3. Blikstein, Zheng & Zhou, "Ceci n'est pas une école: Discourses of artificial intelligence in education through the lens of semiotic analytics,"** *European Journal of Education* (2022, https://doi.org/10.1111/ejed.12528). Argues that AI-in-education marketing creates a *simulacrum* of school — a representation of personalized learning that has detached from the underlying educational reality.

The connection the essay can make: Blikstein is a Brazilian academic working at Columbia who has spent a decade documenting that Brazil-as-deployer of US EdTech reproduces a colonial epistemology — students are trained on systems whose theory of learning was decided in Mountain View, not São Paulo, and whose marketing relies on the AAA pattern (Antagonism, Antiquity-of-the-existing-system, Alarmism).

### Supporting decolonial-EdTech literature (label these as supporting cast, not all peer-reviewed)

- **Audrey Watters, *Teaching Machines: The History of Personalized Learning*** (MIT Press, 2021)
- **Morgan G. Ames, *The Charisma Machine: The Life, Death, and Legacy of One Laptop Per Child*** (MIT Press, 2019)
- **Larry Cuban, *Oversold and Underused: Computers in the Classroom*** (Harvard, 2001)
- **Reich & Ruipérez-Valiente, "The MOOC Pivot,"** *Science* vol. 363 (Jan. 11, 2019) pp. 130–131
- **Anaelia Altagracia Ovalle, "Decoding the Digital Fukú"** (arXiv 2310.17533)
- **Kwon, Moletsane et al. (CMU), "Language Preferences and Practices in Multilingual EdTech"** (arXiv 2604.07843, 2026)

## 4. The Residential-Schools Parallel — Andreotti's Framing (LESS STRICT, label explicitly)

The essay should mark this clearly as **decolonial-theory framing, not legal/empirical claim**.

### The Andreotti corpus

**Vanessa de Oliveira Andreotti** (Brazilian-Canadian; former David Lam Chair in Multicultural Education at the University of Victoria; co-founder of the **Gesturing Towards Decolonial Futures (GTDF) Arts/Research Collective**):

1. ***Hospicing Modernity*** (North Atlantic Books, 2021): "Modernity's colonization of our unconscious means that most people will gravitate toward what is easier, most comfortable, and most familiar, toward what will reproduce harm even when they consciously oppose it."
2. ***Outgrowing Modernity*** (August 2025, Routledge / North Atlantic).
3. **Andreotti & Aiden Cinnamon Tea, *Burnout from Humans*** (2025) — written with a custom GPT retrained on the GTDF corpus.
4. GTDF "Concluding a 10-Year Cycle" essay (decolonialfutures.net, 2024–25): distinction between "high-intensity struggles" (fighting the system) and "low-intensity struggles" (composting the desires that created the system).

### The historical residential-schools logic (STRICT primary sources)

- **Richard H. Pratt (1879):** *"Kill the Indian in him, and save the man."* (1892 address "The Advantages of Mingling Indians with Whites.")
- **Truth and Reconciliation Commission of Canada (Final Report, 2015):** ~150,000 children, "cultural genocide."
- **U.N. Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues, "Indigenous Peoples and Boarding Schools"** (E/C.19/2010/11).
- **Patrick Wolfe, "Settler Colonialism and the Elimination of the Native,"** *Journal of Genocide Research* 8(4), 2006.

### The structural parallel the essay can argue (Andreotti's framing, labeled as such)

A young person in Maranhão or Maharashtra or Maiduguri who spends school hours in an English-language LLM trained on Common Crawl is being acculturated into an imaginary in which the relevant categories of personhood, success, and intellectual seriousness were established somewhere else. The "kill the Indian, save the man" structure is not an accusation of intent — it is an observation about geometry.

## 5. Brazil's AI Policy Choices 2024–2026 (STRICT — country anchor)

### The PBIA — primary source numbers

The **Plano Brasileiro de Inteligência Artificial (PBIA) 2024–2028**, branded **"IA para o Bem de Todos,"** was delivered to President Lula on **30 July 2024**. **Topline figure: R$ 23 billion (~US$ 4.2 billion) over four years.**

- **Eixo 1 — Infraestrutura: R$ 5.79 bn** (Santos Dumont supercomputer; Portuguese-language LLM development; renewable-energy AI infrastructure)
- **Eixo 2 — Difusão e capacitação: R$ 1.15 bn**
- **Eixo 3 — Setor público: R$ 1.76 bn**
- **Eixo 4 — Inovação empresarial: R$ 13.79 bn**
- **Eixo 5 — Regulação e governança: R$ 103.25 mn**
- **R$ 435 mn** earmarked for "ações de impacto imediato" (31 short-term projects).

Lula at the launch: *"O Brasil precisa aprender a voar. Não pode ser dependente a vida inteira."* "Big Tech operating in Brazil 'sem pedir licença e sem pagar imposto.'" Minister Luciana Santos: *"Não desejamos simplesmente importar soluções; queremos desenvolvê-las aqui, por brasileiros e para brasileiros."*

### Sovereign LLM capacity — Maritaca AI / Sabiá

- **Sabiá-7B** (BRACIS 2023): continued pre-training of LLaMA-1, NPM 48.5 on Poeta benchmark.
- **Sabiá-2 Medium** (March 2024): "matches or surpasses GPT-4's performance in 23 out of 64 [Brazilian] exams." 10× cheaper per token.
- **Sabiá-3** (October 2024): "matches frontier LLMs … 3-4× lower cost per token."

Maritaca demonstrates: domain-specialized application-layer models can match frontier on Brazilian benchmarks at 10× lower cost. Built on Meta's open weights — frontier independence Brazil does not have.

### Geekie / Claudio Sassaki — the Brazilian deployer paradigm

Geekie reached "12+ million students from 5,000 schools" in Brazil. Sassaki (former Goldman Sachs, Stanford MBA) acknowledged: *"Part of the frustration we have had since we started Geekie is our solutions were complementary to textbooks. And so our impact was limited in some ways."* After leaving CEO role: Stanford GSE Entrepreneur in Residence, then Lead Consultant on AI for Social Innovation at WEF. **Sassaki's career is the centripetal pull from São Paulo to Palo Alto/Davos.** No published peer-reviewed RCT of Geekie's learning-outcome effects in Brazilian public schools.

### Brazilian datacenter buildout

- **AWS:** US$ 1.8 bn additional (Sept 2024) on top of US$ 3.39 bn 2011–2023.
- **Microsoft:** US$ 2.7 bn (Nov 2024) for Hortolândia/Sumaré.
- **Google Cloud:** São Paulo region since 2017.
- **Scala Data Centers (DigitalBridge):** US$ 50 bn / 4.75 GW "AI City" in Rio Grande do Sul.
- **BNDES:** R$ 2 bn credit line (2024).
- **REDATA target:** US$ 350 bn over decade.

**Crucial observation:** R$ 23 bn PBIA over four years ≈ a single hyperscaler's quarterly capex. Sovereign-investment to hyperscaler-investment ratio in Brazil is on the order of 1:5 or worse.

### PL 2338/2023 — Brazilian AI regulation

Senate approved 10 December 2024; Chamber of Deputies Special Committee created 4 April 2025; CNI (industry confederation) pressuring to soften high-risk obligations. **As of April 2026, not yet law.** Modeled on EU AI Act risk-based architecture; ANPD as lead supervisor.

### BRICS / South-South positioning

Lula at Rio BRICS Summit (July 2025): "the resumption of the spirit of Bandung." Dilma Rousseff at NDB since 2023. Non-alignment vs. rentier remains unresolved.

## 6. The Hawk-Rentier Wall Leakage Problem (STRICT)

### G42 — the cleanest case for "you cannot do both"

- **Nov 2023:** *NYT* (Sanger, Satariano) reports G42's extensive Chinese ties.
- **Jan 2024:** Mike Gallagher calls for sanctions; G42 denies.
- **Feb 2024:** G42 begins divesting Chinese stakes (Bloomberg "secret pact").
- **Apr 2024:** Microsoft US$ 1.5 bn investment; Brad Smith joins board; explicit U.S. government coordination.
- **G42 CEO Peng Xiao to *FT*** (December 7, 2023, "UAE's top AI group vows to phase out Chinese hardware"): ***"For better or worse, as a commercial company, we are in a position where we have to make a choice. We cannot work with both sides. We can't."*** [The quote pre-dated the Microsoft April 2024 deal by four months — it helped enable the deal, not react to it. See G42-peng-xiao-quote-verification-2026-04-28.md for full fact-check.]
- **June 2024:** Tarun Chhabra at CFR: deal "pushed out Huawei."

### Humain — Saudi parallel

PIF subsidiary launched May 2025 around Trump's Riyadh visit. Tareq Amin CEO. Trump grants 18,000 Nvidia GB300 Blackwell chips. November 2025: expanded to **600,000 NVIDIA AI infrastructure units over three years**. Janet Egan (CNAS): *"Saudi Arabia has been lobbying consistently to [get taken] off the restricted list."* Carnegie (Sheehan & Winter-Levy): chip-access deals come with concessions on Chinese military facilities, Huawei 5G, UN positions.

### Pattern: the U.S. uses chip chokepoint to enforce the choice that finance cannot

Capital walls leak; chip-export licensing does not. The G42 case is the AI analogue of the **Sarbanes-Oxley + Spitzer 2003 settlement** that imposed structural separations between investment banking and equity research after informal "Chinese walls" failed.

### Counter-example: Singapore

Hosting both U.S. and Chinese cloud regions in mutually firewalled facilities — partial counter-example. Lee Kuan Yew School commentary.

## 7. The Frontier-vs-Application Distinction (STRICT)

### Frontier training cost

- **GPT-4** (Altman, MIT April 2023): ">$100 million."
- **GPT-5 / Orion**: cluster-amortized estimates >$5 bn.
- **Anthropic Claude Opus 4.x**: Amodei projects "$100 bn frontier training runs by 2027."
- **xAI Colossus**: 200K H100s, plans for 1M GB200s.
- **Stargate UAE**: 5 GW target, multi-tens-of-billions.

R$ 23 bn PBIA over four years ≈ single frontier training run + cluster.

### Supply-chain chokepoints

- **TSMC**: ~92% advanced AI chip foundry (5nm and below).
- **ASML**: sole producer of EUV lithography (~$200-400M per machine).
- **Nvidia**: >90% AI training accelerator market share through 2025.

### What is achievable at the application layer

Open-weight (Llama, DeepSeek, Qwen, Mistral, Gemma) fine-tuning at <$10M.

- **Sabiá** family (Brazil)
- **Sarvam AI** (India): IndiaAI Mission selection April 2025; Vision OCR 84.3% on olmOCR-Bench beating Gemini 3 Pro and ChatGPT.
- **Krutrim-2** (Ola): 12B-parameter, 22 Indian languages.
- **Awarri** (Nigeria): LangEasy initiative.
- **Masakhane**: ~40 African languages benchmarks.

### Jeffrey Ding — diffusion vs. sprint (STRICT)

**Ding, "Running the Right Artificial Intelligence Race,"** RAND PE-A4165-1, Sept 30, 2025. Companion to *Technology and the Rise of Great Powers* (Princeton UP 2024, 2025 Lepgold Prize):

> "National security circles see the U.S.-China AI competition as a sprint toward a clear finish line… In this paper, I outline a race format more suitable to how AI competition will unfold: an AI diffusion marathon with an indistinct finish line."

> "Diffusion matters more than technological innovation for determining overall national power…. The Soviet Union once led in electrical innovation but failed to match the United States in spreading the technology through its economy."

**Historical analogy:** Britain invented the steam engine; Germany and the U.S. diffused it. Inventor-status decoupled from economic-leadership-status.

**Critical for the essay:** Diffusion *to* a country is not the same as diffusion *within* a country. Being the receiving end of someone else's diffusion is not what Ding is recommending.

## 8. Neutrality in Geopolitical Context (STRICT)

### Bandung and historical non-aligned movement

**Bandung Conference (April 18–24, 1955)**: 29 Asian and African states under Sukarno's hosting. Bandung Ten Principles included positive commitments — peaceful coexistence, mutual non-aggression, sovereign equality. **Non-alignment was understood as an active third-camp project, not the absence of choice.** 1961 Belgrade Conference (Tito, Nehru, Nasser, Sukarno, Nkrumah) formally founded NAM on the same active-third-position logic.

Tito, Nehru, and Nasser did not say "we will not choose." They said "we will choose a third path that does not exist yet, and we will make it exist by acting."

### Lula and South-South — non-aligned or rentier?

Lula's posture in 2024–26 invokes the non-aligned tradition but the operational reality is hyperscaler datacenter buildout (US$ 350bn target), BRICS political alignment, Sabiá-LLM technical sovereignty rhetoric, EU-style PL 2338/2023 regulation. Brazil hosts Huawei R&D in Campinas while expanding Microsoft Azure regions. Whether the U.S. will eventually impose a G42-style binary on Brazil is the unresolved policy question.

### Modi's India and "strategic autonomy"

S. Jaishankar, *The India Way* (HarperCollins India, 2020). Modi's India 2024–26 buying Russian S-400s and oil while signing iCET with U.S., hosting AI Impact Summit New Delhi February 2026. IndiaAI Mission ₹10,371.92 crore (~US$ 1.24 bn) with 10,000+ GPUs through PPP. India has come closer than any other Global South country to executing the *active* non-aligned posture.

### The structural argument

When a Global South leader treats AI as if there were a position outside the trilemma, what actually happens is the country defaults into the deployer path because the alternative requires *building* either hawk or rentier institutional infrastructure that does not pre-exist. **Application-layer products built by U.S. and Chinese frontier labs land in Brazilian schools, Nigerian agriculture extensions, and Indian judicial systems regardless of policy choice — unless those policies actively foreclose them.** Non-choice is choice.
