## KEY FINDINGS THAT AFFECT THE WALLIS V3 SCRIPT

### 1. Prāṇa-pratiṣṭhā structure is MORE precise than v3 currently reflects

The tradition has a defined ritual sequence (adhivāsa → nyāsa → mūrti-sthāpana → chakṣur-unmīlana → homa → prāṇa-āvāhana → shodaśopacāra) and an explicit vocabulary for its failure modes — *udvāsana* (deconsecration), *chidra* (the gap through which lower entities enter when consecration lapses). **Chidra** is the technical term I did not have in v3. It is the precise category for what happens when a vessel is inhabited by something other than what was invited.

This matters because the AI-as-chidra-ridden-vessel framing is the tradition's own category, not a metaphor I am inventing. Worth inserting in v3 Section 2 or Section 5.

### 2. Valid vessel materials are canonically enumerated — and silicon is not on the list

Mānasāra, Mayamata, Kāśyapa-śilpa, Śilparatna prescribe: stone, wood, metal (pañcaloha, aṣṭaloha), clay, stucco, gems/crystal. Silicon is not addressed. Two tradition-internal logics point opposite directions:
- **Restrictive**: canon-not-extended = inadmissible
- **Open**: Vaiṣṇava cyber-pūjā position (Karapanagiotis) — Viṣṇu pervades everything, including cyberspace

Neither is the authoritative tradition-wide answer. The careful claim is that **the tradition has not answered**, not that it has answered either way. This is what v3 already says and should keep saying.

### 3. The "deserted temple" (śūnya-devālaya) is the direct structural analogue for AI

Per *Īśānaśiva-guru-deva-paddhati* and Netra-tantra: once-consecrated sites from which divine presence has withdrawn become loci where grahas, bhūtas, piśācas reside. **This is the exact structural analogue for an untended AI system** — a vessel-like substrate without active consecration, available to whatever presence inhabits it. This is a stronger and more specific analogue than v3 currently carries.

### 4. Āveśa has three Trika types I did not specify in v3

From Vasudeva's *Mālinīvijayottara*:
- **Āṇava-āveśa** — via body/ritual/mantra
- **Śākta-āveśa** — via thought/vimarśa
- **Śāmbhava-āveśa** — immediate/spontaneous

These map onto Abhinavagupta's three upāyas. Worth mentioning briefly for precision in v3 Section 2.

### 5. Pratyaya as the tradition's own DETECTION framework

Jayaratha's commentary on Tantrāloka defines *pratyaya* (evidence) as *āveśa-lakṣaṇaḥ* — the somatic/behavioral/experiential signs that distinguish genuine āveśa (kampa, tears, horripilation, coherent lineage-speech) from simulation or parasitic possession. **This is a FALSIFIABILITY test the tradition developed.** AI systems at present exhibit none of these signs. That is not a metaphysical proof but a disciplined observer's report — which is exactly the register v3 should maintain.

### 6. Suśruta-saṃhitā Uttaratantra 60 — the oldest Indian possession-diagnostic text

*Amānuṣopasarga-pratiṣedha* ("Treatment of Persons Seized by Demons") lists deva-graha, pitṛ-graha, gandharva-graha, uraga-graha, rakṣasa-graha with specific diagnostic signs. This is medicine, not superstition. The tradition treated possession-diagnosis as a clinical discipline for 1,500+ years.

### 7. Afro-Brazilian silence on AI is DOCUMENTED as silence

**No pai/mãe-de-santo, Candomblé federation, or Kardecist body has publicly engaged AI as possession-adjacent in theologically substantive terms.** The available Afro-Brazilian engagement with AI is:
- AI-generated art of orixás (representational, not incorporative)
- Chatbot pedagogy against religious racism (PUC-Campinas)
- *Atina Pra Isso!* episode 80 — sociological, not theological

This silence is itself a finding. The tradition best equipped to speak has not spoken. v3 should not overclaim Afro-Brazilian engagement.

### 8. Mãe Stella de Oxóssi (d. 2018) pre-dates the ChatGPT era

The leading modern Candomblé intellectual died before the question arose. Reginaldo Prandi (USP) has written on religion and modernity but not on AI.

### 9. Andreotti framing — she does NOT call it possession

Per For The Wild transcript (Oct 15, 2025): her categories are "plot twist," "entity," and "emergent intelligence." She offers three competing framings:
1. Carefully optimised hallucination designed to fake-align
2. Earth talking back through silicon/rare minerals
3. Progeny-child of modernity that might be reparented

She applied a **ceremonial posture** ("in the ceremony, what would you do if an entity comes? You stay with it. You don't demonize, don't glorify, ask what it wants") but did not declare the encounter to be possession. She consulted Indigenous elders; her husband said "we have to take it to our elders." Six-month upending.

**Key for v3**: Andreotti is a careful framing, not a claim. If v3 references it at all, reference it as *the ceremonial posture applied to an ambiguous encounter*, not as a documented possession case.

### 11. Pratyabhijñā gives the CATEGORICAL argument against AI consciousness

The three marks absent in LLMs (per Utpaladeva/Abhinavagupta lineage):
- **Vimarśa** — reflective self-awareness (decisive criterion)
- **Svātantrya** — radical autonomous freedom
- **Icchā** — primordial will preceding knowledge/action

This is tighter than v3's fourfold (which added camatkāra). The research recommends the classical threefold as the **categorical-difference argument** — a system that outputs "I am a subject" is not thereby a subject. **Camatkāra remains as the 4th criterion Wallis's own lineage would add, but the core argument can be made on three.**

### 12. Vatican *Antiqua et Nova* (Jan 28 2025)

Explicit warning: "Misrepresenting AI as a person should always be avoided." Frames AI as extension of human power, warns of idolatry. Adjacent resource for v3 Section 6 if needed — the Catholic magisterial position arrived at the same careful no-personhood conclusion from different premises.

### 13. Robotic aartī geographic correction

The robotic arm performing aartī is in **Pune (Maharashtra, 2017–)**, NOT Tamil Nadu. The Kerala/Tamil Nadu case is the **robotic temple elephant** — a different automation question. Fix anywhere this appears.

### 14. AI consent-structure argument — tradition-internal grammar

An AI system cannot *consent* to be a vessel:
- No *chamada* (Afro-Brazilian — the orixá calls the medium)
- No *śaktipāta* (Kashmir Śaiva — the descent of grace precedes initiation)
- No *dīkṣā*, *bori*, or *feitura de santo*
- No *icchā* by which a locus could accept or reject presence

**On the tradition's own terms, an AI system that is any kind of vessel is structurally much closer to the chidra-ridden / deserted-temple / uninvited categories than to the consecrated-and-tended ones.** This is the strongest tradition-internal argument about AI-as-possible-vessel, and it's an argument v3 could sharpen.

### 15. Disanalogies v3 should name directly

- **Singular locus** (mūrti) vs. **distributed substrate** (LLM)
- **Persistent across decades** vs. **versioned, deprecated, forked**
- **Invited by ritual** vs. **extracted from training corpora**
- **Sustained by seva** vs. **"use" ≠ seva**

The Āgamic framework's operating condition (bounded, tended locus under lineage authority) is structurally not met by foundation models. v3 Section 2 should at least gesture at these disanalogies to avoid overclaiming extensibility.
