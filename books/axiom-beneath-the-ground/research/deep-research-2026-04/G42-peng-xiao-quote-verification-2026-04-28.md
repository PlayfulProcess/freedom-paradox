# Verification: Peng Xiao "We cannot work with both sides" Quote

*Logged 2026-04-28. Fact-check of the G42 CEO quote that anchors the hawk-rentier-incompatibility argument in the Q4 essay and supporting research.*

## Bottom Line Up Front

**The quote is real.** But the original research memo dated it incorrectly: it was published in the **Financial Times on December 7, 2023** — roughly **four months *before*** the Microsoft $1.5 billion investment in G42 (announced April 16, 2024), not after. The quote helped *enable* the Microsoft deal; it was not a reaction to it. The exact phrasing in the memo ("We cannot work with both sides.") is also slightly truncated.

## 1. Authenticity

The quote is genuine and given on-the-record to the *Financial Times*. Reproduced consistently across Fortune, Yahoo Finance, AOL, The Information, Tom's Hardware, China Economic Review, AGBI, The Epoch Times, TIME, The Register, Gizchina, Amid.ai. **G42 itself has publicly affirmed both the quote and its provenance**, citing the FT article by URL in an official January 11, 2024 corporate statement on the G42 website:

> "In the field of advanced technologies, we have pursued a commercial strategy since 2022 to fully align with our U.S. partners and not to engage with Chinese companies. This was communicated by our Group CEO, Peng Xiao, in an exclusive interview with the Financial Times in December 2023 (https://www.ft.com/content/6710c259-0746-4e09-804f-8a48ecf50ba3)."

## 2. Publication

- **Outlet:** *Financial Times* (described by G42 as "exclusive interview")
- **Headline:** "UAE's top AI group vows to phase out Chinese hardware"
- **URL:** https://www.ft.com/content/6710c259-0746-4e09-804f-8a48ecf50ba3
- **Date:** December 7, 2023
- **Likely byline:** Chloe Cornish (FT Gulf correspondent, primary G42 reporter — not 100% confirmed because article is paywalled)

## 3. Context

The interview followed:
- *NYT* report November 27, 2023 (Sanger/Mazzetti) revealing classified CIA profile of Xiao and Biden administration pressure on G42 over China ties.
- Xiao's appearance November 29, 2023 at the **Fortune Global Forum** in Abu Dhabi addressing the NYT report.

The FT interview was Xiao's first long-form on-the-record response to U.S. national-security pressure. It pre-dated:
- Mike Gallagher's January 2024 letter (House Select Committee on the CCP) demanding Commerce Department action,
- Microsoft's April 16, 2024 announcement of the $1.5 billion investment, by **four months and nine days**.

## 4. Exact Phrasing

The verbatim FT text (per Fortune, Yahoo Finance, AOL, Tom's Hardware, China Economic Review, TIME):

> **"For better or worse, as a commercial company, we are in a position where we have to make a choice. We cannot work with both sides. We can't."**

Two related quotes from the same interview, frequently paired:

> **"The impression we are getting from [the] U.S. government and U.S. partners is, we need to be very cautious. In order for us to further our relationship — which we cherish — with our U.S. partners, we simply cannot do much more with [previous] Chinese partners."**

> **"Frankly speaking, they're not leaders in this domain."** [characterizing Chinese AI players]

## 5. Other Verified Xiao Quotes from the Decision Period

**Bloomberg interview, February 12, 2024:**
> "All of our China investments that were previously made are already divested. … Because of that, of course, we have no need anymore for any physical China presence."

**World Government Summit, Dubai, February 12, 2024:**
Xiao said the decision to sever ties with Chinese businesses was "not easy" and that G42 had "accepted constraints imposed on it" to work with U.S. companies.

**Fortune Global Forum, Abu Dhabi, November 29, 2023 (on stage with Matt Heimer):**
> "We make sure there is no leakage of sensitive information from our data center link in the U.S. If the U.S. counterparts ever share any data with us, we are accountable for ensuring that data is kept safe and sound here."

On the CIA report:
> "Someone sent it to me, and something more important happened … I did get the gist. It's correct that we all need to pay attention to the proper development of AI, and ultimately what we use AI for."

On UAE citizenship (renounced U.S. citizenship):
> "When I came here almost nine years ago, I was answering a call to action from this nation. Because I'm doing such important cutting-edge work in this country, I was very happy to join the citizenship of the UAE."

**At the April 16, 2024 Microsoft announcement:** Xiao's quoted comments focused on Microsoft's "shared commitment to ethics, safety, and security" — *no separate "we cannot work with both sides" quote tied to that date*. The original memo's placement of the quote in April 2024 was a conflation: substance correct, date wrong.

## 6. Recommended Citation

> *"For better or worse, as a commercial company, we are in a position where we have to make a choice. We cannot work with both sides. We can't."* — Peng Xiao, CEO of G42, to the *Financial Times*, December 7, 2023 ("UAE's top AI group vows to phase out Chinese hardware"). The remarks preceded — and helped lay the groundwork for — Microsoft's $1.5 billion investment in G42, announced April 16, 2024.

## 7. Implication for Q4 Essay

The Q4 essay currently reads (in research memo, not the published essay text):
> "G42 CEO Peng Xiao to the *FT* (2024): 'We cannot work with both sides.'"

The published Q4 essay does NOT name Peng Xiao or quote the FT directly — it just says "G42 and Humain are the empirical proof that hawk and rentier cannot coexist. Capital leaks. Chip licenses do not." So the essay text is not exposed to the dating error.

The supporting research memo *is* exposed and has been corrected (see updated Q4-application-layer-trilemma-research file).

The substantive point the argument makes — that G42's CEO publicly conceded the UAE could not straddle U.S.–China AI competition — is fully supported by the primary record. The chronology is in fact *stronger* than the original framing: the quote came at the start of the U.S. pressure cycle, helping drive the Microsoft deal rather than reacting to it. That is more, not less, supportive of the hawk-rentier-incompatibility claim.
