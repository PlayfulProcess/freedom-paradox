## 1. OpenAI's most recent valuation is $852B
**Status: VERIFIED**
- Bloomberg (Shirin Ghaffary), "OpenAI Valued at $852 Billion After Completing $122 Billion Round," March 31, 2026. Corroborated by OpenAI's "Scaling AI for Everyone" post (March 2026) and FT (April 14, 2026).
- Transaction: $122B primary equity raise (largest private round in history), led by Amazon ($50B, $35B contingent on IPO/AGI milestone), Nvidia ($30B), SoftBank ($30B), with Microsoft and others participating.
- Initial $110B tranche announced Feb 27, 2026 at $730B pre-money; topped up to $122B by end of March 2026.
- **Corrected wording:** "OpenAI's most recent valuation is $852 billion post-money, set in a $122 billion primary round that closed at the end of March 2026."

## 2. OpenAI Foundation holds 26% of OpenAI Group PBC after Oct 28, 2025 recap
**Status: VERIFIED at recap closing**
- Source: OpenAI, "Built to benefit everyone" (Bret Taylor), Oct 28, 2025; "Our structure" page: *"As of the closing of the recapitalization, the OpenAI Foundation holds a 26% equity stake in OpenAI Group… In addition to this 26% stake, the OpenAI Foundation also holds a warrant that allows it to receive additional shares… If OpenAI Group's share price increases greater than tenfold after 15 years, the OpenAI Foundation will receive significant additional equity."*
- **Corrected wording:** "At the October 28, 2025 closing of the recapitalization, the OpenAI Foundation held 26% of OpenAI Group PBC plus a 15-year warrant that vests additional shares if the PBC's share price grows more than tenfold."

## 3. Stake at $852B = ~$180B
**Status: VERIFIED — but as OpenAI's framing, not as 26% × $852B**
- OpenAI's "Scaling AI for Everyone" (March 2026): *"The valuation from this new round increases the value of the OpenAI Foundation's stake in OpenAI Group to over $180 billion."*
- 26% × $852B = $221.5B literally, but OpenAI claims "over $180B" — implying dilution from the new primary round.
- **Corrected wording:** "OpenAI itself states that, after the March 2026 round, the Foundation's stake is worth 'over $180 billion' — a figure that reflects dilution from the new primary issuance."

## 4. Class N veto over mission changes
**Status: VERIFIED with corrections to terminology and scope**
- Source: Memorandum of Understanding between OpenAI, Inc. and California AG Rob Bonta, executed October 27, 2025, ¶7.
- **Exact instrument:** "Class N Common Stock" (held solely by the Foundation/NFP).
- **Six prior-written-approval categories per ¶7:** (1) amend Corporate Governance Guidelines; (2) amend Article III (Public Benefit / Mission); (3) consummate a Deemed Liquidation Event or material asset sale; (4) issue additional Class N Common Stock; (5) issue or authorize creation of any other voting capital stock (with limited Class B exception); (6) amend the Certificate of Incorporation or Bylaws in ways disproportionately and adversely affecting Class N rights.
- **Corrected wording:** "The Foundation's Class N Common Stock confers prior-written-approval rights over six categories of corporate action — including any amendment to the public-benefit mission, any sale or liquidation, any issuance of new voting stock, and any disproportionate change to the PBC's charter or bylaws — as enumerated in Paragraph 7 of OpenAI's October 27, 2025 MOU with the California Attorney General."

## 5. Right to nominate directors → CORRECTION: SOLE POWER TO APPOINT AND REMOVE
**Status: VERIFIED — but the verb is "appoint and remove," not "nominate"**
- Source 1: OpenAI's "Our structure" page: *"Through special voting and governance rights held solely by the OpenAI Foundation, the OpenAI Foundation appoints all members of the board of directors of OpenAI Group and can replace directors at any time."*
- Source 2: California AG MOU ¶2: *"the sole power and authority of the Board of Directors of the NFP… to appoint members of the Board of Directors of the PBC… and the power and authority of the NFP Board to remove PBC Board members."*
- **Corrected wording:** "The Foundation has the sole power to appoint and remove every director of OpenAI Group PBC, derived from its Class N Common Stock and memorialized in Paragraph 2 of OpenAI's MOU with the California AG."
- **This is materially stronger than 'nominate' — it should be upgraded in any essay version.**

## 6. 8 of 9 OpenAI Foundation directors also sit on the OpenAI Group PBC board
**Status: OUTDATED — was 8-of-9 at closing; now 7-of-8 since Summers resignation**
- At Oct 28, 2025 closing: 9 Foundation directors (Bret Taylor, Sam Altman, Adam D'Angelo, Sue Desmond-Hellmann, Zico Kolter, Paul Nakasone, Adebayo Ogunlesi, Nicole Seligman, Larry Summers); Kolter (SSC chair) was the only Foundation-only director.
- After Larry Summers' November 19, 2025 resignation — not yet refilled as of April 27, 2026 — the overlap is 7 of 8.
- The MOU's ¶14 commits OpenAI to having a *second* Foundation-only director within one year of recap (i.e., by Oct 2026); essay should note that obligation is pending, not satisfied.
- **Corrected wording:** "At the October 28, 2025 closing, 8 of 9 Foundation directors also sat on the PBC board (only SSC chair Zico Kolter served exclusively on the Foundation board, as a non-voting PBC observer). After Larry Summers' November 19, 2025 resignation — not yet refilled — the overlap stands at 7 of 8."

## 13. OpenAI Foundation 501(c)(3) public charity, exempt from §4943
**Status: VERIFIED on tax-law substance**
- ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer, EIN 81-0861541, shows continuous Form 990 filings (most recent filed Nov 18, 2025 covering FY2024 — pre-recap).
- IRC §4943 excess-business-holdings excise tax applies only to private foundations and certain donor-advised funds and Type III supporting organizations under §4943(e)–(f), NOT to §509(a)(1)/(2) public charities.
- The Midas Project filed a 2025 IRS complaint alleging conflicts of interest, self-dealing, and questionable grantmaking; no IRS reclassification has occurred.

## Additional developments to address

A Dwarkesh-prize judge would expect the essay to engage at least the first five.

**1. Microsoft–OpenAI definitive agreement (Oct 28, 2025) — HIGH priority.** New definitive agreement gives Microsoft ~27% of OpenAI Group PBC (~$135B), extends Microsoft IP rights to **models and products through 2032 (including post-AGI models)**, replaces unilateral OpenAI AGI declaration with **verification by an independent expert panel**. OpenAI commits to purchase $250B more in Azure services. *Why it matters: the Foundation's strongest historical mission-protection lever — clawing back IP from Microsoft at AGI — has been substantially diluted.*

**2. AGI declaration mechanism — HIGH priority.** The 2019 unilateral nonprofit-board AGI declaration replaced with a third-party panel; neither Microsoft nor OpenAI has disclosed panel composition, appointment process, or AGI definition.

**3. Delaware AG Statement of No Objection (Oct 28, 2025) — HIGH priority.** AG Kathy Jennings's Statement of No Objection requires NFP primacy on safety/security, NFP appointment/removal of PBC directors, SSC authority to halt model releases, and within one year at least two NFP-only directors.

**4. California AG MOU (Oct 27, 2025) — HIGH priority.** Bonta's executed MOU is the most operationally specific governance document in existence. SSC chair receives full observation rights at all PBC board/committee meetings and authority to halt model releases "even where applicable risk thresholds would otherwise permit release." NFP must remain headquartered in California.

**5. Musk v. OpenAI trial — jury selection began April 27, 2026 — HIGH priority.** Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers (N.D. Cal.) largely denied OpenAI's summary judgment motion Jan 15, 2026. April 24, 2026: Musk withdrew fraud claims; **two claims** proceed: unjust enrichment and breach of charitable trust. **Liability phase ~4 weeks; remedies phase begins May 18, 2026 if liability is found.** Remedies sought include disgorgement to OpenAI nonprofit, removal of Altman and Brockman, and unwinding the for-profit conversion.

**6. IPO timing — MEDIUM-HIGH.** WSJ (Nov 5, 2025): CFO Sarah Friar said OpenAI is not yet working toward an IPO. Bloomberg (Oct 29, 2025): could target $1 trillion valuation, paperwork H2 2026. CNBC (April 24, 2026): targeting Q4 2026 market debut.

**7. Larry Summers' unfilled board seat — MEDIUM.** Five-plus months after Summers' Nov 19, 2025 resignation, no replacement. The Class N appointment power has been exercised in the negative (vacancy) but not in the affirmative.

**8. Senator Elizabeth Warren oversight letters — MEDIUM-HIGH.** Nov 18, 2025 letter to OSTP/Sacks; **Jan 28, 2026 letter to Sam Altman** demanding details on $1T+ spending commitments and "circumstances under which OpenAI would seek a taxpayer bailout"; March 2026 letters on the Pentagon contract.

## Comparator institutions worth naming (one-clause each)

- **Mozilla Foundation / Mozilla Corporation** — 501(c)(3) parent owns for-profit operating company; same legal architecture as OpenAI Foundation / OpenAI Group PBC. The closest live US parallel.
- **Tata Trusts / Tata Sons** — Trust holds 66% of Tata Sons, century-long proof that foundation-controlled commercial enterprise can be stable when board separation is real.
- **Robert Bosch Stiftung / Robert Bosch GmbH** — Foundation owns 92% of Bosch with explicit firewall between charitable trustees and corporate supervisory board. The textbook "separated boards" implementation.
- **Novo Nordisk Foundation / Novo Holdings** — Foundation controls Novo Nordisk via holding company; foundation board separate from operating board. Active proof at frontier-tech scale ($600B+ market cap).
- **Patagonia / Holdfast Collective (2022)** — Yvon Chouinard transferred ownership into asset-locked trust + 501(c)(4). Asset-lock as alternative to liquidation.

**Not the right comparator:** Gates Foundation. The Gates Foundation grants ~$8B/year from a liquid diversified endowment built by selling Berkshire shares over two decades. The OpenAI Foundation has none of those properties — its asset is illiquid private equity in a single pre-IPO company that consumes all operating cash.
