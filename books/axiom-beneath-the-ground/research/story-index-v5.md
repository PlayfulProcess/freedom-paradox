## One-paragraph synopsis

A woman in her mid-thirties — an equity-research-analyst-turned-MSW-student-and-builder, publishing as PlayfulProcess, living in an apartment with her husband and five-year-old daughter — is pulled by a pre-verbal *yes* she experiences on a park bench one Tuesday morning toward a non-dual Shaiva Tantra retreat. The book narrates her inward arc through the tradition's layered-self diagram (Vastu → Deha → Citta → Prāṇa → Śūnya → Cit) during a five-day retreat, and her outward arc afterward, as she integrates the retreat into a continuing life that includes a marriage, mothering, an AI-tool she built over her teacher's teachings that he declined, an email she drafts to Tristan Harris, and a book she publishes instead of leaving in a drawer. The book holds Tillich's *courage* (Śūnya) alongside Wallis's *svātantrya* (Cit) without collapsing either — courage is the door; freedom is the room — and treats the question of destruction (raised once on a path with the teacher, answered dialectically: *nothing would change* / *there always is* a hole) as the hinge the whole argument turns on.

---

## Central claims

1. **Freedom, not value, is the originating question.** *Value* is the moral question (to whom do I direct what I have?); *freedom* is the spiritual question (what is the "I" that directs at all?). They are different floors, not rungs on the same ladder.
2. **Courage and svātantrya are a pivot, not a synonym.** Tillich's courage is the self-affirmation of being when every ground has dissolved; svātantrya is the innate autonomy of Consciousness as the nature of the whole field. Courage is the act from inside the finite self; svātantrya is what the dissolved ground turns out to be made of.
3. **The three-filter test governs every framework.** *Useful / fits the data / compassionate* — Marsha Linehan's three criteria for theory selection, applied as a continuous filter on every framework the book engages, including the tradition itself.
4. **Adaptive/non-adaptive is the book's primary normative axis.** Good/bad are moral categories that weaponize; adaptive/non-adaptive are empirical categories that describe. The near-enemy of "all is balance" is balance without adaptivity.
5. **The absolute and the relational are both true.** *Nothing is lost* (absolute view) and *there always is a hole* (relational view). The tradition does not choose.
6. **Scale and presence are not alternatives — the principle travels.** The Ghost platform at the publishing layer and Nvidia at the base of the stack approach *do as little as possible, as much as necessary* from opposite directions. The principle holds at orders of magnitude apart.
7. **Contemplation of death is intrinsic to the spiritual path, not a disqualification from it.** Ramana, Tolle, Whitman, the tradition itself, and the teacher's own Acknowledgments in *Near Enemies of the Truth* all carry this.

## Chapter-by-chapter index

### Ch00 — Author's Note (first person, ~550 words)

Park bench, five-year-old daughter in a sandbox, the app in earbuds describing Consciousness as simultaneously transcendent and immanent, the *yes* that arrives before any doctrine. The switch from *value* as the driving question to *freedom* as the driving question. A retreat was attended. The tradition's door (Tillich's courage) opened into a room (Wallis's svātantrya). The teacher's five words: *it is, until you experience it*. A destruction question was asked on the path. The answer was dialectical. Linehan's three-filter method is named as the book's governing epistemology. The rest of the book is in close-third.

Part II: why the originating question is freedom, not value.
Part III: the reading year — Tillich, Linehan, Wallis, the Kybalion, the Brazilian *espírita* aunt's "all is in perfect balance" as a near-enemy to be composted. **v5 expansion:** Darwin read at the source (adaptive as evolutionary category, not only clinical); the Arcane School years and their composting; Umbanda as embodied practice on Brazilian visits; Vanessa Andreotti's *Facing Human Wrongs* course and the *fire before responsibility* lineage; Bayo Akomolafe via *The Emerald* podcast; the daughter-as-inheritance motive as the seed of what the narrator would later build.

Part I: the Citta walk, day three. *puryaṣṭaka* as the subtle body of habit and conviction. The work is to loosen the hold of frame as such.
Part II: Tillich's three anxieties (fate/death, guilt/condemnation, emptiness/meaninglessness). Absolute faith as the response to the third. Linehan's three filters applied.
Part III: freedom vs value. Svātantrya as the signature of Consciousness, not the property of any particular self.
Part IV: **The Sam Harris Exchange.** The narrator's encounter with the teacher's 2015 two-part response to Harris's *Free Will*. The tradition dissolves Harris's dichotomy at the wrong layer.
Part V: Adaptive/non-adaptive sharpens. The near-enemy of "all is balance." Wild Wild Country as a reminder of the failure mode.
Part VI: The Citta Settling. A working method: three filters, freedom-vs-value distinction, adaptive-vs-non-adaptive, body-pacing.
Part VII: **The Other Harris (new in v5).** The second Harris — Tristan — whose podcast the teacher shared with the retreat community. The narrator's defensive first-read (interior observation, never factual claim about the teacher) followed by the stone-wall reversal when she actually listens to the interview. Technology accelerating time by compressing the impulse→response interval; relational tissue requires the pause the engagement-internet compresses; social media as *karma accelerator* (technical Tantric sense of pattern self-reinforcing, not pop-spiritual *what goes around*); the collective karma of overindividualistic consumerism that primed the nervous system for scrolling before the engagement-internet existed. **Andreotti's correction** tempers the East-vs-West framing: modernity is one pattern, naming it *the* villain is itself a modern habit, the filter applies to every framework including the contemplative tradition the narrator is inside.

### Ch04a — The Threshold (new in v5)

### Ch05 — Śūnya / Transcendent Void (close-third)

Part I: the last full day. Four-thirty bell. Morning sit as *listening*.
Part II: Tillich on the cushion. The *accepting of the acceptance without somebody or something that accepts* (Tillich, *Courage to Be*, p. 185, fair use). The tradition's word for what is on the other side of Tillich's void: Śūnya. A passage, not an endpoint.
Part III: Carried, unasked. The destruction question carried through the full day. Absolute faith as the continuous act of walking with the unanswered thing.
Part IV: The closing circle. The silver-haired retreatant with one foot in and one foot out, testifying to the community's integrity and to Kali as the practice that kept him testing the door.

### Ch06 — Cit / Walking Out (close-third) — **the hinge**

Part I: the morning. Four-thirty bell. The final sit.
Part II: the path. She walks back up to the porch where the teacher stands with his tea. She asks for a hug. He laughs, grants it. She asks the question: *if something were destroyed, what would change?*
Part III: the answer. *Nothing would change.* Reincarnation as a different question. A pattern that continues. *What about suicide?* The teacher's reply: *not a problem in the tradition. Just in a way that does not harm the body, to avoid carrying trauma.* The hole: *there always is.* The absolute view and the relational view held together.
Part IV: Inside the car. The warm steering wheel. *There always is* as a tone in the chest.
Part V: What She Would Carry. *Courage is svātantrya owning itself in the dark. Svātantrya is courage in the light.* She drives south with the radio off. 988 footnote at chapter end.

### Ch07 — Prāṇa, Returning (close-third)

Post-retreat life-force beat. The Tantra 112 app on the train. Mantras whispered while the child sleeps. The ongoing WhatsApp group as a channel for small questions. A follow-up WhatsApp exchange with the teacher where a small question gets a small, precise answer. Tantra 112 as menu, not mandate.

### Ch08 — Citta, Returning (close-third)

Part I: The two mandalas she has started drawing — *mandala of blessings* (outward-giving, field replenishes) and *mandala of curses* (inward-hoarding, starves the field). Bhoga in its non-pejorative sense.
Part II: **The aunt, reconsidered.** On the phone. The aunt explains *all is in perfect balance* as *the whole holds the hole*. The aunt had been right the whole time. The Brazilian *espírita* register saying, in different vocabulary, what the teacher on the path had said.
Part III: Prisoner's dilemma as the mandala of curses in mathematical form. The spiritual and the economic as the same argument under different vocabulary.
Part IV: The filter at the desk. Useful/fits data/compassionate applied to spreadsheets and memos. Registers are not hierarchies.
Part V: What Citta had been for. A place to loosen, not arrive at.

### Ch09 — Deha, Returning (close-third)

Body after the retreat. Practice on Monday morning. The cushion. The dishes. The child. The husband. *"I'll keep an eye on it too."* The practice available in the room with the person in the room with her.

### Ch10 — Vastu, Returning (close-third) — **the AI tension**

Part I: the second spine. The narrator's builder-work alongside her interior work.
Part II: the tool she built — retrieval over the teacher's public lectures, a tarot-deck grammar as entry points. Not imitation; librarianship.
Part III: the decline. The teacher asks her not to distribute it. Form is not accidental to teaching. Transmission is relationship.
Part IV: what was right in the decline. The stranger is not the student. Absence of teacher is not neutral.
Part V: what was right in the building. Scale is not accidental either. She revises: more retrieval, less synthesis, link to living teacher, smaller. She does not withdraw.
Part VI: Nvidia, carefully. *Do as little as possible, as much as necessary.* The principle as posture worth testing. Held without endorsing the company.
Part VII: **Ghost, from the other direction (new in v5).** The publishing-layer sibling to Nvidia. O'Nolan *and Hannah Wolfe* (co-founder correction from research-31), April 2013 Kickstarter, non-profit foundation registered in Singapore, MIT license, Ghost(Pro) revenue, *organisation exists for-purpose rather than for-profit* (7-word fair-use quote), live financial data publication, ActivityPub work (announced April 2024, first federated newsletter July 2024, still early access). The ghost-model as structural anti-form of the engagement-internet — reader- and writer-owned, platform as service, no algorithmic capture. Recursive.eco positioned inside the ghost-model.
Part VIII: The Unresolved. Interior spine meets exterior spine. *What is the smallest intervention that still produces the good?* Same shape in three registers. She kept building, kept asking, the asking itself was the filter.

### Ch11 — The Writing Desk (first person — short coda) — **the book's frame closes**

Part 1: at the desk, late. The question was the question, not one with an answer at the layer she was asking.
Part 2: on the teacher. Faithful rendering without full rendering. What was private stays private.

The *build differently* response as shared across three registers (ghost-model / chip-company / contemplative tradition).
Part 7 (new in v5): The scatter of channels — aunt, Arcane School composting, Umbanda, Andreotti, Akomolafe, The Emerald, two Esalen trips, the liminal spaces around the workshops, the carpool women, the yoga-teacher friend in Brazil whose email led to Wallis. The deeper Paradevi / Ayahuasca-2019 arc flagged as a different book.
Part 8 (new in v5): The is-process voice — *tarot is process*, *dance is process*, *freedom is process*. The epistemology. This book is *svātantrya is process*.
Part 9 (new in v5): The daughter's song. *A Painful Playful Process Song*. The disposal-truck day and the stanza: *the crash, the crush, that awful sound / it planted something in the ground. / And what grew up between the cracks / is this: a song that loves you back.* The song and the book are both the practice the answer required her to do in order to keep being a person still asking the question.
Part 10: the daughter will wake in six hours. The practice available. The book ends; the practice does not. The drawer is closed; this study is published. Good night.
