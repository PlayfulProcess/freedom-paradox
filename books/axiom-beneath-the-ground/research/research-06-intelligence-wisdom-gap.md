## Core Thesis

Intelligence — the trait humanity celebrates most — may be its most dangerous. The form of intelligence amplified through AI is precisely the narrow, decontextualized, manipulative mode that every wisdom tradition warns against. The AI moment is the culmination of a pattern visible from Pleistocene megafauna extinctions through Sumer's salinized fields to the nuclear age: intelligence outrunning wisdom at every scale.

## Key Findings for the Books

### 1. Intelligence is not monolithic
- g-factor accounts for 40-50% of variance but what g IS remains contested
- Mutualism model (van der Maas 2006): g as index variable like Dow Jones, not causal entity
- Process Overlap Theory: "most cognitive scientists agree there is no such thing as general intelligence; psychometricians have become remarkably good at measuring it"
- LLMs show positive manifold (r=0.73) but this reflects shared training data, not intelligence
- ARC-AGI-3 (2026): current AI scores below 1% on novel reasoning tasks humans solve consistently

### 2. Intelligence is embodied, cultural, and extended — not computational
- 4E cognition: embodied, embedded, enacted, extended
- Dreyfus (vindicated): genuine intelligence requires embodiment, being-in-the-world, tacit knowledge
- Tomasello: children and chimps equal on physical cognition; children far superior on SOCIAL cognition only
- Henrich's collective brain: "It's our collective brains, not individual brains, that explain our fancy technologies"
- Tasmanian technology regression: when collective brain shrinks below threshold, knowledge erodes despite individual intelligence remaining constant
- AI has no shared intentionality, no cultural membership, no genuine social cognition

### 3. The cognitive niche is inherently self-destructive
- Tooby/DeVore: humans evolved to mount "evolutionary surprise attacks" — prey can't evolve counter-adaptations fast enough
- Megafauna extinctions on every continent correlated with human arrival
- Sternberg (2019): "greater general intelligence leading to species self-destructive behavior" is the paradox
- Garrett (2024): AI may be the Great Filter — civilizations last ~100-200 years after widespread AI adoption
- E.O. Wilson: "Paleolithic emotions, medieval institutions, and god-like technology"

### 4. Progress traps and complexity collapse
- Tainter: societies solve easy problems first; complexity faces diminishing marginal returns; AI is the latest and most dramatic escalation
- Wright: progress traps — initial success creates dependency, costs accumulate invisibly. "An Ice Age hunter only half-evolved towards intelligence; clever but seldom wise"
- Quinn: Taker culture's conviction that intelligence can overcome any natural limit

### 5. Every wisdom tradition distinguishes intelligence from wisdom
- Buddhist prajñā: transcendent wisdom categorically different from conceptual knowledge. "Highest wisdom requires transformation of the knower, not improvement of the knowing system"
- Kashmir Shaivism: cit (pure consciousness) vs buddhi (limited intellect). AI operates entirely at buddhi level with zero access to cit
- Yogic viveka: existential discernment requiring consciousness, not data classification
- McGilchrist: AI as "the left hemisphere's apotheosis" — narrow, focused, manipulative attention without the right hemisphere's contextual, embodied, empathic awareness

### 6. AI intensifies Tillich's three existential anxieties
- 92.7% of participants in 2024 study reported fear of meaninglessness as primary AI concern
- Fate: opaque algorithms shape life outcomes contingently
- Guilt: automated judgments on human worth without moral understanding
- Meaninglessness: automation of creative work, synthetic meaning, erosion of authentic/fabricated distinction

### 7. The fire myths across cultures
- Greek Prometheus: technology as illicit power, divine prerogative seized by unworthy
- Hindu Samudra Manthan: poison emerges BEFORE benefits; containment requires divine wisdom and self-sacrifice (Shiva swallows the poison)
- Chinese Suiren: technology as natural human development, no theft, no punishment
- The Hindu parallel most resonant for AI: powerful technologies inevitably produce both treasure and poison; poison first; containing it requires wisdom no technical solution can substitute

### 8. Non-human intelligence challenges all our definitions
- Octopuses: 500M neurons, 2/3 in arms not brain, intelligence distributed not centralized
- New Caledonian crows: metatool problems on first trial, causal reasoning about hidden agents
- Orca cultural transmission: distinct hunting traditions, vocal dialects, so rigid captive orcas died rather than eat dead fish
- Mycorrhizal networks: carbon exchange, defense signals — "cognition" without neurons
- Intelligence evolved independently at least 3 times in complex organisms

## For the Axiom Book

The intelligence-wisdom gap IS the axiom-recognition gap at the cognitive level:
- Intelligence (buddhi, left hemisphere, g-factor) can pose the question of inherent value but cannot answer it
- Wisdom (prajñā, cit, right-hemisphere integration) is the mode in which the answer lives — but wisdom requires transformation of the knower, not just improvement of the knowing system
- AI amplifies intelligence without any access to wisdom — it is the ultimate expression of the axiom problem: you can compute everything about the ground without ever standing on it
- The person in the void (Ch 3) is experiencing the intelligence-wisdom gap from the inside: their intelligence can formulate the question "do I have inherent value?" but their intelligence CANNOT answer it, because the answer lives in a mode of knowing that intelligence cannot access

The key sentence for the book: **"Intelligence asks the question. Wisdom lives the answer. The gap between them is the void."**
