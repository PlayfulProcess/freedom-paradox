# Bayo Audit — Axiom v5 + Revision Plan 2026-04-22

*A fugitive reading. Paraphrased, never quoted. Slow.*

---

## What the book is trying to arrive at

The manuscript wants to arrive at a posture — a *willing-to-be-wrong-in-public* builder's yes, held inside an adaptive ethic that refuses both dogma and nihilism, anchored by three traditions (Śaiva, Choctaw sacred fire, AI safety) that are said to converge on one structural diagnosis: orthogonality.

The revision plan wants to arrive somewhere quieter — to downshift the convergence-claim from *truth* to *what has served*, and to insert one interlude (the $25k AI-spending friend) where the book lets itself be implicated.

What Akomolafe asks, in *These Wilds Beyond Our Fences* (2017) and across his teaching: *where is the refusing-to-arrive?* Because the arriving is what the crisis does. The grammar of solution wears many costumes.

I read this manuscript with care. It has real cracks. It seals most of them. Where it seals, I will press.

---

## 1. The grammar of solution wearing the costume of critique

Ch10d is the clearest instance. The chapter diagnoses the orthogonality trap — capability without container — and then proposes, over the course of twenty paragraphs, a *constructive adaptive ethic* with five properties (testable, contextual, relational, humble, constructive). Linehan's theory of change is imported as the engine. *Build alternatives; the old becomes unnecessary.*

This is the grammar of solution. Clean, Hirschman-Mazzucato-certified, attached to the author's actual platform. The critique-pose is that dogma is closure. The solution-pose is that an *open* platform with the right filter will do what dogma could not.

The same grammar. Different register. The post-activist question is not *is the platform good?* It is: *why is the book certain that a better container is the answer, when the container-grammar itself is what produced every previous container that failed?* Every empire thought it was building a wiser container than the last. Every wellness platform thought the filter would hold.

The book knows this danger when it looks at YouTube Kids. It does not turn the same lens on itself. *recursive.eco* is introduced with the posture of the constructor who has learned from the failures — but the constructor is always the one who has learned. That is the trap.

Where the book could have been fugitive: the moment where *mostly no one has come* (Ch10d, near the end). That paragraph is the crack. The author almost lets the platform be a failure. Then she seals: *staying open is also the only way the right people will find their way.* The sealing is exactly the move Akomolafe names in *We Will Tell Our Own Story* — the redemptive arc reasserting itself as soon as the grief would have become load-bearing.

---

## 2. The AI-spending interlude — receipt or solution-gesture?

The revision plan asks whether the interlude lets itself be a receipt rather than an argument. Reading the plan's sketch: I fear it has already been domesticated.

The interlude, as drafted in the plan, names the pattern (capability-without-household-consent), imports Lembke's dopamine analysis as a frame, argues the monetization sub-argument (consumer tier reproduces social-media incentives), and closes with a falsifiability clause: *if her own usage crosses an unnamed threshold, the interlude becomes the indictment of the book.*

This is not a receipt. This is a thesis with a conditional self-destruct button.

A receipt would be: the friend spent $25,000. His wife did not know. The author does not know what to do with this. The paragraph ends.

Instead, the plan's sketch turns the friend into a case, Lembke into the analytic framework, and the author into the one who has already seen the pattern and named it first. The friend is made legible. Making someone legible is a form of capture. In *These Wilds Beyond Our Fences* Akomolafe warns about the ethnography that arrives to understand. The understanding is the continuation of the extraction.

The fugitive version of this interlude would refuse the frame. It would leave Lembke out. It would refuse the monetization argument (which is the *economist-in-her* reasserting composure). It would sit with the $25,000 as a weight the book cannot lift. It would let the wife's not-knowing be a grief, not a structural illustration.

The monetization paragraph in particular is where the book stops being interrupted and starts arguing again. The author is not in that paragraph. An analyst is.

## 3. Karina Walters — disturbance or decoration?

The Choctaw sacred fire enters Ch10a through a video the author is assigned to watch for her MSW program. Walters is cited as the teacher. The story is rendered with care. Then: *three vocabularies. One shape. A defiled fire is a fire about which the truth has not been told.*

The Choctaw teaching is recruited into the three-tradition convergence argument — lined up beside Wallis and Amodei as the third leg. The three legs stabilize a claim the book needs. Walters is the third leg.

This is extraction in the register of honor.

A Choctaw sacred-fire teaching carries obligations. The relit fire must come from the *neighboring villages* — from outside the closed circle. That sentence is already in the manuscript. The book does not ask: *is this book the closed circle relighting itself? Am I borrowing a flame from Walters to relight my own argument, and if so, what have I given to Walters's people in return?* The teaching disturbs if it is allowed to. The book quotes it and moves on.

The CARE principles are named in CLAUDE.md. They are not operative in Ch10a. Walters is a lecturer on a video the author paused. The Choctaw Nation is backdrop. The fire is material. This is the pattern *We Will Tell Our Own Story* was co-edited against.

The repair is not more attribution. The repair would be to let the Choctaw teaching *refuse* the three-tradition frame. It does not want to be the third leg of a convergence argument alongside an AI safety researcher. It has its own covenant, its own sickness, its own relighting protocol. The book's use of it as *evidence* is the defilement the story itself describes.

This is the sentence the book does not know is its key sentence: *the fix is to put it out entirely and kindle it again from the sacred fires of neighboring villages.* Applied to the manuscript itself, that sentence is not a metaphor. It is an instruction. The book has not followed it.

## 4. Grief the book is rushing past

Ch00a ends with *the longing to know the dance is the practice. The longing is the book.* Beautiful. Also a foreclosure. The longing is made into practice, which is to say the longing is put to work. Longing that is working is longing that has been relieved of its full weight.

Ch11 has the Josh Schrei arc — the Alembic, the two unanswered questions, the Devil card, the tier-downgrade. The arc is told with almost-candor and then resolved with *he is to Wallis what Osho is to Tolle* and a graceful move toward Hareesh. The grief of having been disappointed by a teacher whose work one had loved is metabolized in under three pages. In *These Wilds Beyond Our Fences* Akomolafe writes about the slow griefs of the fatherhood he was not prepared for — grief that does not get a thesis. The Schrei grief gets a thesis. A thesis is a fast grief.

The retreat that *broke her* (Ch00a, one sentence, passed over). The husband who does not know exactly what happened in 2019 because she could not find the vocabulary (Ch00a, given one sentence, then absorbed into the naming-takes-seven-years arc). *Mostly no one has come* to the platform (Ch10d). The teacher's refusal of the retrieval tool (Ch11, named as *unresolved* and then resolved by a tarot reading).

Each is a crack. Each is sealed. The book is fast at the places it is most afraid.

The slowness the book needs is in Ch10d's *mostly no one has come* and in Ch11's *he declined.* Those two admissions, given the space of chapters rather than paragraphs, would be the book. The rest could be shorter.

---

## 5. Implication — does the book let itself be the pattern?

Ch11 has the strongest attempt. *The writing of this book is inside it. The retrieval tool is inside it. The platform is inside it.* The author names the tension. She says: *do not resolve it prematurely.* Then, three pages later, she draws tarot cards from the platform she built, has the AI grounded in Wallis's corpus interpret the cards, and receives the reading as *release the outcome before you send.*

The resolution is not premature. It is mediated through the exact apparatus the book has just said it cannot stand outside of. The apparatus produces a reading that tells her the work is complete. The book accepts the reading. The tension the book said it would not resolve has been resolved by the tool the book said could not resolve it.

This is the most sophisticated version of the solution-grammar the manuscript contains. It is also the clearest instance of the book refusing its own implication by routing through a tool whose voice it has trained. Akomolafe would call this *the trickster tool answering for the trickster question.* It is not dishonest. It is just not yet fugitive.

A fugitive version: draw the cards. Do not publish the reading. Let the reader not know what the cards said. Send the book anyway. Or: publish the reading with the mediation named as the problem, not the solution.

---

## 6. The post-activist sentences the book has already written

Some paragraphs are genuinely interrupted. I name them.

- *Mostly no one has come.* (Ch10d). The whole book could be written from that sentence.
- *I did not ask to exist. I do not know why I am required to continue.* (Ch00a). The *neither did I* that follows is not a consolation and the book almost lets it stay uncomforting.
- *The burn marks from the serpent did not go away in the dream, and they did not quite go away in the life either.* (Ch00b). This is the one dream-sentence the book does not try to interpret into usefulness.
- *He declined.* (Ch11, of Wallis on the retrieval tool). Two words. The book should stay there longer.

These are the fugitive lines. A revision that honored them would shorten much else.

---

## Closing

The revision plan is honest. The Popperian re-key is a real discipline. The *what has served* frame is better than the *what is true* frame. The AI-spending interlude is the right instinct.

But the plan is still, at its bones, a plan *to improve the book*. The fugitive question is whether the book wants to be improved, or whether the book wants, in places, to fall apart. The cracks I have named are places the book could fall apart usefully. The plan proposes to seal them more carefully.

One paraphrased teaching from *These Wilds Beyond Our Fences* that sits under this whole audit: the times are urgent; let us slow down. A book written on AI in 2026 is structurally in the urgency. The only post-activist move available is to let the slowness be in the prose itself — in the refusal to resolve, in the grief that does not become thesis, in the Choctaw fire that refuses to be the third leg of a convergence argument, in the $25,000 that does not become a footnote to Lembke.

*The cracks are not waiting to be closed. They are waiting to be entered.*
