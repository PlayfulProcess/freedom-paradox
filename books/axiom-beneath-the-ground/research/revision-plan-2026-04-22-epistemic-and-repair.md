# Revision Plan — Epistemic Discipline + Repair + AI-Spending Interlude
*Draft 2026-04-22 · to be audited by /critic, /bayo, /popper, /kant before execution*

## Context

The critic audit (2026-04-21) surfaced a structural contradiction: the book's content argues orthogonality (intelligence ≠ alignment on value-laden claims), while the book's method occasionally uses *convergence of traditions* as evidence — which presupposes exactly what orthogonality denies. The author does not want to make **truth-claims** at all. She wants to stand with the numinous as **what has served**, not as what is true.

This plan turns that stance into three concrete moves, plus one new interlude the book has been avoiding.

---

## Move 1 — Epistemic re-key: from "true" to "what has served, so far, and is falsifiable"

**Principle.** The book will no longer claim that a tradition, a practice, or a convergence *tracks truth*. It will claim only two things:

1. **Pragmatic function** — "this has served, so far, in lives I can name" (falsifiable against outcomes).
2. **Openness to revision** — "if it stops serving, or if a specific outcome is shown not to follow, the claim drops."

This is Popperian demarcation + Jamesian pragmatism + Kantian humility about noumena, held together. It is not relativism. It is refusing the specific claim the orthogonality thesis forecloses.

**Scope.** Audit the whole manuscript for sentences of the form:
- *"X is real / true / the deepest pattern."*
- *"Three traditions converge on X, therefore X."*
- *"Intelligence / enlightenment / insight reveals that X."*

Replace with:
- *"X has served, for the people I am describing, under conditions I can specify."*
- *"Three traditions have, independently, found it useful to name X. I do not know why. I notice that I have too."*
- *"I cannot claim to know whether X is real. I can report that living as though it were has produced outcomes I can describe."*

**The Lilac Dream footnote.** Add one footnote (Ch00b, first Lilac passage) that names the epistemic frame explicitly — something like: *"I report dreams the way a naturalist reports a sighting. Colors, sequence, what was in the body on waking. The meaning I have been allowed to find in them over years is a separate report, and falsifiable: if the frame stops helping me act well, I drop it."*

## Move 2 — Ch11 reframe: desert-pragmatism + seasons-of-forgiveness

**Dario passage.** Reframe from *"I endorse him because I converge with him"* to:

> Given the moral desert of 2026, his is among the few paths I can name that admits both the capability problem and the alignment problem and keeps working inside the constraint. I do not claim he is right. I claim the alternatives I have seen are worse under current scarcity. If the scarcity changes, the endorsement drops.

**Schrei passage.** Reframe from *"I sorted him"* to (using author's own language from the message):

> I stepped back because I could not sustain the relation at the level he was asking for, in the season I was in. The content I received from his work was real. The failure was not his; it was mine, or it was ours, or it was nobody's — seasons end and begin again. I suspect I will come back. Forgiveness is not a verdict about who was right. It is the willingness to let the relation resume when the season turns.

**Thin-alignment admission.** Add a short passage (≈250 words) in Ch11 that names the move: the book is *not* making convergence-claims. It is making seasons-under-scarcity claims. Different logical structure. Survives orthogonality intact.

**MFT weave — correction.** Silva (610) and Emily (611) are fictional teaching vignettes, not the author's clinical work or her relationships. They are **not usable** as case material in the book — that would be authority-smuggling of the kind the prior critic audit flagged.

What **is** usable is the *framework-training* she has internalized. Citation is frame-attribution only, one-sentence, MSW-style — never a textbook paragraph, never a fictional case.

Usable frames (from `transcripts/MFT/`):
- **Emotionally Focused Therapy** (Sue Johnson) — the rupture-withdraw-pursue-repair cycle. This is the clinical underlayer of the "seasons" reframe. One attribution sentence: *"The clinical literature I was trained in calls this cycle rupture-and-repair; the season-frame is its everyday name."*
- **Gottman's Four Horsemen** (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) — the specific markers that distinguish repairable from unrepairable patterns. Useful for quietly claiming her parting from Schrei was **not** one of the four; it was a season-limit, not a pattern-break. Single attribution, no expansion.
- **Imago dialogue** (Harville Hendrix — mirror, validate, empathize) — the shape repair takes when both parties consent to it. Can be named as what she would bring if the season turns.
- **Bowen differentiation** — the frame beneath her own sentence *"I stepped back because I could not sustain the relation at the level he was asking for, in the season I was in."* That sentence is already Bowenian; the MFT cite just names what she is doing.

**Skip for this pass**: IPV literature (asks for a wrong comparison), Silva formulation, Emily case, any third-party case material. Her own reflective essays and the 612 Final Role-Playing script are hers but in academic register; they would need full translation to serve the book and that is out of scope now.

**Weave rule**: frame-attribution only. One sentence per frame. No case material. No textbook register. The MFT training is cited as *what has served the author's capacity to see*, not as authority over the reader.

## Move 3 — New interlude: the AI-spending shadow (≈600–900 words)

**Placement.** Either late Ch10 (after "Constructive Half") or early Ch11 (before the pragmatism passage). To be tested with agents.

**Content sketch.**

The author has built this manuscript on a monthly plan of $20, raised to $100, one month of $200, back to $100 — checkable in commit history. That is the *bounded* usage the book's axioms endorse: action-anchored, transparent to a household, falsifiable against outcomes.

A person she knows spent $25,000 in a couple of months on AI-assisted work, his wife unaware of the amount, the work of uncertain payoff. He reported that Claude Code "changed his life." She believes him. She does not know what to do with that report.

The interlude sits with the question without resolving it. It names:
- The capability-without-alignment pattern reproducing at the **household scale** — the tool delivering capability to the user while the user's relational and financial constraints stay orthogonal to the capability.
- That the book is complicit — she is writing *on* the tool; she cannot claim exteriority.
- That the axiom *knowledge without action is a burden* must extend to *capability without household consent is a harm* — the same structure, one level down.
- That she does not know whether her own bounded usage will remain bounded. She can only report that it has, so far, and that her spouse knows the number.

**No verdict.** No claim about the other person. No prescription. The interlude is a *receipt*, not an argument. It is the book catching itself in the pattern it is naming.

**Dopamine Nation weave.** Anna Lembke, *Dopamine Nation* (2021), is the analytical frame — but used lightly, one paragraph, not a literature-review. The key points to carry:
- The dopamine-loop is driven by **variable-reward schedules**, not by ad-funding. AI tools have variable reward by construction (the next response might be brilliant or mediocre, and the uncertainty is itself reinforcing).
- The loop operates regardless of whether the product is sold B2B, B2C, or by subscription.
- Therefore the fact that AI coding tools are mostly B2B/subscription (better than social media's ad-funded B2C) is a **partial** structural relief, not a full one.

**The monetization sub-argument** (≈200 words inside the interlude).
- The only structurally different thing about AI coding tools, compared to social media, is the **enterprise-B2B tier** — where an organization pays for measurable productivity and the individual user is not the one being monetized. That tier differs genuinely from social-media extraction logic.
- That tier does not reach the author, or her friend, or most individual developers. They are on the **consumer tier** ($20 / $100 / $200 / metered API).
- On the consumer tier, every incentive social media had is reproduced: metered billing scales revenue with usage; tier-upgrades capture heavy users; engagement-loops and variable-reward dopamine cycling drive retention; and at some providers (OpenAI) advertising is already arriving.
- The author's own $20 → $100 → $200 → $100 path and the $25k case are both the consumer-tier mechanism working as designed.
- The honest claim: the business model is **not** the safeguard. The structural difference exists only at the enterprise tier; at the consumer tier, the social-media incentive pattern is substantially reproduced.

**Falsifiability clause.** If her own usage crosses a threshold she has not named aloud to her household, the interlude becomes the indictment of the book. That is the frame.

3. **/popper** (new) — which specific sentences in the manuscript remain non-falsifiable as written? Kuhn overlay: where is the book performing a paradigm-shift move (unfalsifiable by design) vs. a normal-science move (falsifiable against outcomes)?
4. **/kant** (new) — phenomena vs. noumena discipline. Where does the book still smuggle noumenal claims ("what is real," "the deepest pattern") into phenomenal register? Where is the categorical imperative actually at stake (the AI-spending interlude is a test case)?

Each agent produces a short audit (≤1500 words) in `research/`. Synthesis: a single revision diff applied to v5, with a line-by-line log of what changed and why.

## Verification

A reader who has not seen this plan should be able to, after the revision:

1. Find zero sentences of the form *"three traditions converge on X, therefore X is real."*
2. Find the Schrei passage and not read it as a verdict.
3. Find the Dario passage and not read it as endorsement of his claims, only of the constraint under which she acts.
4. Find the AI-spending interlude and not read it as either confession or accusation — read it as a receipt.
5. Find, in the footnotes, an explicit statement of the book's epistemic frame (Popperian + Jamesian + Kantian, without jargon).

If any reader reports the book still makes truth-claims, the frame has failed and the pass is revised.
