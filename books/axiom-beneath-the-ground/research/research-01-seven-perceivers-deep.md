## Key Findings for the Book

### 1. The void IS a recognized stage — and explicitly a trap

The seven perceivers framework places the void precisely:
- **Pralayākala** (level 2): nothingness without awareness — the nihilistic dead-end
- **Vijñānākala** (level 3): conscious void, passive witness — "stuck in the transcendent"

Wallis: "To some people, it feels like terrifying nothingness, and they pull back immediately; to others, it feels like blessed peace, and they love immersing in it, even to the point of escapism."

**This IS the existential crisis the letter describes.** The tradition recognizes it, maps it, and treats it as a stage to be traversed — not a destination.

### 2. THE VIEW CONSTRAINS THE REALIZATION

Kṣemarāja (Sūtra 8): "whatever level of reality you believe to be ultimate determines the final limit of your awakening."

This is the axiom argument FROM INSIDE THE TRADITION. The View you hold determines how far you can go. Materialists get stuck at the body. Buddhists who venerate the void get stuck in the void. Even Vedāntins who touch the Core fail if they deny dynamism.

**If your View says "the ground is chosen, not given," that View constrains your realization to the void.** If your View says "the void is pregnant with possibility," you can proceed.

The tradition is saying: the axiom matters. Choose the right one.

### 3. The full void (pūrṇa-śūnya) is the answer

Not groundlessness but luminous ground. Kṣemarāja: "absolutely full, absolutely empty, both, and neither vibrating in absolute simultaneity."

Wallis: "You experience this radical void... absolutely empty of all qualities. But then you're supposed to go beyond that, to the ultimate realization, which is that same void, but now realized as pregnant with infinite possibility."

### 4. The complete practitioner ascends AND descends

"The one who rises and cannot descend is incomplete." Both transcendence and immanence required. Maps onto Tillich's courage to be as a part AND as oneself.

### 5. Grace as the exception to the axiom constraint

Śaktipāta (grace) is "the only exception" to the rule that your philosophical view limits your progress. Grace "can reveal a deeper reality even to one who doesn't believe in its possibility."

**For the book:** This is the tradition's answer to the circularity. You cannot reason your way out of the void. But grace — which arrives unbidden, through the teacher, through practice, through the co-regulatory field — can bypass the constraint your View imposes. The axiom constrains. Grace transcends the constraint.

### 6. Seven levels mapped

1. **Sakala** — ordinary embodied. All 3 malas. Can't believe other reality exists.
2. **Pralayākala** — dissolved void. 2 malas. Nihilistic dead-end.
3. **Vijñānākala** — conscious void, passive. 1 mala. Stuck in transcendent.
4. **Mantra** — wisdom-perceiver. 0 malas. Dynamic but unstable.
5. **Mantreśvara** — "I am this universe." More stable.
6. **Mantra-maheśvara** — Irreversible. "I am this universe" with subjectivity predominant.
7. **Śiva** — absolute nondual. Pure "I."
