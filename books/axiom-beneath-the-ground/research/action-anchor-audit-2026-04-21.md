# Action-Anchor Audit — Axiom v5 (Ch10a–Ch10f)

*Generated 2026-04-21. Applies the book's own principle — "knowledge without action is a burden" — to its claim-heavy chapters. Each major claim gets: (1) the claim summarized, (2) the action that anchors it, or (3) a flag if no anchor exists. The author decides per flag: do the action, cut the claim, or add an anchor already extant.*

**Verdict legend:**
- ✅ **Anchored** — action exists in the author's life or in a commit. Book references it (or could).
- 🔶 **Partial anchor** — action exists but is weaker than the claim, or is an absence-of-action (e.g. refusal to do something).
- ❌ **Unanchored** — no action points at this. Flagged for decision.
- 📖 **Reader-invitation anchor** — the claim points at an action the reader can take; counts as anchored if the invitation is concrete.
