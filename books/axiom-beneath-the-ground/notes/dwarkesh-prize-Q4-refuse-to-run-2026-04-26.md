## DRAFT — "The race is the trap. What India and Nigeria should do instead." (~1,650w; cut to 1,000)

### The argument in four steps

**One. The "frontier" is a story, not a place.**
- Amodei's *Machines of Loving Grace* + Aschenbrenner's *Situational Awareness* + Altman's "no third option" build a finish-line narrative
- Stanford AI Index: China leads publications/patents but trails frontier capability
- Toner's "missile gap" critique (*Foreign Affairs* 2023)
- Jeffrey Ding (RAND, Sept 2025): "diffusion marathon," not sprint to invent
- Catch-up frame loads the dice — debt-financed Stargate copies vs. asking where productive intelligence lives in the economy

**Two. Sovereignty over the same artifact is not sovereignty.**
- Karen Hao's *Empire of AI*: East India Company patterning (ideology + extraction + labor exploit + manichaean rivalry)
- Sabelo Mhlambi: ubuntu / rationality-as-personhood critique
- Yuk Hui: cosmotechnics / monotechnology problem
- Indigenous Protocol & AI Position Paper (Lewis et al.): AI as kin, not property
- Krutrim 12B losing to GPT-4o while AI4Bharat IndicTrans2 already beats it on Indic translation

**Three. The race framing is what makes export-control whipsaw possible.**
- Biden 3-tier Diffusion Rule (capping India/Nigeria as Tier 2)
- Trump pay-to-play: H200 to China at 25% govt cut (Dec 2025)
- India improved access via $500B trade pledge
- UAE Stargate via aligning with US foreign policy + divesting Huawei
- Andreotti's "single story of forward"; Akomolafe's "the times are urgent — let us slow down"

**Four. The leverage that actually exists isn't where the strategy documents are looking.**
- Three Global South assets: large coherent populations, regulatory authority, moral standing
- UN Scientific Panel + Global Dialogue on AI Governance (launched Sept 2025)
- AU Continental AI Strategy July 2024; Bharat Mandapam Feb 2026 (92 countries)

### The Modi/Tinubu strategy (2026–2030, five concrete moves)

**Q4 2026: Global South AI Compact** — India + Nigeria + Brazil + Indonesia + South Africa + Kenya + Egypt
- (a) Reciprocal data-residency interoperability (UPI-PIX-Aadhaar pattern)
- (b) Coordinated market access regime (>10M users requires shared bloc eval suite)
- (c) Shared 50,000-GPU consortium across IndiaAI + Brazil supercomputer + South African MainOne

**2027:** Redirect domestic spend from frontier-imitation to application layer (agriculture, public health, judicial backlog, climate adaptation in Indic and African languages)

**2028:** Bloc regulatory authority requires traceable provenance + consent for training data sourced from bloc populations; revenue royalty paid to bloc-administered fund (Couldry/Mejias data-colonialism critique → balance-of-payments instrument)

**Throughout:** Refuse AISI miniaturization. Build Global South AI Evaluation Commons (200 staff, $500M over 5 years) co-hosted by IIT Madras + Strathmore CIPIT + AIMS + Tec de Monterrey

**Akomolafe move:** Decline urgency. Five-year not five-month strategy. Aschenbrenner's revenue/timeline predictions already missing by half.

### 3. For/against broader inclusion

**For inclusion:** Legitimacy, distributed benefits, sovereignty, counters US-China duopoly, addresses imperial methodology critique. Toner + Ding + Sheehan all argue race framing empirically/morally weak.

**Against (taken seriously):** Proliferation risk; weakens functional safety mechanisms; Aschenbrenner-style coordinated-democratic-bloc argument; Hendrycks/Schmidt/Wang nonproliferation paper.

**Honest synthesis:** "For caution" mostly fails empirically. Export controls leaking. AISI network shrinking. Frontier safety commitments unevenly honored. Strongest "against" is bioweapon proliferation — addressable through targeted controls, not general gatekeeping.
