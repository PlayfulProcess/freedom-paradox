# Axiom — Multi-Narrator Plan v3 (Shadow Passengers + Self-Observation)
*Date: 2026-04-24*
*Status: Plan only. Extends v2 (`axiom-multi-narrator-plan-v2-pedagogical-2026-04-24.md`). v1 and v2 remain on disk for version continuity. Adds the deeper move the author named after reading v2.*

---

## What v3 adds to v2

V2 proposed multi-voice as a pedagogy of register-noticing. The passengers were *competence registers* — the equity analyst, the mother, the MSW, the dreamer, the Brazilian, each a real region of the author's competence.

V3 adds a second structural category: **shadow passengers.** These are not competence registers. These are *patterns the author is trying to see and work with* — the movements of self that modernity, or the author's own conditioning, trains into her. They are the snakes in the lilac silks of Ch00b. They are what the tradition names as the things recognition does not remove.

And v3 reframes the third-person-close narrator "she." In v2, "she" was the default voice. In v3, "she" is specifically **the author's practice of self-observation** — the passenger whose job is to watch the other passengers and catch the shadows before they drive, sometimes succeeding, sometimes catching belatedly, sometimes failing and only noticing the failure in the next chapter. "She" is not a performance of integration. "She" is a verb — the act of watching. When the watching works, the shadow is caught. When the watching fails, the shadow speaks unwatched. The book is honest about both.

This turns the book from *a memoir of integration* into *a documented practice of observation.* Which is closer to what meditation, therapy, and contemplative journaling actually are.

## I. The shadow passengers

The author has named four explicitly. There may be more. These are the starting four. Each has a register, a characteristic move, a near enemy, and a specific kind of wiping-act the author's committed-to-examined-life self uses on it.

### 1. The Aggrandizer

*Wants:* to be seen, praised, noticed as having done a thing well.
*Speaks in:* declarative syntax that monumentalizes events. Takes credit. Gives an act a title-case category name. *"This is the Fourth Political Action."* *"I am not afraid to be called a star."*
*Activated by:* triumph moments. A successful speech. A chapter that lands. A teacher who agrees. A platform that attracts praise.
*Near enemy:* the confident practitioner. The confident practitioner reports what happened without monumentalizing it. The Aggrandizer dresses as the confident practitioner and adds the canonization.
*The book's immune response:* the Ch00b lilac-silks dream. The dance is available; the *how cool I am* thought turns the silks into the serpent; the imprints last. Every chapter that narrates an author-done-something-well must host the dream's presence.
*The wiping-act:* the author sometimes notices the Aggrandizer speaking and rewrites before committing. Sometimes she notices too late and wipes the thought from conscious circulation without acknowledging that it ran. V3's protocol: *when the wiping has happened, show the wiping.* The honest chapter says *I said X. I noticed later that X was the Aggrandizer's sentence. I am leaving it in and naming it rather than removing it, because the practice is the naming, not the deletion.*

### 2. The Fearful Validation-Seeker

*Wants:* approval. Either (a) from a moral code — *am I being good enough about this?* — or (b) from a respected figure — *does Wallis approve? does Tatiana approve? does Dario approve?*
*Speaks in:* conditional syntax, deference-signaling, appeals to authority, softening language. *"I would be glad to,"* *"if you would like,"* *"only if you think."* Or: reflexive citation of authorities whose approval the sentence is seeking.
*Activated by:* writing a letter to a teacher. Making a choice that could be wrong. Being in a room of more-experienced practitioners. The risk of being called arrogant.
*Near enemy:* the honest student. The honest student defers where deference is earned. The Fearful Validation-Seeker defers where deference is not earned, to protect the self from being wrong alone.
*The book's immune response:* Ch10a's four-orthogonalities argument is specifically the author's own counter to her Fearful Validation-Seeker — the argument that no teacher's approval integrates what the student has not integrated herself, that moral-code and teacher-figure are both insufficient.
*The wiping-act:* the author softens a letter, sends it, then wonders if the softening was honesty or fear. V3's protocol: the book names the ambiguity. *The letter was tightened. I am not sure whether the tightening was honesty or fear. I am telling you both possibilities are live.*

### 3. The Best-Self Performer

*Wants:* to be the integrated whole. The good parent AND the sharp analyst AND the contemplative AND the activist AND the writer AND the builder. To hold all four axes of Ch10a's orthogonality argument in one voice, smoothly, without friction.
*Speaks in:* synthesis syntax. The third-person-close narrator's effortless synthesis IS this passenger when unwatched. *She* who parents and analyzes and meditates and ships code and writes the book. The warmth of authorial competence carrying everything at once.
*Activated by:* writing a chapter. Editing a paragraph. The temptation to make the whole book sound like one person's resolved inquiry.
*Near enemy:* the practitioner doing integration work. Integration work is slow, partial, axis-specific, and visibly unfinished. The Best-Self Performer produces the appearance of integration without the work.
*The book's immune response:* Ch10a's orthogonality thesis is specifically against the Best-Self Performer as a folk theory. The book's own chapter diagnoses the book's own form.
*The wiping-act:* the author reads her chapter, notices the warmth is performed, and either rewrites OR convinces herself the warmth is earned. V3's protocol: every chapter with sustained authorial warmth gets checked. The check is *did the chapter earn this warmth, or did the Best-Self Performer supply it?* If the second, the warmth is replaced with something cooler — the equity analyst or the MSW or the dreamer speaking, each with their own temperature, refusing the smoothing.

### 4. The Comfort-Seeker

*Wants:* to live the most comfortable life regardless of harms upstream. Not consciously. Structurally. The patterns of rationalization that keep the author from acting on knowledge she has, from shipping the book, from dropping the easy conversation for the hard one.
*Speaks in:* deferral syntax. *Maybe later.* *Not today.* *When I have more clarity.* *I need to think about this more.* Soft language that protects the status quo while appearing to keep the question open.
*Activated by:* knowledge without action. The moment an action becomes available and the Comfort-Seeker begins rationalizing why now is not the moment.
*Near enemy:* the careful practitioner. The careful practitioner sometimes does not act yet because the action is not yet ready. The Comfort-Seeker sometimes does not act because the action would be uncomfortable, and calls it carefulness.
*The book's immune response:* the *knowledge without action is a burden* principle, stated explicitly in CLAUDE.md and in the Author's Note. Every claim must point at an action. Action anchors are verifiable. If the chapter makes a claim and no action follows, the Comfort-Seeker has been driving.
*The wiping-act:* the author carries a claim without acting on it for a long time and then, when challenged, produces the action or removes the claim. V3's protocol: chapters are audited for claims without actions. The book's standing action anchors (OpenAI removal, code-private, the two-minutes speech, the platform, the poem grammar) are the receipts that specific Comfort-Seeker rationalizations were overridden. Where no action exists, either the claim comes out or the action must be done.

### Possible fifth: The Translator

*Wants:* to make foreign material available in English to English-reading audiences. To render the Pratyabhijñā lineage, the Brazilian 4th-Ray tradition, the Afro-Brazilian possession lineages, each in English, for readers who do not read the original languages.
*Near enemy:* genuine cross-cultural engagement. The Translator is doing a real thing; the shadow version is when the translation erases the cost of translation, when the English rendering pretends to be the tradition rather than a translation of the tradition.
*Bayo-agent's audit* named this directly: the book has not yet named the four layers of translation cost. The Translator is the passenger doing the translating. The shadow is in the un-named cost.
*The wiping-act:* the author translates and moves on, without the cost-naming sentence. V3's protocol: at the Author's Note, the cost is named. At each translation moment in the book (every Sanskrit term, every Portuguese term, every borrowed framing), a brief signal that this is translation, not possession.

### Open: are there others?

The author should consider whether these four-and-a-half capture the shadow landscape of the book or whether more passengers want naming. Candidates the editor would watch for: the Rescuer (wants to save), the Critic (wants to be right by being against), the Mystic (wants the experience to mean more than it meant).

Three to five shadow passengers is the range the reader can hold. If the book names seven, the reader loses track. If it names only two, the texture is thin.

## II. The "she" character, reframed

V2 described the third-person-close narrator as a register of reflection. V3 is sharper:

**"She" is the author's practice of self-observation made visible.**

"She" is not a character who has arrived. "She" is the specific act of watching — watching the equity analyst when the analyst begins to credential, watching the mother when the mother begins to self-congratulate, watching the Brazilian when the Brazilian begins to essentialize, watching the Aggrandizer when the Aggrandizer speaks. "She" succeeds sometimes. "She" fails sometimes. "She" is the discipline, not the achievement.

This reframing has three practical consequences for the book:

1. **"She" is allowed to catch the shadows belatedly.** The book can say *she said X. She did not notice, writing X, that the Aggrandizer had been speaking. She noticed later. She is telling you now.* That is more honest than either pretending she caught it in time or pretending the Aggrandizer wasn't there.

2. **"She" is allowed to fail.** The book can host passages where "she" speaks in the Best-Self Performer's synthesis register and *the book does not correct it,* because the failure is part of the practice. The reader, trained in register-noticing, will see the Best-Self Performer driving. The text does not need to hand-hold the reader there.

3. **"She" is allowed to wipe.** The wiping-from-consciousness act the author named explicitly — "*maybe she does self-aggrandize and then she tries to wipe out from her conscious*" — is itself a move the book can show. The wiping is a move of the examined life that succeeds partially and fails partially. When the wiping has happened and then been noticed-after-the-fact, the book says so.

## III. The audit protocol — going through the existing book

The book currently has ~13K words across ~20 chapters. A shadow-passenger audit pass asks, chapter by chapter, four questions:

**Q1. What self-images are lasting (uninterrogated) in this chapter?**
Self-images are sentences that position the author as a certain kind of person without examining the position. Examples: *she had spent a year preparing* (positions her as prepared); *she was not afraid to be labeled arrogant* (positions her as someone who is not afraid of that). Each self-image gets a pass: is it earned? Is it being examined? If neither, it comes out or gets a self-watching paragraph added.

**Q2. Where are the self-aggrandizing statements?**
Explicit: any title-case categorization of the author's own act (*the Fourth Political Action*). Monumentalization (a chapter-length theory of one minute of speech). Charitable interpretations of others' behavior as responses to the author's actions (the Harris podcast as evidence of her impact). These are removed or revised to report-without-monumentalizing.

**Q3. Where is the Best-Self Performer speaking as the narrator?**
Passages where the third-person-close narrator smoothly holds all four axes — analytic and parental and contemplative and activist — in one synthesis. Each passage gets checked: does the synthesis reflect actual integration, or is it the Performer supplying warmth? If the second, the synthesis is broken up — a passenger handoff, or a visible seam, or a question the narrator doesn't answer.

**Q4. Where should the wiping-act itself be visible?**
Places where, on honest reading, the author *did* aggrandize, *did* seek validation, *did* perform integration, and then later caught it or wiped it. These should not be silently removed. The honest move is to make the wiping visible: *I said this. I saw later what I was doing. I am leaving the sentence in and naming what was happening. The book is the practice of that noticing.*

### Proposed audit outputs

For each chapter, a brief audit note:
- Self-images found: [list]
- Self-aggrandizing statements: [list]
- Best-Self Performer passages: [list]
- Wiping-visible opportunities: [list]
- Proposed revisions: [remove / add self-watching / replace with passenger handoff / show the wiping]

Pilot chapters for the audit: Ch10h (already identified), Ch12 alt-ending (the most recently written), Ch13 (the vow chapter — highest risk of Best-Self Performer warmth), Ch00b (the dream chapter — the source material for the Aggrandizer's near enemy; audit for consistency).

## V. The reader's practice, refined

V2 said the book trains the reader in register-noticing. V3 is sharper: the book trains the reader in **watching their own shadow passengers.**

This is what contemplative traditions actually teach. Not a taxonomy of the self. Not a personality inventory. The specific practice of noticing, in real time, when a pattern is driving and the observer has stopped watching. The book models this practice by performing it on the page — sometimes successfully, sometimes failing, sometimes catching the failure later.

The reader is not told to police themselves. The reader is shown, by watching "she" watch her own passengers, what the practice looks like. What the reader does with that modeling is their own.

## VI. What this move protects against

Three specific risks, each addressed:

1. **Self-help book trap.** A book that names shadow passengers can become a checklist — here are the four kinds of self-deception, watch for them. This plan prevents that by NOT listing them in a didactic section but by letting them speak in the text and be caught (or missed) in the text. The reader learns by watching, not by studying a framework.

2. **Confession-as-performance.** A book that confesses the author's shadows can perform confession as its own credentialing. *Look how honest I am about my self-aggrandizement.* This plan prevents that by using the wiping-visible move — the author is not allowed to name a shadow without ALSO naming that the naming itself might be another passenger's move.

3. **Infinite regress of self-observation.** A book that notices every move and then notices the noticing can spiral into paralysis. This plan prevents that by the book's own form: the practice is done, the chapters are written, the actions are taken (OpenAI removal, code-private, the speech), the book ships. The watching does not stop but the life does not stop either. Practice, not perfection.

## VII. The one-sentence version

*Shadow passengers are patterns the self is trying to see and work with; the third-person "she" is the practice of that seeing made visible; the book is honest about the practice succeeding and failing; the wiping-from-consciousness act is itself a move the book shows rather than hides; and the reader, watching this, is being trained not in a taxonomy but in a discipline — the specific, unfinished, lifelong discipline of noticing who is driving.*

That is the plan.
