# Axiom — Structural Plan (Option B: Diary-as-Chapter-Opening-Primary-Source)
*Date: 2026-04-24*
*Status: Plan for a multi-session follow-up. Not executed. Supersedes the flavor-bites mapping document.*

## Premise

The author's retreat diary (handwritten, California 2026 Tantra Illuminated retreat, 29 pages, transcribed) is the book's actual primary source. The book's current third-person-close narration is a reflective register that has been speaking *for* the primary source without the primary source being visible. Option B makes the primary source visible at the head of each major chapter, and lets the narrator be what it has been all along: the voice that reflects on what the diary named.

This is the García Márquez move applied to this book: not adding ornaments, but letting the voice the book is in become clearly the voice the book is in, by exposing what the voice is responding to.

## Principle

**One diary excerpt per chapter, at the chapter's head, offset and italicized, unchanged from the notebook.** The narrator voice follows, responding, reflecting, carrying the reader through the chapter's theme. The chapter's thematic structure is preserved. What changes is that the chapter no longer generates from nowhere; it generates from a specific page of a specific notebook written at a specific retreat.

## Format details

**Visual treatment** — every diary opener appears in:
- Italic
- Offset (indented or set in a distinct register)
- Preceded by a small notation: *— Notebook, retreat day [N], California 2026 —* or similar, so the reader knows this is primary source, not narrator
- Followed by a blank line before the chapter's main text begins

**Register handoff** — the narrator's first sentence of each chapter should *not* directly comment on the diary opener. Let the diary stand. Let the reader make the connection. The narrator picks up the theme implicitly, not the quote explicitly.

**Voice integrity** — the diary is not edited for polish. Typos, idiosyncratic spelling, all-caps if that is how it was written. The only edits are: line breaks restored, [?] markers resolved by the author, contact info removed where present. The handwriting's rawness IS the voice the book is carving toward.

## What this preserves

- The current thematic chapter structure. No chapter dissolves or merges.
- The third-person-close narration. The *she* stays.
- Every argument the book is currently making.
- The tattva-ladder organization (Playground → Deha → Prāṇa → Citta → Shunya → Walking Out → returning chapters).
- All existing interludes (Ch01a Cracks, Ch04a Threshold, Ch06a Maps, Appendix A Zusya) — these are already in-register and do not need a diary opener.

## What this changes

- Every major chapter now visibly *responds* to a specific diary page rather than generating from the narrator alone.
- The book becomes, visibly, a book rooted in a specific week of specific practice with a specific teacher, rather than a reflective essay that happens to reference that week.
- The author's voice at its least-performed — the notebook — sets the agenda each time. The narrator's voice at its most-crafted carries the reflection. The two voices are now both the author, at two different registers, in visible dialogue.

## What this does NOT do

- Does not restructure the chapter order.
- Does not merge or dissolve chapters.
- Does not convert third-person to first-person.
- Does not add material that was not in the notebook.
- Does not claim the diary is the book. The diary is the primary source; the book is what responds to it.

## Risk assessment

**Low risks** — handled:
- Voice clash between diary and narrator — mitigated by visual offset (italic + indentation + attribution line).
- Reader disorientation — mitigated by consistency (every chapter does the same thing).
- Length overshoot — ~3-4K words added on a 13K base is well within range.

**Moderate risks** — flagged:
- Some diary openers may not land for every chapter. Author may want to swap or cut. Session structure accommodates this.
- The two-minutes-speech in Ch10d is long (~800 words if quoted in full). May warrant its own sub-section rather than chapter-opening interlude treatment. Author to decide.
- Q11 is proposed for both Ch08 and Ch10a; overlap may read badly. Replace one with a different excerpt if author concurs.

**High risks** — avoided by this option's scope:
- Chapter restructure: not done.
- Voice conversion: not done.
- Format change (two-column braid): not done.

These remain available for later if, after reading the B version, the author determines they are warranted. The plan for those would be Option C or D, not this.
