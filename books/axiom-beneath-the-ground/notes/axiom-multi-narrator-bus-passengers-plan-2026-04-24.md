# Axiom — Multi-Narrator Bus-Passengers Plan
*Date: 2026-04-24*
*Status: Plan only. To be audited by [T] and Bayo agents before any chapter is touched.*
*Supersedes the Option B "diary-as-chapter-opening" plan from earlier today; goes further than B without going as far as a full restructure.*

## Premise

Following Vanessa Andreotti's *bus of passengers* framing (from *Hospicing Modernity*, GTDF materials, and Andreotti's work with Bayo Akomolafe): the self is not a unified driver. The self is a bus carrying many passengers — ancestors, descendants, the child the adult was, the mother the child will become, the equity analyst, the MSW student, the dreamer, the Brazilian, the one who has not yet been named. Modernity has trained us to identify with a single passenger — the "driver" — and suppress the others. Decolonial practice is letting the other passengers speak in their own registers instead of having the driver translate them.

Applied to the book: the current third-person-close narrator ("she") is doing the work of multiple passengers at once, smoothing them into one consistent reflective register. That smoothing is the book's quiet version of the very moralism the book is trying to name — the *having-arrived-at-the-answer* voice that protects both the reader and the author from the cost of entering the inquiry with all its registers at once.

The Bayo-register diagnosis: *the book has been walled where it needed to be membraned.* The current single-narrator voice is the wall. Multi-narrator / bus-passengers is the membrane — governed permeability, each passenger's raw register let through at the moment the chapter needs it, while the reflective narrator gives companionship. The immune-system move Ch10d names theoretically; the book's form not yet enacting.

## The bus's passengers

These are the passengers identifiable in the current book, each with a distinct register and at least one existing passage where the register is already present (even if submerged):

1. **Third-person-close "she"** — the reflective narrator. Current default. Voice of synthesis and argument. Carries most of the book. *Marker:* roman type, third person, measured syntax.

2. **Diary "I"** — the raw, handwritten, in-the-moment, asking voice. Primary source. Currently absent from the book's committed text except as narrated remembrance. *Marker:* italic, first person, sometimes all-caps or fragmentary, attributed: *Notebook, retreat day N, California 2026.*

3. **Mother "I"** — the parenting voice. Currently present in Ch12 Composting (the daughter's song), glimpsed in Ch13 (the sandbox return). *Marker:* first person, present-tense scenes, specific sensory detail of domestic life.

4. **Author-to-reader "I"** — direct address. The voice of the Author's Note, Ch13 vow, occasional footnotes where the author steps out of narration to speak directly to the reader. *Marker:* first person, direct address, often begins *I want to say plainly...* or *I do not know...*

5. **Equity analyst "I" (or "she-as-analyst")** — the research-report register from the author's Goldman Sachs years. The voice that names numbers, checks footnotes, follows the money. Currently submerged inside the third-person-close narrator. Wants to come forward in the Pentagon/Anthropic material, the Ch10d receipts, the Fire Before Responsibility crossovers. *Marker:* analytical syntax, named sources, numeric precision, *who benefits from this narrative.*

6. **MSW student "I"** — the social-work register. Linehan, Bowen differentiation, attachment theory, Gottman. Currently submerged in the narrator. Wants to come forward in Ch10d's Linehan-change-theory material, Ch08 Citta Returning, Ch13 on *love as secure attachment.* *Marker:* clinical specificity, named frameworks, the Linehan filter explicit, therapeutic attunement to body.

7. **Brazilian-born "I"** — the Portuguese-thinking self, the Cildo-Meireles-formed self, the Brazilian-esoteric self (Trigueirinho / 4th Ray), the Afro-Brazilian-adjacent self. Currently partly present in Ch13's right-size meditation and in the alt-ending's *re-pair*. *Marker:* Portuguese terms preserved, Brazilian references without translation, direct invocation of Brazilian artists and thinkers in their own idiom.

8. **Dreamer "I"** — the voice of the Paradevi dream, the lilac silks, the burn marks. Currently present as interlude in Ch00b. *Marker:* italic, present-tense-in-the-dream, non-explanatory, the *data-not-meaning* posture from Ch00b.

9. **Ancestor voice / field voice** — the voice that spoke in the Ayahuasca ceremony (*Neither did I*). Currently attributed to *the voice,* not owned as a narrator. Could be a passenger, treated carefully, never claimed as the author's. *Marker:* italic, no first-person pronoun, rendered as reported speech from elsewhere.

10. **Future-descendant voice** — the voice of what the author will have become, or of the reader she will never meet, or of the daughter reading this at thirty. Currently absent. Andreotti's framing explicitly names this passenger. Could appear in Ch13 and at the end of each major section, as a voice addressing back from the future toward the present practitioner. *Marker:* second-person addressed to the current author, or a distinct italic register.

## The Bayo / Andreotti question the plan must answer

Not every passenger should become a visible narrator in every chapter. That would be bus-as-theatre, not bus-as-membrane.

The question the plan must honestly answer, for each chapter: *which passengers is this chapter for?* And the honest answer is always fewer than all of them. The skillful book is the one where the right passengers speak at the right moments and the wrong passengers stay quiet.

The craft rule, following Andreotti's own practice in *Burnout From Humans* and related work: **never more than three distinct narrator voices per chapter.** Usually two. Often one. The multiplicity lives at the *book* level; the chapter discipline is restraint.

## What this does NOT do

- Does not dissolve the third-person-close narrator. "She" remains the most-used voice. The book stays readable.
- Does not turn the book into experimental fiction. The passengers are named in the Author's Note frame; the reader knows what they are meeting.
- Does not require typographic innovation beyond italic and offset. No two-column layout. EPUB, KDP, and mdbook pipelines all handle this.
- Does not change chapter order or merge chapters.
- Does not invent material. Every passenger already exists in the book; the plan is to *let them be distinct* where they currently blend.

## Risk / reward

**Risks:**
- Reader fatigue if the register-shifts feel decorative. Mitigation: the three-passengers-per-chapter cap, plus the honest test *did this chapter need this passenger?*
- Preciousness. Mitigation: the diary is in ALL-CAPS because that is how she wrote it; the passengers' registers are real, not affected.
- Inconsistent execution across chapters. Mitigation: a written style guide for each passenger's register, reviewed after pilot chapters.

**Rewards:**
- Honors the actual multiplicity of the author's voice (Andreotti).
- Enacts the membrane-not-wall principle (Bayo).
- Carves toward the voice the diary is in without erasing the reflective voice.
- Enacts the book's anti-moralism argument in its form — no single performed voice.
- Opens the book to readers who would otherwise hear the third-person-close as distant.

## Author-frame move

The Author's Note should, at the start, name the bus. One paragraph. Something like: *The book you are about to read is narrated by several voices. Most of it is in the register of a reflective third-person narrator — a woman who is, and is not, me. Some of it is in the register of the handwritten notebook she carried to a retreat in April 2026; that voice is offset, italic, rendered as it was written, typos and all. Some of it is in the register of a mother describing her daughter. Some is in the register of the analyst she once was, still is in places. Some is in the register of the Brazilian self the narrator mostly translates but sometimes does not. The multiplicity is not a stylistic choice. It is an honest report on who is writing.*

This frame is the kula-invitation. The reader enters the book knowing who they will meet.
