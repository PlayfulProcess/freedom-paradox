# Axiom — Multi-Narrator Plan v3.1 (Unlimited Passengers — Correction to v3)
*Date: 2026-04-24*
*Status: Short refinement note. Corrects a structural error in v3. Does not replace v3; reframes it.*

---

## What v3 got wrong

V3 proposed a taxonomy: four shadow passengers (Aggrandizer, Fearful Validation-Seeker, Best-Self Performer, Comfort-Seeker), plus a possible fifth (Translator), plus the question-left-open of whether a few more might join the list.

The author corrected this. **The passengers are unlimited.** Paradevi is a passenger. All the gods the author has encountered are passengers. The forest voice from 2019 is a passenger. A thought-form that has no name yet is a passenger. The author's mother is a passenger. The author's grandmother is a passenger. The author's future granddaughter is a passenger. The grief that predates the author is a passenger.

The conscious self perceives a few of them at a time. That is the contraction. That is what a self *is* in the tradition's account — *aṇu*, the limited perceiver, a focused apperture onto a field that exceeds it.

**Self-images in the form of fixed personalities limit perception further.** When the book names a passenger — *the Aggrandizer* — and treats that name as a stable personality the reader can track, the name becomes a wall against the reader perceiving any other movement of self-performance that does not fit under it. The taxonomy becomes the new contraction, on top of the contraction the conscious self was already doing.

This is Pratyabhijñā-precise. Consciousness is unlimited. Any specific formation is a contraction. A catalog of contractions is itself a contraction. The book that catalogs the passengers, however usefully, has performed the very limitation the book is trying to make visible.

## What v3 got right, and what holds

V3's core moves still stand:

1. Multi-voice is a pedagogy of register-noticing.
2. The third-person "she" is the practice of self-observation made visible.
3. Passages can catch the noticing belatedly or fail to catch at all.
4. The wiping-act can be shown rather than hidden.
5. Integration of the [T] + Bayo audits.

What shifts: **the four-shadow-passenger taxonomy becomes offered-examples rather than a fixed list.** The book does not train the reader in *watching for the Aggrandizer.* The book trains the reader in *watching who is driving right now, knowing that whatever name you give the driver will, in the moment of being named, become its own limiting formation.*

## The correction, operationalized

Three changes to the plan.

### 1. The shadow passengers become examples, not inventory

V3's descriptions of the Aggrandizer, the Fearful Validation-Seeker, the Best-Self Performer, and the Comfort-Seeker remain in the plan as *cases the author has noticed in her own practice and is offering as examples of what noticing looks like.* The plan no longer claims they are exhaustive. The plan does not claim they are stable. The plan says: *these are passengers I have met. There are others. Some I have not met. Some will arrive tomorrow and they will have no name.*

The book's text follows the same discipline. When a shadow-pattern is named in a chapter, the naming is prefaced with *something that looked like* or *a movement the author has called, for now, X.* The name is acknowledged as provisional. The reader is not invited to learn four types. The reader is invited to watch their own.

### 2. The competence registers also become examples, not inventory

V2's eight competence registers (equity analyst, mother, MSW, dreamer, Brazilian, ancestor, future-descendant, third-person narrator) are similarly offered as *the passengers the author has most often found driving, in the period of writing this book.* They are the book's most-visited passengers. They are not the full bus. Every chapter where one of them speaks, there are others present and silent. The book names what it hosts without pretending the hosting is complete.

### 3. The divine / the field / the gods as passengers

The author's encounters with Paradevi, with the forest voice in 2019, with whatever is happening in ceremonial practice — these have a place in the bus. Not as metaphysical claim. As *the conscious self being, in specific moments, open to formations it did not generate,* which is the tradition's own account of what happens when a practitioner is genuinely available.

This does not commit the book to a cosmology. The book remains committed to the three-filter test (*useful, fits the data, compassionate*) and to the Linehan discipline of acting inside the not-knowing. What changes: the book does not pretend these passengers are not on the bus. When Paradevi spoke in the Ch00b dream, Paradevi is a passenger. When *neither did I* came through in Ayahuasca, that was a passenger. The book can host them without claiming them, and without dismissing them as reducible to psychology.

The test for a divine/field passenger in the book: the passenger is reported as speaking, with attribution *(the voice, the dream, the figure)*, not translated into an authorial claim. The reader is not told whether the passenger was inner or outer. The *refusing to decide* is the register's discipline.

## The practice, restated

The book is not teaching the reader four shadow-patterns to watch for.

The book is teaching the reader the specific discipline of *asking, in this moment, who is driving,* and holding the question open long enough for an answer to arrive that is not already on any list.

Any passenger — competence register, shadow pattern, goddess, ancestor, grief, desire, fear, the voice of a tree, a thought-form that has no name yet — can be driving. The conscious self only perceives a few at a time. The practice is to keep the attention available, to catch the driver when noticing arrives, and to release the naming back into the field when the naming has done its work.

The book shows this by doing it. Not by listing it.

## What this changes in the execution order

Step 1 of v3 (the Ch10h pilot revision) remains. The revisions to Ch10h happen. But the self-watching paragraph inserted in Ch10h no longer says *the Aggrandizer was speaking.* It says *a movement of self-performance was speaking — I will not name it stably because the naming, for me, tends to harden into a new self-image. I will note that the sentence "I am not afraid to be called a star" is itself a star-sentence. The dream in Ch00b warned me about this. I am leaving the sentence in and naming what I can name. What I cannot name I am leaving unsealed.*

This is less legible than v3's *the Aggrandizer.* It is also more honest. The reader is not given a taxonomy to adopt. The reader is given an example of the discipline, which they can do in their own idiom.

The audit protocol changes correspondingly. V3 asked *find the Aggrandizer's self-images.* V3.1 asks *find the self-images that last. Some of them will fit patterns I have seen before. Some of them will not. In either case, the work is the noticing, not the categorization.*

## The one-sentence version, revised

*The passengers are unlimited; the conscious self perceives a few; any stable naming of a passenger becomes its own contraction; the book trains the discipline of asking who is driving in this moment, without pretending the answer will fit a list the book has prepared.*

That is the plan, as corrected.
