## What the K.G.M. material is, structurally

Three claims, each worth different shelves:

1. **Empirical / legal:** §230 does not cover product-design claims; engagement-design is now a litigated theory of harm; bellwether returned $6M in March 2026.
2. **Structural:** the mechanisms named in the K.G.M. complaint (variable-reward, persistent personalization, push-style re-engagement, engagement-optimized recommendation) are the same mechanisms in consumer AI products. The legal theory is portable.
3. **Authorial / personal:** the author's own platform, the author's own choice to keep it small, the author's stated commitment to turn AI features off if a bellwether-grade ruling lands against AI companies.

Different books need different combinations of these.

## Book-by-book recommendation

### Fire Before Responsibility (THE main book) — **YES, weave centrally**

This is the book where the K.G.M. material does the most structural work. The book's thesis is *do before you know vs. know before you do.* K.G.M. is the legal system finishing the *do* with retroactive consequence — the social-media platforms were allowed to scale before anyone established what they were doing; the courts are now establishing it.

**Where it lands:**

- **Skeleton Ch9 ("The Trap of Solving") or its successor in skeleton-v3** — the chapter on responsibility infrastructure as the missing layer. K.G.M. is the negative case: what happens when responsibility infrastructure was absent for thirty years and the courts have to construct it ex post. The variable-reward / persistent-memory / engagement-optimization list is the *concrete content* of what responsibility infrastructure would have constrained.
- **The AI-as-fourth-fire chapter (whichever it becomes in v3)** — the parallel between fire-without-hearth and AI-without-accountability is sharpened by K.G.M. The third fire (industrial / fossil) is being litigated in climate court; the second fire (social media) is being litigated in K.G.M.; the fourth fire (AI) will be litigated in cases that are already filed (Raine et al.) and will reach bellwether stage on a 2027–2029 timeline. The book can name the trajectory.
- **Footnote landscape:** working footnote drafted in the research file; copy verbatim.

**What NOT to do:** do not turn the chapter into a legal brief. Two paragraphs on K.G.M. + one footnote is the right weight. The book is structural; the case is anchor not argument.

## Trigger conditions to revisit this plan

- Next bellwether (summer 2026) returns a verdict against an AI company specifically (not social media). At that point K.G.M. is no longer the door-opener case; it is the *first* case in a sequence, and the cross-book weaving may need to be larger.
- The first AI-specific case settles for material money (≥$10M). Same trigger.
- The plaintiffs' bar files a complaint specifically against a divinatory / spiritual AI tool. At that point Axiom Ch10 needs more than a footnote; it needs a paragraph.
- Federal regulation lands (KOSA, an FTC rule, or new federal AI-specific liability statute). At that point the material may shift from court-based to regulatory-based and the weaving changes shape.

## A note on tone

The K.G.M. material is *factual*. The temptation is to use it polemically — *the law is finally catching up; the bad actors will be punished.* The author's voice in these books is not polemical. The K.G.M. integration should sound like the rest of the books: *here is what happened, here is what it means, here is what the author is doing about it on her own platform.* The author's choice to keep her platform small is part of the argument; the legal verdict is the empirical anchor; the structural claim about engagement-design is the through-line. None of the three should be louder than the other two.
