## Principle

The retreat notebook is the book's primary source. The book has been operating from the author's memory of the retreat; these are the actual on-the-ground artifacts. Weaving them in *with care and economy* grounds the book in lived evidence without converting it into a diary.

Three formats proposed for each excerpt:
- **[INTERLUDE]** — italicized, offset from narrative, in the author's first-person voice. Like the Kali interlude in Ch4, the Cit silence after Ch6, the cracks interlude Ch01a.
- **[INCORPORATE]** — woven into narrator's third-person as *she wrote in the notebook that morning, "..."* or similar. Keeps the current voice intact, brings in a direct quote.
- **[FOOTNOTE]** — evidence the scholar-reader might want, out of body text.
- **[DON'T USE]** — in the notebook but not book material.

## What this does NOT do

- **Does not rewrite any chapter.** Each proposed insertion is a specific paragraph or block at a specific location. Chapter structure intact.
- **Does not change the book's voice.** Third-person narrative remains the default. Diary excerpts appear as interludes (italicized, offset) or as direct-quotation within the narrator's third-person.
- **Does not claim the diary is the book.** The book remains an argument; the diary is evidence for the argument, presented where it earns its place.
- **Does not violate privacy.** All diary excerpts proposed for book-use are the author's own words about her own experience. Nothing names other retreat participants. Contacts and specific names are excluded.
