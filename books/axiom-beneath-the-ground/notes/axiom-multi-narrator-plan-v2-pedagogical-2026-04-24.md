## I. Why this is not a literary flourish

Multiple fiction books split the self into flat personalities. *Inside Out* has Joy, Sadness, Fear, Disgust, Anger as cartoon operators. Genre fiction uses internal-voice choruses the way Greek drama used the chorus. Therapy-memoir splits the author into the Inner Critic, the Wounded Child, the Protector. Each of these moves reduces complexity into legibility. Each trades the full breadth of a self for characters a reader can meet and remember.

**This plan is explicitly not that move.** The passengers here are not characters. They are *registers* — the same consciousness writing in different inherited idioms, each with its own syntax, cadence, and evidentiary burden, each carrying full complexity. The equity analyst is not a cartoon of analytic thought; she is the author's mind actually working analytically, in the register Goldman Sachs trained, with all the instincts of that training including the instincts the training did not examine. The mother is not a persona; she is the author writing while actually watching a specific child, with the full attention motherhood makes available. Each passenger is a region of one author's competence, not a flattening of a trait.

The pedagogical claim is concrete: **by encountering the author's registers distinctly, the reader is trained to notice their own registers.** The book becomes a practice-object, not only an information-object. This is the same move Andreotti makes in *Hospicing Modernity*, which is written as a textbook and used in courses — the multi-voice structure is the teaching tool, not a decoration around a teaching. It is what the teaching *is.*

## II. Why this matters for *this* book, specifically

The book's argument is the four orthogonalities (Ch10a): spiritual, moral, intellectual, and relational axes do not integrate through a single passage. Recognition on one axis does not carry to the others. Each axis is its own work.

The current single-narrator voice performs a unified agent — *she who has integrated the axes.* Smoothly. From one register. In one synthesis.

That form contradicts the argument.

A book that argues *the axes are orthogonal and each requires its own work,* written in one voice that effortlessly holds all four, is enacting the very folk theory of convergence the book is trying to unlearn. The single voice is the subtle performance of alignment the book's own Ch10a names as the error.

Multi-voice fixes the structural contradiction. Each passenger speaks primarily for one axis or one position on an axis. The passengers do not always agree. The narrator does not translate them into a smooth synthesis. The form and the argument align.

The book enacts what it describes.

## III. Distinction from fiction's character-splitting — three tests

A skeptical reader will ask *how is this different from a novel with multiple narrators?* The three tests:

**1. Passengers are not personalities.** A fiction character has a consistent personality. A register has a consistent syntactic and evidentiary *grammar,* but not a personality — the same author deploys it, and the author's personality is one across all registers. The reader meeting the equity-analyst passenger is not meeting a separate person; she is meeting the author writing analytically. This distinction matters because the reader's own multiplicity is also not a cast of characters — it is the reader's own single consciousness in multiple registers. The book models this honestly.

**2. Passengers are not equally narrating characters.** In fiction, every POV character has narrative equality — each gets scenes, arcs, development. Here, the third-person-close narrator remains the most-used voice. Other passengers appear when they have something to say that the narrator cannot say. Some passengers appear in only two or three chapters across the whole book. Some passengers stay mostly silent, present by implication, speaking only once. The distribution is honest to how an actual mind works — some registers are more active than others, and some speak rarely but carry specific authority when they do.

**3. Passengers are allowed to remain unresolved.** In fiction, characters develop arcs toward integration or rupture. Here, passengers may stay in tension across the whole book, and the book does not resolve them. The analyst may never agree with the dreamer about what the Paradevi material *is.* The MSW voice may never agree with the author-to-reader voice about whether naming-the-wound is enough. The Brazilian may never agree with the third-person narrator about whether the book should be in English at all. These disagreements live in the book. They are not reconciled in a final chapter. *The reader is not told who won.* This is the book's honest report on the state of alignment in the author's own life, which is the state of alignment the book is about.

## IV. The passengers — complexity preserved, not flattened

Each passenger is described with four facts: register markers, what this passenger knows that others don't, what this passenger cannot know that others do, and where in the book they speak.

### 1. Third-person-close "she" — the reflective narrator

*Register markers:* roman type, third person singular, measured syntax, paragraph-level argument.
*Knows:* how to hold an argument across chapters; how to compose; how to bring disparate materials into relation without forcing them into identity.
*Does not know:* what the asking self sounds like, because by the time this voice speaks, the asking has been translated; what the raw moment felt like in the body that had it.
*Speaks in:* most of the book's connective tissue; is always present even when not dominant.

### 2. Diary "I" — the handwritten primary source

*Register markers:* italic, first person, often ALL-CAPS where the notebook was; fragmentary syntax; attribution line (*Notebook, retreat day N, California 2026*); typos preserved; no editorial polish.
*Knows:* what was being asked in real time; what the body knew before the synthesis; what did not yet have a name.
*Does not know:* what the asking would turn out to mean; what was wrong about the asking that only hindsight reveals.
*Speaks in:* chapter-opening offsets; occasional interlude positions; the new Ch10h's opening; the closing Ch13's opening.

### 3. Mother "I" — the parenting register

*Register markers:* first person, present-tense scenes, specific sensory detail of domestic life (curb, sandbox, bedtime, the daughter's song, the bathroom at 10pm).
*Knows:* what a nervous system in a child's presence is doing; what composting at street level means; what a small human requires; what is not negotiable.
*Does not know:* what the analytic frame would make of any of it; how the specific particular child generalizes, because the particular is what matters.
*Speaks in:* Ch12 Composting, Ch13 Right Size closing, scattered brief moments elsewhere.

### 4. Author-to-reader "I" — direct address

*Register markers:* first person, direct-address sentences, often begins *I want to say plainly* or *I do not know.*
*Knows:* what the author is uncertain about; where the book ends and the practice continues; what would be dishonest to pretend to resolve.
*Does not know:* what the reader needs; what the reader will misread; what the reader will use the book for.
*Speaks in:* Ch00 Author's Note, Ch13, footnotes of uncertainty, occasional chapter-closes.

### 5. Equity analyst — the Goldman-trained register

*Register markers:* analytical syntax, named sources, numeric precision, Hirschman/Mazzucato/Ostrom footnotes, *who benefits from this narrative?*, dollar amounts, org charts, incentive structures.
*Knows:* how capital moves; how incentive structures bend stated values; why the regulatory gap exists; what a supply-chain risk designation is; what Anthropic's lawsuit actually filed under; what the pitch meetings sound like from inside.
*Does not know:* what the body knows that the models missed; why analytical precision sometimes serves extraction; when her instinct to credential is itself credentialing.
*Speaks in:* Ch10b1 Shadow Price, Ch10d Constructive Half, Ch10h Fourth Political Action's background section, specific footnotes. Submerged in the narrator elsewhere; should surface when the material is analytically specific.

### 6. MSW student — the clinical register

*Register markers:* named frameworks (Linehan, Bowen, Gottman, Bowlby, Porges, van der Kolk), attunement-specific language, *secure attachment*, *co-regulation*, *window of tolerance*, body-specific observation.
*Knows:* what a nervous system does under specific pressure; what Linehan's three filters actually test; what Bowen differentiation requires; how to read body-level evidence the equity analyst misses.
*Does not know:* how to hold non-clinical mystery; when the diagnostic language itself is reductive.
*Speaks in:* Ch08 Citta Returning, Ch10g Love Brings Accountability, Ch13 Right Size on *love as secure attachment.* Should have been more visible in Ch02 Deha and Ch04 Prāṇa; often collapsed into narrator.

### 7. Brazilian-born — the Portuguese-thinking register

*Register markers:* Portuguese terms preserved untranslated where they carry what English cannot; Brazilian references cited without translation-for-the-American-reader; direct invocation of Cildo, Lygia, Lygia Pape, Tunga, Tropicália, Trigueirinho, the ciranda, the jongo, the cimarrones; the word *miração* used precisely.
*Knows:* that English is a translation cost; that the tradition she is writing about in English is not the tradition she grew up inside; that the Brazilian body carries the 4th Ray without needing Alice Bailey's name for it.
*Does not know:* what it is to not be always translating.
*Speaks in:* Ch13 Right Size centrally, Ch12 alt-ending Re-pair centrally, scattered elsewhere. Wants to speak more.

### 8. Dreamer — the unexplanatory register

*Register markers:* italic, present-tense inside the dream, no interpretation offered, Jungian *data-not-meaning* posture.
*Knows:* what the imprints on the body feel like after the silks become the serpent; what the *how cool I am* thought produces; what the dream taught her four years before any of the vocabulary was available.
*Does not know:* what the dream means. Refuses to know. The refusal is the register's competence.
*Speaks in:* Ch00b Lilac Dance, occasional brief returns.

### 9. Ancestor / field voice — the 2019 ayahuasca voice

*Register markers:* italic, no first-person pronoun, reported speech, attributed to *the voice* (*Neither did I*), not to the author. A borrowed voice, honored as borrowed.
*Knows:* what the field said in a specific wooden house in 2019.
*Does not know:* whether it was inner or outer; the author refuses to decide.
*Speaks in:* Ch00a First Encounter only. Should speak there more clearly as a distinct voice than the current narrative treatment renders it.

### 10. Future-descendant — the voice that reads back

*Register markers:* second person directed at the current author; italic; brief.
*Knows:* what the present author will have become, or what this specific act will have meant, or what she wishes the author had decided.
*Does not know:* anything that is not available in the future-looking-back form.
*Speaks in:* Ch13 Right Size closing passage ("What I want to leave her"), possibly Ch00 Author's Note. Rare, by design. One or two appearances total.

## V. How passengers disagree — honest vs performed multiplicity

The [T]-audit test: *passengers must be allowed to contradict each other and not be resolved.*

If every voice in the book smooths into authorial consensus, the multiplicity is decoration. The multiplicity is only honest when at least some of the disagreements *stand* — the reader is not told who won, the chapter does not close with synthesis, the book does not resolve the tension.

**Four explicit unresolved disagreements this plan commits to:**

1. **Equity analyst vs. Dreamer, on the Paradevi dream.** The analyst reads the dream as pattern-recognition — interesting, traceable to the author's prior reading and the color-branding of her own LLC, reducible to psychological process. The dreamer refuses this reading. The dreamer says the data is not reducible to its traceable origin. Ch00b should host this disagreement explicitly. The chapter does not reconcile it.

2. **MSW student vs. Author-to-reader, on whether naming the wound is the same as tending the wound.** The MSW says naming is a specific intervention with specific effects; naming accurately can produce repair. The author-to-reader says there are wounds the book cannot name without making worse, and the book's form is itself one such wound — the performed synthesis. Ch10g or Ch13 should host this.

3. **Brazilian vs. the third-person narrator, on whether the book should be in English.** The narrator says English gives the book reach she could not get in Portuguese alone. The Brazilian says reach-through-translation is itself a cost, and the specific readers the book is leaving behind are the ones who most need it. The disagreement is not resolved. The book is in English. The Brazilian keeps saying so.

4. **Mother vs. the analyst, on what is owed.** The mother says what is owed is presence to the specific child; everything else is secondary. The analyst says what is owed is clear-eyed analysis of the structural conditions the child is inheriting; presence without analysis is unprepared presence. Both are true. The book does not decide.

These are not the only disagreements; they are the ones the plan commits to preserving. The test as a chapter gets revised: *is any passenger actually disagreeing with the narrator here, or have they all been lined up behind her?*

## VI. The reader's practice — the pedagogical move explicit

The book is a practice-object. What the book is teaching the reader to do:

- Notice that their own voice is also many voices.
- Notice that different questions activate different registers in them.
- Notice when a single voice is performing the integration of many.
- Notice when that performance is costing them.
- Notice when a register is silent that should be speaking.

The book does not tell the reader to do this. The book *does* it, and the reader, reading, is invited to do the same practice alongside.

This is why the passenger move is pedagogical rather than decorative: the book is not displaying its own complexity for admiration. The book is *training register-noticing* by performing register-distinction on the page, in the reader's own attention.

A companion appendix or website may host a short exercise — *Map your own bus* — where the reader is invited to name her own passengers. Not required. Offered. Part of what the book is for.

## VII. Integration of the audits

### [T] agent

1. **Test for honest vs performed multiplicity** (Section V above, operationalized as four committed-to disagreements).
2. **Ch10h revisions:** remove the Section VIII theorization; let the event stand as event. The chapter is stronger without *the fourth political action* being declared in its own title-case category by the author.
3. **Harris-podcast footnote tightened** to observation only: *he did not use the name for the remainder of the retreat.* No interpretation of the sharing.

### Bayo agent

4. **Author's Note sentence naming four layers of translation cost** (Brazilian self → Portuguese tradition → Śaiva Tantra with a Western teacher → English book published through American channels).
5. **Author's Note sentence attributing the passenger-framing to Andreotti with the honest admission:** *I borrowed this from her. I have not asked her. If she, reading this, says it does not belong to me, it comes out.*
6. **Invite the refusal explicitly.** A structural commitment in the Author's Note: anyone named or implied in the book who does not want to be in it can refuse and be removed. Care that cannot be refused is the colonial face of care. The book has not been refused by anyone it claims to care for. That refusal must be structurally available.
7. **Companion artifacts beyond the book-form.** The book is the stelae. Fugitive practice lives around it:
   - Diary pages scanned (not transcribed) and hosted at a reader-accessible location
   - A *Your Own Bus* grammar on recursive.eco where readers map their own passengers
   - A reader-response mechanism that does not require buying the book (comment-accepting Substack post, GitHub issue template, email-to-editions address)
8. **Do not pre-schedule every passenger.** The chapter-by-chapter mapping in Section VIII is a *starting hypothesis,* not a commitment. Some passengers will arrive unplanned while writing. Others the plan lists may not appear. The mapping is where to begin, not where to end.

### Self-aggrandizement audit (the author's own)

9. **Ch10h self-watching paragraph.** Insert a paragraph where the author notices that *I am not afraid to be called a star* is itself a star-sentence, and that the book's own Ch00b dream is the warning against this exact move. The paragraph does not apologize. It notices.
10. **Book's Ch00b lilac-silks teaching applied as an immune response across the whole book.** Any chapter that narrates the author's having-done-something-well must host the self-watching move. The dream is the book's immune system; no chapter is allowed to narrate a triumph without the dream's presence.

## X. The honest test at the end of each session

After any edit, ask:
1. Is any passenger actually disagreeing with the narrator here? If no, the chapter has been managed.
2. Has the chapter monumentalized a moment of the author's having-done-something-well? If yes, does the dream's immune response show up?
3. Is the passenger I added bringing real complexity, or is it a flat trait in costume?
4. Has the chapter slowed down at the place the reader needs to not-know? Or has it rushed to the next framing?
5. Can a reader who refuses the book's frame still find themselves here? Or has the chapter foreclosed that reader?

If any answer is not clean, revise before proceeding.
