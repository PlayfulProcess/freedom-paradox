# Editorial Process Notes — Lessons from Axiom v3

*Created April 2026 after Kindle review revealed narrative inconsistencies.*

---

## Before Writing Narrative Chapters

1. **Create a character bible** before writing the first narrative chapter
   - Full backstory, family composition (exact number of children, ages, names/no-names)
   - Professional history with correct job titles (equity research analyst, not "investment banking analyst")
   - Spiritual/existential history with clear timeline
   - Key objects, apartment layout, household vocabulary

2. **Create a domestic timeline**
   - When does the character wake, eat, sleep?
   - What is the bedtime routine? What time?
   - When does the void/question arrive? Ground it in real parenting rhythms.
   - No mother is routinely up at 2am. The void arrives at ~10pm.

3. **Decide family composition before writing**
   - How many children? Ages? Gender?
   - Stick to the decision across ALL chapters
   - Search for "children" vs "child" before publishing

4. **Map logistics for travel scenes**
   - How does she get to the retreat? Car or train? Pick one.
   - If she drives, she drives both ways. Don't have the husband drive her to a train and then have her driving a car.

5. **Read source material carefully before referencing**
   - The Silverstein poem "Importnt?" has TWO parts — little a's claim AND big G's response
   - The original text only presented little a's side and got the poem's conclusion wrong
   - Always read the actual source, not your memory of it

## Narrator Rules

6. **The narrator observes, never evaluates**
   - No: "compelling," "devastating," "brilliant," "extraordinary," "remarkable," "striking," "powerful," "profound," "radical" (unless technical term like "radical acceptance")
   - These words are OK only in: first-person Author's Note, character's interior thoughts, direct quotes
   - "Who says it's compelling? Let the reader decide."

7. **No absolutist claims**
   - "the only" → "among the few"
   - "every tradition" → "the traditions examined here"
   - "the most corrosive" → just state it directly without superlative ranking

8. **Judgment-adjective audit before publishing**
   - grep for: compelling, devastating, brilliant, extraordinary, remarkable, striking, powerful, profound, radical, central to, the most
   - For each hit: is this narrator voice or character voice? If narrator, cut or rephrase.

## Consistency Checks

9. **Run these searches before any EPUB build**
   - `children` vs `child` — make sure family size is consistent
   - `2am` / `two in the morning` / `two o'clock` — check all time references
   - Character names / titles — make sure they match across chapters
   - Transportation — trace any travel scene for logical consistency

10. **Cross-reference the character bible against each chapter**
    - Does the apartment description match?
    - Does the family composition match?
    - Does the timeline match?
    - Does the backstory match?
