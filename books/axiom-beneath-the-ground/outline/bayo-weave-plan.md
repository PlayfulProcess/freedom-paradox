## Where Bayo currently lives in the v5 manuscript

Ch11 — three light references:
1. In the **channels** section: first encountered via *The Emerald*, re-encountered
   as "the voice someone would cite to me on a deck at Esalen." Framed as partial
   in the way every channel was partial. "Bayo taught at the cracks. He still does.
   I am still learning to sit there."
2. In the **Josh Schrei** section: Bayo is the figure whose work was
   deeply intertwined with Vanessa Andreotti's and who PlayfulProcess accessed
   via *The Emerald* before understanding what she was accessing.
3. In the **IP interlude**: "Bayo Akomolafe would call AI a trickster, questioning
   our whole anthropocenic view of things. Intelligence is not human." One sentence,
   followed by a paragraph that flags the framing as "the deepest frame available to
   me" without operationalizing it.

The references are honest but thin. They function as load-bearing references for a
stance the book is taking, without actually doing the work of engaging Bayo's
frame at the level it can change what the book argues.

## What the weave should accomplish

Three things, in order of importance:

1. **Deepen the anthropocenic-frame critique** so that when the book invokes
   Bayo in the IP interlude ("AI is a trickster; intelligence is not human")
   the invocation sits on top of three or four sentences of Bayo's actual work,
   not on an unexplained gesture. Current text assumes the reader recognizes the
   move; a significant portion of readers will not.

2. **Name the specific Bayo thread that interlocks with the book's argument**:
   *post-activism / politics beyond the psychologized individual*. The Emerald
   episode "The Revolution Will Not Be Psychologized, Part 2 (Interview w
   Báyò Akómoláfé)" is the most concentrated source we have access to for this.
   The thread — that therapeutic individualism is itself a container that limits
   the political imagination — intersects Axiom's central move (freedom is the
   spiritual question, value is the moral question) in a way we should make
   explicit rather than elide. Bayo's critique complicates the book's therapeutic-
   adjacent register in a useful and honest way.

3. **Hold the tension between Bayo's cracks-as-sites frame and the book's
   svātantrya / Pratyabhijñā frame**. These rhyme but are not the same. The
   tradition says: the ground is already free; practice returns you to the
   recognition. Bayo says: the cracks are where the dominant frame can no
   longer narrate, and new modes of being become possible *because* the
   narration has failed. Both frames privilege the break. The tradition
   reads the break as personal recognition; Bayo reads the break as collective
   and ecological. Name the difference. Do not pretend it is absent.

## Where to place the deepened material

Option A (preferred): **A new short interlude in Ch11**, placed between the
IP interlude and the Channels section. Title-less per the chapter style. Length:
~600–900 words. Title in my head: "The frame that is asking the question."

Option B: **Expand the existing one-paragraph Bayo reference inside the IP
interlude.** Lower effort, but weakens the chapter because Bayo deserves more
than a footnote to a Dario discussion.

Option C: **A new interlude chapter (ch11b)**, only if the material generates
more than ~1,500 words of honest prose. Unlikely needed. A chapter would
over-weight Bayo relative to other figures.

**Decision: Option A.** Aim ~700 words. If it runs long, compress before
bloating to chapter length.

## What the interlude should and should NOT do

**Should do:**
- Name *post-activism* as Bayo's own term and explain it in two sentences.
- Draw the crack-site / anthropocenic-frame-interrogation line.
- Connect to Vanessa Andreotti's Facing Human Wrongs work (already named in
  the Channels section) as the collective behind the frame.
- Hold the honest distinction: PlayfulProcess is building inside the system
  Bayo is questioning. She does not resolve this. She names it.
- Quote Bayo VERY BRIEFLY (≤30 words, attributed) if a quote earns its keep.
- Reference the *Emerald* interview without naming the specific episode
  in the body text (per CLAUDE.md podcast-local-only rules — episode titles
  of copyrighted material are also not committed). Phrase it as "in a
  long interview conversation with Josh Schrei" or similar.

**Should NOT do:**
- Operationalize Bayo as if his frame gives the book its answer. It does not.
  It gives the book its *question*.
- Flatten the distinction between Bayo's decolonial-ecological frame and the
  tantric frame the book mostly inhabits.
- Lean on secondary sources or my paraphrase without verification against
  Bayo's own material.
- Turn the interlude into a second "channels" list. One figure, one frame.

## What this interlude is NOT

This is not the place to write the decolonial critique of the book's own
frame. That is a whole book, or at least a whole essay, and it is not what
Axiom is. The Bayo interlude must *name the frame that is asking the
question the book cannot fully answer*, and then let the book continue to do
what the book is doing — with the frame now visible to the reader.

If PP wants the full decolonial engagement with the tradition, that is a
different book. Mark it for the memoir project, alongside the Ayahuasca and
Paradevi threads.
