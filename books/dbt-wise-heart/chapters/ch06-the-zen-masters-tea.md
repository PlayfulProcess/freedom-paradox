# The Zen Master's Tea

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

A student rushed into the Zen master's room.

'Master! I need to learn everything! Teach me about meditation, about wisdom, about the meaning of life — and please hurry, I have another lesson at three!'

The master said nothing. She poured tea.

She poured the cup full. Then she kept pouring.

Tea spilled over the edges, onto the table, onto the floor.

'Stop!' cried the student. 'The cup is full! You can't put more in!'

The master smiled. 'You are like this cup. You are so full of things you want to learn that there's no room for any of them. First — empty your cup.'

The student sat down. Slowly.

The master poured a fresh cup. One cup. Nothing else.

'Now,' said the master. 'Drink THIS tea. Just this one. Don't think about the next cup. Don't think about your lesson at three. Just drink this tea.'

The student drank.

It was the best tea he'd ever tasted.

Not because the tea was special.
But because he was finally HERE for it.

## The Song

Do one thing with all your might —
This one thing. This breath. This bite.

## The Skill

This story teaches ONE-MINDFULLY — doing one thing at a time with your full attention. Not thinking about what's next while you're doing what's now. Just this. Just here.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: ONE-MINDFULLY (Core Mindfulness — 'How' Skills). One-mindfully means doing one thing at a time, with full awareness. In an age of distraction and multitasking, this is revolutionary. Practice: Try 'one-mindful minutes' — eating one bite mindfully, brushing teeth without thinking about school, walking to the car noticing only your feet. Start with just 60 seconds.

## Try This

Eat one raisin (or one bite of anything) like the Zen master's tea. Look at it first. Smell it. Put it in your mouth slowly. Chew and notice everything. How is this different from how you usually eat?

## Reflection

What do you usually think about while you're eating? While you're brushing your teeth? What would it be like to do JUST that thing?
