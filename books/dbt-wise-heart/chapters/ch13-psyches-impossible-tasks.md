# Psyche's Impossible Tasks

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

Aphrodite hated Psyche and gave her four impossible tasks.

Sort this mountain of mixed seeds — by morning.
Fetch golden wool from the killer rams.
Fill this flask from the river that flows between the cliffs.
Go to the underworld and bring back Persephone's beauty in a box.

Each task was designed to make Psyche give up.

Psyche sat before the mountain of seeds and wept. It WAS impossible.

But then — ants came. Thousands of tiny ants. And they sorted the seeds.

The reeds by the river whispered: 'Wait until the rams sleep. Gather the wool from the bushes.'

An eagle carried her flask to the impossible river.

A tower told her the path to the underworld.

Psyche didn't have the power to do any of these things. What she had was WILLINGNESS — she showed up to each impossible task. She sat before the mountain. She walked to the river. She went to the underworld.

And because she was willing, help appeared.

Willingness doesn't mean you can do it. It means you'll show up and try — even when it looks impossible.

## The Song

I don't have to do it all — just start.
Willing hands and open heart.

## The Skill

This story teaches WILLINGNESS vs. WILLFULNESS — showing up to do what's needed, even when it seems impossible. Willfulness says 'I can't, so I won't try.' Willingness says 'I can't, but I'll show up anyway.'

## For Parents

DBT Skill: WILLINGNESS vs. WILLFULNESS (Distress Tolerance). Willingness is doing what's needed in the moment, fully and without reservation, even when you don't feel ready. Willfulness is refusing to tolerate the moment, digging in, giving up. Practice: When your child says 'I CAN'T,' try: 'You might be right. But would you be willing to sit with it for two minutes? Just be near the problem?' Willingness is the door. Ability comes after.

## Try This

Think of something you've been avoiding because it feels impossible. Don't try to do the whole thing. Just go SIT near it. Open the book but don't read. Stand by the mess but don't clean. Just be willing to be there. See what shows up (your version of the ants).

## Reflection

What's your mountain of seeds — the task that feels impossible? What might happen if you showed up anyway, without needing to know how to do it?
