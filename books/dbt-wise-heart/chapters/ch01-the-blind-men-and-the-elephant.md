# The Blind Men and the Elephant

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

Once, six wise men lived in a village. They were wise about many things, but they had never seen an elephant.

One day an elephant came to the village.

The first wise man touched the elephant's side. 'An elephant is like a wall!' he said.

The second touched the tusk. 'No — an elephant is like a spear!'

The third touched the trunk. 'You're both wrong — an elephant is like a snake!'

The fourth touched the leg. 'It's like a tree!'

The fifth touched the ear. 'It's like a fan!'

The sixth touched the tail. 'It's like a rope!'

They argued and argued. Each one was sure he was right.

Then a child walked up. The child couldn't see either — but the child listened.

'You're all right,' the child said. 'And you're all wrong. You each touched ONE part. But the elephant is ALL of it.'

The wise men went quiet.

The elephant swished its tail and walked away.

And the child smiled — not because she knew more, but because she held all the pieces together without choosing just one.

## The Song

Two minds talking, one feels, one thinks —
Wise Mind listens to both, and blinks.

## The Skill

This story teaches WISE MIND — the place where your thinking mind and your feeling mind meet. When you only think, you miss what you feel. When you only feel, you miss what you know. Wise Mind holds both.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: WISE MIND (Core Mindfulness). Wise Mind is the synthesis of Reasonable Mind (logic, facts) and Emotion Mind (feelings, intuitions). It's not about suppressing either — it's about finding the place where both are true. Practice: When your child is upset, try saying 'What does your thinking brain say? What does your feeling heart say? What does your wise mind — right here in your belly — say?'

## Try This

Put one hand on your head (thinking mind) and one hand on your heart (feeling mind). Now put both hands on your belly. That's where Wise Mind lives. Next time you have a hard choice, try asking your belly what it thinks.

## Reflection

Can you think of a time when your thinking said one thing and your feelings said another? What happened when you listened to both?
