# Anansi and the Stories

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

Anansi the spider wanted something impossible: he wanted to own ALL the stories in the world.

The Sky God laughed. 'You? A tiny spider? Even the great kingdoms couldn't buy my stories.'

'Name your price,' said Anansi.

The Sky God named four impossible tasks: bring me Onini the python, Mmoboro the hornets, Osebo the leopard, and Mmoatia the spirit.

Anyone else would have tried to fight them. Anansi was too small to fight.

So Anansi did what WORKED instead.

For the python, he argued with his wife about whether the python was longer than a stick — until the python stretched out to prove it, and Anansi tied him to the stick.

For the hornets, he poured water on them and told them it was raining, then offered them his gourd as shelter — and sealed them inside.

For the leopard, he dug a pit and covered it with branches.

For the spirit, he made a tar baby and placed food next to it.

Anansi didn't use the RIGHT way. He used what WORKED.

And the Sky God gave him all the stories. Every story you've ever heard — that's Anansi's gift.

Because sometimes being effective is better than being right.

## The Song

Don't need to be right, don't need to be strong —
Just do what works and move along.

## The Skill

This story teaches EFFECTIVELY — doing what works in a situation, instead of insisting on what's 'right' or 'fair.' Anansi couldn't fight, so he found what actually worked. Sometimes effective is more important than correct.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: EFFECTIVELY (Core Mindfulness — 'How' Skills). Acting effectively means focusing on what works in a situation rather than on what's 'right,' 'fair,' or 'should' be. It doesn't mean abandoning values — it means being strategic about how you achieve them. Practice: When your child is stuck in a 'but it's not FAIR' loop, validate the feeling and then ask: 'You're right, it's not fair. So — what would actually WORK here?'

## Try This

The Anansi Challenge: Think of a problem you have. Now think of THREE different ways to solve it — including silly ones. Which one would actually WORK best? (It might be the silly one!)

## Reflection

Can you think of a time when being right was less important than being effective? What did you do?
