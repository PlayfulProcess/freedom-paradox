# Stone Soup

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

A hungry traveler arrived in a village. She had nothing. No food, no money, no friends in this place.

She set a pot of water over a fire and dropped in a stone.

'What are you making?' asked a villager.

'Stone soup!' she said. 'It's delicious — though it would be even BETTER with a carrot.'

The villager brought a carrot.

'Wonderful! Of course, a little onion would make it perfect.'

Another villager brought an onion.

'Oh! If only we had a potato...' And so it went. Bit by bit, person by person, the soup grew richer. Herbs. Beans. Bread to go with it.

By evening, the whole village was eating together. Laughing. Sharing.

The traveler didn't make the soup.
She accumulated it.

One small positive experience at a time. One carrot. One conversation. One tiny moment of connection.

And by the end, the pot was full.

You build a good life the same way. Not all at once. Not by waiting for something huge and wonderful. But by collecting small positives — one good moment, one kind exchange, one thing you enjoy — until your life is a pot full of soup you made together.

## The Song

One small good thing every day —
Drop it in and watch it stay.
Bit by bit, the pot fills up —
Life is made from cup by cup.

## The Skill

This story teaches ACCUMULATE POSITIVE EXPERIENCES — building a life worth living by collecting small good moments. You don't wait for happiness to arrive all at once. You build it like stone soup — one carrot, one onion, one kind word at a time.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: ACCUMULATE POSITIVE EXPERIENCES (Emotion Regulation — ABC Skills). This skill has two parts: short-term (do pleasant things NOW — daily) and long-term (build a life worth living — values, goals, relationships). The stone soup metaphor captures both: each ingredient is a daily pleasant experience, and the communal meal is the long-term life you're building. Practice: Make a 'soup pot list' with your child — small pleasant things they can add daily. A walk. A favorite song. Petting the cat. Drawing. The key insight: positive experiences must be ACCUMULATED, not waited for.

## Try This

Start a Soup Pot Journal: Every day, write down (or draw) one small good thing that happened. Just one. After a week, read them all together. After a month, you'll see: your life has more good in it than you thought. You were just too busy to notice the carrots.

## Reflection

What are the 'carrots' in your life — the small good things you might not notice? What would happen if you started collecting them on purpose?
