# Scheherazade's Thousand Nights

*Interpersonal-Effectiveness*

## The Story

The king had decided to kill his wife every morning. Every night he married a new woman. Every dawn, she died.

Scheherazade volunteered to be next.

Her father begged her not to go. 'You'll die!'

But Scheherazade had a plan.

That night, she told the king a story. A wonderful story. But she stopped RIGHT before the ending.

'What happens next?' demanded the king.

'I'll tell you tomorrow night,' said Scheherazade.

The king let her live one more day.

The next night, she finished that story — and started another. Again, she stopped before the ending.

One more day.

Night after night after night. A thousand and one nights.

Scheherazade didn't beg. She didn't fight. She didn't run.

She DESCRIBED what she could offer (the stories).
She EXPRESSED why they mattered (they revealed truth about human nature).
She ASSERTED what she needed (one more night).
She REINFORCED the king's choice to listen (each story was a gift).

She was MINDFUL — never pushing too hard.
She APPEARED confident — even when she was terrified.
She NEGOTIATED — always offering something valuable in exchange.

And after a thousand and one nights, the king didn't just spare her. He loved her. Because she had shown him who he could be instead of who he'd been.

## The Song

Say what you need, with steady voice —
Respect yourself and give them a choice.

## The Skill

This story teaches DEAR MAN — how to ask for what you need effectively. Describe the situation. Express your feelings. Assert what you want. Reinforce why it's worth it. Be Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: DEAR MAN (Interpersonal Effectiveness — Objectives Effectiveness). DEAR MAN is the core interpersonal skill: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, (stay) Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate. Scheherazade is the master class. Practice: Before your child asks for something important, help them prepare their DEAR MAN: 'What's the situation? How do you feel? What exactly do you want? What can you offer in return?'

## Try This

The Scheherazade Script: Think of something you want to ask for. Now write your script: (1) Describe: 'Here's what's happening...' (2) Express: 'I feel...' (3) Assert: 'I would like...' (4) Reinforce: 'If you do this, it will...' Practice saying it to a mirror.

## Reflection

Scheherazade saved her life with words, not weapons. When have words been your most powerful tool?
