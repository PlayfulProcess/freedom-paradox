# Orpheus at the Threshold

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

Orpheus went to the underworld to bring back Eurydice, his love.

Hades gave him one rule: 'Walk out. She will follow. But do NOT look back.'

Orpheus walked. He could hear footsteps behind him. (Or could he?) He could feel her presence. (Or was he imagining it?)

Every step, his mind screamed: LOOK BACK. CHECK. MAKE SURE.

His fear said: 'What if she's not there?'
His doubt said: 'What if Hades lied?'
His love said: 'JUST ONE LOOK.'

Step after step, he turned his mind back to the path. Forward. Forward.

But at the very last moment — just before the sunlight — he turned.

And Eurydice vanished forever.

Here is the lesson, and it's a hard one: Orpheus knew what to do. He chose it, step after step. But turning the mind isn't a one-time choice. It's a choice you make AGAIN and AGAIN, step after step.

Sometimes you make it all the way.
Sometimes you turn at the last gate.

Either way, the practice is the same: turn your mind back. Again. And again.

## The Song

Turn around, turn around, face the way —
I can choose again today.

## The Skill

This story teaches TURNING THE MIND — choosing acceptance over and over, even when your mind keeps pulling you back to fear, doubt, or the urge to control. It's not a one-time choice. It's a practice.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: TURNING THE MIND (Distress Tolerance). Turning the mind means making a conscious choice to accept reality, and then making that choice again when the mind wanders back to non-acceptance. It's the bridge between deciding to accept and actually getting there. Practice: When your child is stuck in a worry loop or 'what if' spiral, gently say: 'Your mind went back again. Can you turn it back to right now?' Make it feel like a muscle, not a test.

## Try This

Sit still for one minute. Try to think about just one thing — a color, a sound, your breath. Your mind WILL wander. That's fine! Just notice it wandered, and turn it back. Count how many times you turn it back. That number? That's how strong your turning muscle is.

## Reflection

What's your 'Eurydice' — the thing your mind keeps turning back to check on, even when you know you should keep walking forward?
