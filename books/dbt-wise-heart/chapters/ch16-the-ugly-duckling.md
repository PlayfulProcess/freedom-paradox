# The Ugly Duckling

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

The ugly duckling was laughed at by everyone. The ducks. The hens. Even his own mother.

'You're too big. You're too grey. You're too WRONG.'

He ran away. Through the cold autumn. Through the terrible winter. Through the loneliness that felt like it would never end.

But every day — every single day — he swam. He paddled through icy water. He stretched his wings even though they were too small. He practiced being alive, even when being alive hurt.

He didn't know what he was becoming. He just kept going.

And then spring came. And he looked in the water.

A swan. The most beautiful bird on the lake.

He didn't BECOME a swan in spring. He was always a swan. But the daily practice — the swimming, the stretching, the surviving — that's what built the strength to finally see it.

Building mastery means doing one hard thing every day. Not because it fixes everything. But because each small thing you survive makes you stronger for the next.

## The Song

Do one hard thing, just one today —
Small and strong, that's the way.

## The Skill

This story teaches BUILD MASTERY — doing one slightly challenging thing every day to build confidence and competence. Not huge things — small things. Each one makes you a little more capable of handling the next hard thing.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: BUILD MASTERY (Emotion Regulation — reducing vulnerability). Building mastery means engaging daily in activities that give a sense of accomplishment and competence. The key is choosing activities that are challenging but achievable — in the 'stretch zone.' Practice: Help your child set one small daily challenge: learn a new word, do one more push-up than yesterday, try a food they've never had. Celebrate the trying, not just the achieving.

## Try This

The Duckling Challenge: Pick one thing that's just a LITTLE bit hard for you. Do it every day for a week. It could be: making your bed, saying hi to someone new, reading one extra page. Draw a feather each day you do it. By the end of the week, you'll have wings.

## Reflection

What's something you couldn't do last year that you can do now? How did it happen — all at once, or one small step at a time?
