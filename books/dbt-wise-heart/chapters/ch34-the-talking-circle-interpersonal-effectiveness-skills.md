# The Talking Circle: Interpersonal Effectiveness Skills

*Module*

## The Song

Say what you need, hear what they say —
Two hearts talking, that's the way.

## For Parents

This module covers DBT Interpersonal Effectiveness — skills for navigating relationships. The three sub-skills address different goals: DEAR MAN (get what you want), GIVE (maintain the relationship), FAST (maintain self-respect). The dialectic: all three matter, and sometimes they conflict. Validation is the master skill — research shows that feeling validated reduces emotional arousal faster than almost any other intervention.

## About

Every relationship is a story we tell together. These four stories teach the three goals of interpersonal effectiveness: getting what you need (DEAR MAN), keeping the relationship (GIVE), and keeping your self-respect (FAST). Plus the skill that underlies all relationships: validation — seeing someone as they really are and saying 'yes, that's real.'

## How to Use

DEAR MAN (Scheherazade) is great for school-age children learning to advocate for themselves. GIVE (The Giving Tree) opens a conversation about boundaries. FAST (Nasreddin) is crucial for children who over-apologize. Validation (Velveteen Rabbit) is the one to read first — it models what the child needs to RECEIVE before they can offer it to others.
