# The Giving Tree

*Interpersonal-Effectiveness*

## The Story

There is an old story about a boy and an apple tree.

When the boy was small, the tree gave shade. The boy climbed and played. The tree was happy.

When the boy grew older, he took the apples. 'I need money,' he said. The tree gave its apples.

When the boy was a man, he took the branches. 'I need a house.' The tree gave its branches.

When the man was old, he took the trunk. 'I need a boat.' The tree gave its trunk.

And when the very old man came back, there was only a stump. 'I just need somewhere to sit,' he said. The stump offered its last self.

Now — some people tell this story as a beautiful story about love. And it IS beautiful.

But there's another way to see it. The tree never said: 'Wait — what about ME?'

Being gentle matters. Being kind matters. But being gentle doesn't mean giving away everything you are.

The GIVE skills say: be Gentle, act Interested, Validate, use an Easy manner. YES. Do all those things.

But don't give away your trunk.

The best kindness includes being kind to yourself.

## The Song

Be gentle, be kind, listen with care —
But keep enough of yourself to share.

## The Skill

This story teaches GIVE — how to be kind in relationships AND keep yourself whole. Gentle (no attacks). Interested (listen). Validate (show you understand). Easy manner (lighten up). But never give away your whole self.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: GIVE (Interpersonal Effectiveness — Relationship Effectiveness). GIVE skills maintain relationships: be Gentle, act Interested, Validate, use Easy manner. But this story adds the dialectic: you can give AND keep yourself. Practice: When your child is being 'too nice' (giving away toys they love, always letting others choose), validate the kindness AND ask: 'Are you giving from your branches or from your trunk?'

## Try This

Draw a tree with roots, trunk, branches, leaves, and fruit. Label the parts: Roots = what makes you YOU. Trunk = what you need to stay strong. Branches = what you can share. Fruit = what you happily give. Now: which parts are you giving away that you should keep?

## Reflection

Have you ever given so much to someone that you felt like a stump? What would you do differently now?
