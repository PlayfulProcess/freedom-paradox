# The Dark Wood: Distress Tolerance Skills

*Module*

## The Song

When the dark wood has no door —
Breathe and bend, you've been here before.

## For Parents

This module covers DBT Distress Tolerance — skills for surviving crisis without making things worse. These are NOT problem-solving skills (that's Emotion Regulation). These are 'the building is on fire and I need to survive until the fire trucks arrive' skills. Key insight: the goal is not to feel better, it's to not make things worse. Radical Acceptance is the cornerstone — pain + non-acceptance = suffering.

## About

Sometimes the dark comes. You're inside the whale, in the underworld, in the labyrinth. You can't think your way out. You can't fix it. The only skill is SURVIVING without making it worse. These ten stories teach crisis survival: how to stop before you react, accept what is, calm your body, distract wisely, improve the unbearable moment, and show up even when it seems impossible.

## How to Use

These stories are best read BEFORE crises, not during them. Read them when your child is calm. Practice the body-based skills (TIPP, self-soothe) regularly so they're available during distress. The ice cube exercise from Jonah is an excellent practice.
