# Vasilisa and the Doll

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

When Vasilisa's mother died, she gave her daughter one thing: a tiny doll.

'Feed the doll when you're afraid,' her mother whispered. 'It will help you.'

So when the cruel stepmother sent Vasilisa to Baba Yaga's hut — the witch in the woods, the house on chicken legs — Vasilisa was terrified.

But she held the doll.

She imagined her mother's hands sewing it. (Imagery.)
She thought: 'My mother wouldn't have sent me with nothing. This doll MEANS something.' (Meaning.)
She whispered to the doll as if praying. (Prayer.)
She fed the doll and felt her breathing slow. (Relaxation.)
She focused on just the next task Baba Yaga gave her. (One thing in the moment.)
She imagined surviving this night, then the next one. (mini-Vacation.)
She told herself: 'You can do this. Your mother believed you could.' (Encouragement.)

And the doll — the tiny doll — sorted the poppy seeds from the dirt. Cleaned the house. Did the impossible.

Not because it was magic.
Because Vasilisa was IMPROVING the moment. She was making the unbearable slightly more bearable, one small comfort at a time.

She walked out of Baba Yaga's forest carrying fire.

## The Song

When you can't make it better yet,
Make this moment softer — don't forget:
One small comfort, one small light —
Improve the moment, hold on tight.

## The Skill

This story teaches IMPROVE the Moment — seven ways to make an unbearable moment slightly more bearable. Imagery, Meaning, Prayer, Relaxation, One thing in the moment, Vacation (brief mental escape), Encouragement. Vasilisa couldn't escape Baba Yaga. But she could improve each moment with her doll.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: IMPROVE the Moment (Distress Tolerance — Crisis Survival). I = Imagery (imagine a safe place). M = Meaning (find purpose in the suffering). P = Prayer (or connection to something larger). R = Relaxation (progressive muscle relaxation, slow breathing). O = One thing in the moment (focus only on now). V = Vacation (brief mental or physical break). E = Encouragement ('I can handle this'). Practice: The doll is a beautiful metaphor for a transitional object. Help your child identify their 'doll' — something small they can hold or touch when things feel impossible.

## Try This

Make your own Vasilisa's doll — not a literal doll (unless you want one!), but a small object you can carry that reminds you: 'I can handle this.' A smooth stone. A button from someone you love. A tiny note in your pocket. When things get hard, touch it and IMPROVE the moment.

## Reflection

What's your doll — the small thing that makes the unbearable slightly more bearable? Is it a person? A memory? Something you can hold?
