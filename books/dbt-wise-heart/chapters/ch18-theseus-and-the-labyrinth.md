# Theseus and the Labyrinth

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

Theseus was going into the Labyrinth to face the Minotaur.

The Labyrinth was a maze so twisting that no one who entered ever found their way out. And at the center waited a monster.

Theseus could have rushed in — brave, fearless, stupid.

Instead, he planned. With Ariadne's help, he prepared a ball of thread.

'Tie one end at the entrance,' Ariadne said. 'Unwind it as you go. When you've faced the Minotaur, follow the thread back out.'

So Theseus tied the thread. He walked into the dark. He unwound the ball, step by step.

He found the Minotaur. He faced it. (That part was hard.)

But here's the thing most people forget: the hardest part wasn't the Minotaur. The hardest part was the MAZE — the confusing, twisting, disorienting dark.

And he'd already solved that. Before he even went in.

The thread was his cope-ahead plan. He rehearsed the way out before he needed it. So when the crisis came, he could focus on the monster instead of panicking about the maze.

## The Song

Plan the thread before you go in —
Cope ahead and you will win.

## The Skill

This story teaches COPE AHEAD — rehearsing how you'll handle a difficult situation before you're in it. Theseus didn't just face the Minotaur. He planned the thread BEFORE he entered the maze.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: COPE AHEAD (Emotion Regulation). Cope ahead means mentally rehearsing how you'll handle a future stressful situation: imagine the situation, imagine the feelings, imagine yourself using skills to cope. It's not about eliminating anxiety — it's about having a plan so anxiety doesn't run the show. Practice: Before hard events (tests, dentist, social situations), walk through it together: 'What do you think will happen? How might you feel? What will you do? What's your thread?'

## Try This

Pick something coming up that you're nervous about. Now imagine it like a movie: see yourself walking in, see the hard part, and then see yourself doing something that helps (breathing, asking for help, taking a break). Play the movie three times. You just built your thread.

## Reflection

What's your Labyrinth right now — the hard situation coming up? What thread could you tie before you go in?
