# The Fox and the Golden Bird

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

A young prince needed to find a golden bird. He met a fox in the forest.

The fox said: 'Go to the castle. You'll find the bird in a wooden cage next to a golden cage. Take the wooden cage. Do NOT take the golden cage.'

The prince went to the castle. He saw the bird. He saw the golden cage, shining so beautifully.

'Surely,' he thought, 'the golden cage is better.'

He reached for the golden cage.

ALARM! Guards everywhere! He was caught.

The fox found him again. 'You didn't WATCH,' the fox said sadly. 'You didn't notice what was really there. You saw what was shiny and grabbed.'

The fox gave him another chance. And another.

Every time, the prince had to learn the same lesson: STOP. LOOK. Notice what is ACTUALLY here — not what your wanting mind sees.

Finally, the prince learned to observe first. And when he did, everything worked.

## The Song

Stop and look with quiet eyes —
What is here? What is wise?

## The Skill

This story teaches OBSERVE — paying attention to what's really there, not what your wanting mind sees. Before you grab, before you choose, just watch. Notice.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: OBSERVE (Core Mindfulness — 'What' Skills). Observing means attending to events, emotions, and sensations without trying to change them. The prince keeps failing because he reacts to desire before observing reality. Practice: Play 'What do you notice?' — sit quietly with your child for 30 seconds and each share three things you observe (a sound, a feeling, something you see).

## Try This

The Fox Game: Before you pick something up, eat something, or choose something — wait 3 seconds. Just look at it. What do you REALLY see? Is it what you thought?

## Reflection

When have you grabbed for something shiny and missed something better that was right there?
