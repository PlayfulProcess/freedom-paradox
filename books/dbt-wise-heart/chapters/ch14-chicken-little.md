# Chicken Little

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

An acorn fell on Chicken Little's head.

'THE SKY IS FALLING!' screamed Chicken Little.

Chicken Little told Henny Penny. Henny Penny told Ducky Lucky. Ducky Lucky told Goosey Loosey. Goosey Loosey told Turkey Lurkey.

Soon every animal in the barnyard was running and screaming: 'THE SKY IS FALLING! THE SKY IS FALLING!'

Nobody stopped to check.

Nobody looked up.

Nobody asked: 'Wait — did anyone actually SEE the sky falling?'

If they had looked up, they would have seen: a tree. With acorns. On a windy day.

The sky wasn't falling.
An acorn fell.

But by the time anyone checked, Foxy Loxy had led them all into his den for dinner.

The feeling was real — Chicken Little really DID feel something hit her head. But the story she told about the feeling? That was just a story.

And it was wrong.

Feelings are real. But the stories our feelings tell us? Those we can check.

## The Song

Is it true? Is it really real?
Check the facts behind what you feel.

## The Skill

This story teaches CHECK THE FACTS — when you have a big feeling, check whether the story your feeling is telling matches reality. The feeling is real. The story might not be.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: CHECK THE FACTS (Emotion Regulation). When emotions are intense, we generate interpretations that feel like facts. Checking the facts means asking: What triggered this? What am I ASSUMING? What are other possible explanations? Does the intensity of my reaction match the facts? Practice: When your child is upset, validate the feeling first ('That feeling is real'), then gently explore: 'What happened? What do you think it means? Could there be another explanation?'

## Try This

Next time you feel a big feeling, play Chicken Little Detective: (1) What happened? (Just the facts.) (2) What STORY am I telling about it? (3) Is there another story that fits the same facts? (4) Which story matches the facts best?

## Reflection

When was the last time an 'acorn' felt like 'the sky falling'? What was the acorn? What was the story?
