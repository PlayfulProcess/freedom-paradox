# The North Wind and the Sun

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

The North Wind and the Sun had an argument.

'I am stronger than you,' said the Wind.

'Let's see,' said the Sun. 'See that traveler with a coat? Whoever makes him take off his coat is stronger.'

The Wind went first. It BLEW. It HOWLED. It RAGED.

But the harder the Wind blew, the tighter the traveler held his coat.

The Wind blew harder. The traveler wrapped himself tighter.

Finally the Wind gave up.

Then the Sun simply shone. Gently. Warmly.

The traveler loosened his coat. He unbuttoned it. He took it off.

The Sun won. Not by blowing harder, but by doing the OPPOSITE.

When you're angry and want to SHOUT — what's the opposite? Speak softly.
When you're scared and want to HIDE — what's the opposite? Step forward.
When you're sad and want to stay in BED — what's the opposite? Go outside.

The opposite isn't always easy. But it often works when blowing harder doesn't.

## The Song

When the storm says push and shove and shout —
Try the opposite way out.

## The Skill

This story teaches OPPOSITE ACTION — when your emotion is telling you to do something that won't help, do the opposite. Anger says attack? Be gentle. Fear says hide? Approach. Sadness says withdraw? Connect.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: OPPOSITE ACTION (Emotion Regulation). When an emotion doesn't fit the facts OR when acting on it would make things worse, doing the opposite of the emotion's action urge can change the emotion itself. Key: the action must be done ALL THE WAY (not half-heartedly). Anger → gentle kindness. Fear → approach. Sadness → activate. Shame → share. Practice: Name the emotion, name the urge, then ask: 'What would the Sun do?'

## Try This

The Sun Challenge: Next time you feel a strong urge (to yell, to hide, to quit), try the opposite for just one minute. If you want to stomp, try walking softly. If you want to be alone, try sitting near someone. How does it feel?

## Reflection

Are you more like the Wind or the Sun? Can you think of a time when doing the opposite of what you felt like doing actually worked better?
