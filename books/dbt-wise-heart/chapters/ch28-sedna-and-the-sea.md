# Sedna and the Sea

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

Sedna fell into the sea.

Her father had thrown her from the boat. Her fingers, clinging to the edge, were cut off one by one. They became the seals, the walruses, the whales — all the creatures of the ocean.

Sedna sank to the bottom of the sea and became the Mother of the Deep.

Her feelings were enormous. Rage at her father. Grief for her lost fingers. Pain beyond naming.

But she didn't try to stop feeling.

She sat at the bottom of the ocean and WATCHED her feelings move through her like currents. The anger came — she let it pass. The grief came — she let it stay as long as it needed. The pain came — she breathed into it.

She didn't become her anger. She wasn't destroyed by her grief. She watched them the way the ocean watches its own waves.

'I am angry' became 'I notice anger.'
'I am sad' became 'Sadness is here.'
'I am broken' became 'Pain is passing through.'

The feelings were still enormous. She didn't make them smaller. She made HERSELF bigger — big enough to hold them. As big as the sea.

The hunters above learned: when the sea is angry, it's because Sedna's hair is tangled and no one has combed it. When you tend to her feelings — acknowledge them, comb them gently — the sea calms.

Feelings don't need to be fixed. They need to be witnessed.

## The Song

I am not my wave of feeling —
I'm the ocean, deep and healing.
Watch it come and watch it go —
I am bigger than I know.

## The Skill

This story teaches MINDFULNESS OF CURRENT EMOTIONS — observing your feelings without trying to change them, push them away, or become them. Sedna didn't fight her feelings. She became big enough to hold them. You are the ocean, not the wave.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: MINDFULNESS OF CURRENT EMOTIONS (Emotion Regulation). This skill bridges mindfulness and emotion regulation. Instead of suppressing, acting on, or being overwhelmed by emotions, the child learns to observe them as temporary experiences: 'I notice sadness' instead of 'I AM sad.' This doesn't minimize the feeling — it creates space around it. Practice: When your child is feeling something big, try: 'What would it be like to just WATCH that feeling, like watching a wave? It's big. It's real. And it will pass through.'

## Try This

Ocean Breathing: When a big feeling comes, imagine you are the ocean and the feeling is one wave. Breathe in as the wave rises. Breathe out as the wave falls. The wave is real. The wave is big. But YOU are the whole ocean. Do this for five breaths and see if anything shifts.

## Reflection

When you feel angry or sad, do you feel like you ARE the feeling — or like the feeling is moving THROUGH you? What's the difference?
