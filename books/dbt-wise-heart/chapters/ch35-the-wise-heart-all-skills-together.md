## For Parents

DBT was developed by Marsha Linehan, who drew from Zen Buddhism, behavioral science, and her own lived experience of emotional suffering. The four modules are taught in order for a reason — each builds on the last. But in practice, children will need different skills at different moments. Let them guide you. The mantras ('The Songs') are designed to be singable, repeatable, and retrievable under stress — the Daniel Tiger pattern. Practice them until they become automatic. That's when they work.

## About

The Wise Heart holds all four chambers together: the Quiet Eye (mindfulness), the Dark Wood (distress tolerance), the Feeling Compass (emotion regulation), and the Talking Circle (interpersonal effectiveness). These aren't four separate skills — they're one skill seen from four directions. You need to SEE clearly before you can SURVIVE. You need to survive before you can UNDERSTAND your feelings. And you need to understand yourself before you can truly CONNECT with others.

## The Pattern

Mindfulness is the ground.
Distress Tolerance is the roots (holding on when the storm comes).
Emotion Regulation is the trunk (growing strong and flexible).
Interpersonal Effectiveness is the branches (reaching toward others).

The whole tree is the Wise Heart.
