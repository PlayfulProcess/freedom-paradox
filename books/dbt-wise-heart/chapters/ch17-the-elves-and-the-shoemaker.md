# The Elves and the Shoemaker

*Emotional-Regulation*

## The Story

The shoemaker was so poor he had only enough leather for one pair of shoes.

He cut the leather and laid it out. Too tired to sew, he went to bed.

In the morning — beautiful shoes! Perfectly made! Someone had finished them in the night.

He sold the shoes, bought more leather, laid it out. Again, the elves came. Again, beautiful shoes.

This went on. The shoemaker was saved!

But one night, his wife said: 'These little helpers — they come every night in the cold, with no shoes of their own. We should take care of THEM.'

So they made tiny clothes for the elves. Tiny coats. Tiny shoes.

And when the elves found the clothes, they danced with joy. They were warm! They were cared for!

They didn't come back to make shoes anymore. They didn't need to — the shoemaker was doing well now. And the elves were finally taking care of THEMSELVES.

Here's the secret: the elves are you. The little helpers inside you — your energy, your patience, your calm — they work hard every day. But if you don't feed them, rest them, and keep them warm? Eventually they can't show up.

Take care of the helpers.

## The Song

Sleep and eat and move and rest —
Take care of me to feel my best.

## The Skill

This story teaches PLEASE skills — taking care of your body so your emotions have a stable foundation. PhysicaL illness (treat it), Eating (balanced), Avoid mood-altering substances, Sleep, Exercise. Your inner helpers need care too.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: PLEASE (Emotion Regulation — reducing vulnerability). PLEASE stands for: treat PhysicaL illness, balanced Eating, Avoid mood-altering substances, Sleep, Exercise. When these basics slip, emotional vulnerability skyrockets. Practice: Instead of focusing on emotion directly, ask: 'Did you sleep enough? Did you eat? Did you move today?' Often the emotional crisis is a body crisis in disguise.

## Try This

The Elf Check: Every night before bed, check on your five elves: (1) Did I eat well today? (2) Did I drink enough water? (3) Did I move my body? (4) Am I going to bed on time? (5) Is my body feeling okay? Give each elf a little nod of thanks.

## Reflection

When you feel your worst, what's usually going on with your body? Are you tired? Hungry? Have you been sitting too long?
