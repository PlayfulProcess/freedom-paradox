# Thumbelina's Winter

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

Thumbelina was tiny — no bigger than your thumb.

When winter came, she was alone in the cold. No home. No food. No warmth.

She found a fieldmouse's door in the frozen ground.

'Please,' she said. 'May I have just one grain of barley? I'm so cold.'

The fieldmouse let her in. And inside — oh! It was warm. There was soft straw to lie on. There was barley to eat. There was the gentle sound of the fieldmouse humming.

Thumbelina wrapped herself in a leaf. She ate a tiny meal. She listened to the humming.

She was still lost. She was still far from home. Nothing was fixed.

But for this one moment, she was WARM. She was FED. She was SAFE.

And that was enough. Not forever — just for now.

Sometimes, when everything is hard, you don't need to solve the problem. You just need to wrap yourself in something soft, eat something warm, hear something gentle.

And let that be enough. For now.

## The Song

Something soft, something warm, something kind —
Soothe my body, soothe my mind.

## The Skill

This story teaches SELF-SOOTHE WITH THE FIVE SENSES — using touch, taste, smell, sight, and sound to comfort yourself when things are hard. You don't have to fix everything. Sometimes you just need something warm.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: SELF-SOOTHE (Distress Tolerance — TIPP/Crisis Survival). Self-soothing means using the five senses to calm the nervous system when distressed. It's not avoidance — it's giving your body what it needs to return to baseline. Practice: Help your child build a 'soothe kit' — something soft to touch, a favorite smell, a warm drink, a calming image, a soothing sound. When things get hard, the kit is ready.

## Try This

Build your Soothe Kit! Find one thing for each sense: something soft to hold (touch), something that smells nice (smell), a picture that makes you feel calm (sight), a favorite gentle song (hearing), and a warm drink or treat for hard days (taste).

## Reflection

Which of your five senses helps you feel calm the most? What's your 'fieldmouse door' — the place or thing that makes you feel safe when everything is cold?
