# The Salt Prince

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

A king asked his three daughters how much they loved him.

The eldest said: 'More than gold!'

The second said: 'More than jewels!'

The youngest said: 'More than salt.'

The king was FURIOUS. Salt? Just SALT? He banished her from the kingdom.

But he hadn't stopped. He hadn't paused. He hadn't asked himself: 'Wait — what does she actually MEAN?'

If he'd stopped — really stopped — he would have realized: salt is in every meal. Salt preserves food through winter. Salt is what you miss the moment it's gone. She was saying: I love you in every bite, in every ordinary day, in the thing you need most and notice least.

But the king reacted.

He didn't Stop. He didn't Take a step back. He didn't Observe what was happening inside him. He didn't Proceed mindfully.

So he lost the daughter who loved him most.

Later — when the food had no salt and the kingdom tasted like nothing — he understood.

But later is harder than stopping.

## The Song

STOP! Take a step back, look around —
Before you leap, stand on the ground.

## The Skill

This story teaches STOP — a four-step skill for not reacting on impulse. Stop what you're doing. Take a step back. Observe what's happening (inside and outside). Proceed mindfully. The king skipped all four steps, and lost everything.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: STOP (Distress Tolerance — Crisis Survival). S = Stop (freeze, don't act). T = Take a step back (physically or mentally step away). O = Observe (what am I feeling? what's actually happening?). P = Proceed mindfully (choose a response instead of reacting). Practice: When your child is about to react impulsively, say 'STOP — salt prince!' It becomes a playful code word for 'let's pause before we react.'

## Try This

The Salt Prince Pause: When you feel a big reaction coming, literally freeze like a statue for 5 seconds. Then take one step backward. Then ask yourself: 'What am I actually feeling?' Then decide what to do. The 5-second freeze is the hardest part — and the most important.

## Reflection

Can you think of a time you reacted before you understood what someone really meant? What might have happened if you'd paused?
