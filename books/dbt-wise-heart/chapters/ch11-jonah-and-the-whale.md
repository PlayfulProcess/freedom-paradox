# Jonah and the Whale

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

God told Jonah to go to the great city of Nineveh.

Jonah didn't want to. He was afraid. So he ran the other way. He got on a boat sailing as far from Nineveh as possible.

But the sea rose up. A great storm came. The sailors were terrified.

Jonah knew: this storm was his running catching up with him.

'Throw me in the sea,' he told the sailors. 'The storm will stop.'

They threw him in. The storm stopped.

And a great whale swallowed Jonah whole.

Inside the whale it was dark. And wet. And Jonah couldn't run anymore.

So he did the only thing he could: he BREATHED. In that dark belly, with nowhere to go, Jonah breathed. And prayed. And waited.

Three days.

His body was cold — and the cold slowed him down.
His breath was all he had — and the breath calmed him.
His muscles had nothing to fight — so they relaxed.

And after three days, the whale brought him to shore.

Jonah walked to Nineveh.

Not because the fear was gone. Because his body had calmed enough to carry the fear and walk anyway.

## The Song

Cold water, deep breath, count to four —
My body calms, I can do more.

## The Skill

This story teaches TIPP — using your body to calm your brain in a crisis. Temperature (cold calms you down), Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation. When your feelings are a storm, change your body first.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: TIPP (Distress Tolerance — Crisis Survival). TIPP changes body chemistry fast: Temperature (cold water on face triggers dive reflex, slowing heart rate), Intense exercise (burns adrenaline), Paced breathing (long exhale activates parasympathetic), Paired muscle relaxation (tense/release). These are physiological interventions — they work even when you can't think straight. Practice: Splash cold water on your wrists. Breathe in for 4, out for 8. These work for adults too.

## Try This

When you feel really upset: (1) Hold something cold — an ice cube, cold water on your face, a frozen orange. (2) Breathe in slowly for 4 counts, out for 8 counts. (3) Run in place for 30 seconds. Notice how your body feels different after.

## Reflection

When you're inside your own 'whale' — a really hard moment — what helps your body calm down? Have you noticed that calming your body helps calm your mind?
