# Odysseus and the Sirens

*Distress-Tolerance*

## The Story

Odysseus knew the Sirens were coming. Their song was so beautiful that every sailor who heard it steered toward the rocks and drowned.

Odysseus wanted to hear the song. But he also wanted to live.

So he thought it through BEFORE the crisis hit.

'Here is my plan,' he told his crew. 'Fill your ears with beeswax so you can't hear. Tie me to the mast. No matter what I say, no matter how I beg — do NOT untie me.'

The crew blocked their ears. They tied Odysseus to the mast.

Then the Sirens sang.

The song was more beautiful than anything Odysseus had ever heard. He screamed. He begged. He WEPT for his crew to untie him.

But the ropes held. And the wax held. And the ship sailed past.

Odysseus survived because he made the decision BEFORE the crisis. When you're calm, you can think. When the Sirens are singing, you can't.

That's why you make the plan when the sea is still.

## The Song

Think it through while the sea is still —
The calm-me plan needs a calm-me will.

## The Skill

This story teaches PROS AND CONS — thinking through decisions BEFORE you're in crisis. When you're calm, list what happens if you act on the urge vs. if you resist. Odysseus tied himself up BEFORE the singing started.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: PROS AND CONS (Distress Tolerance — Crisis Survival). Making a pros/cons list while calm — comparing acting on an urge vs. resisting it — is a powerful tool because it pre-commits to a decision. Practice: Help your child make a 'Siren Plan' for their hardest moments (bedtime meltdowns, sibling conflicts, homework frustration). Write it together while calm. Post it where they can see it. When the sirens sing, the plan is already there.

## Try This

Make your Siren Plan! Think of a time you always lose your cool. Now, while you're calm, write down: (1) What the 'siren song' feels like (the urge). (2) What happens if you follow it. (3) What happens if you don't. (4) What will help you hold on (your 'ropes').

## Reflection

What's your siren song — the urge that's hardest to resist? What could you set up NOW, while you're calm, to help your future self?
