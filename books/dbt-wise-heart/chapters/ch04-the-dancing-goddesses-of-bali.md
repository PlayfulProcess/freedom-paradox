# The Dancing Goddesses of Bali

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

In Bali, there is a dance that has been danced for a thousand years.

The dancers don't think about the dance. They don't watch themselves from outside. They don't wonder if they're doing it right.

They BECOME the dance.

Their hands move like flowers opening. Their eyes follow stories only their bodies know. The music plays and they are inside it — not performing, not watching, not judging.

Just dancing.

A visitor once asked a dancer: 'How do you remember all the steps?'

The dancer laughed. 'I don't remember them. My body does. When I think about the steps, I stumble. When I let go and dance, the steps are already there.'

This is what it feels like to be FULLY IN something — not watching yourself do it, not worrying about doing it wrong, just doing it with your whole self.

You know this feeling. When you're playing and you forget about time. When you're drawing and your hand just moves. When you're laughing so hard you can't think.

That's it. That's the dance.

## The Song

Don't watch yourself — just jump right in!
Let your whole self play and spin.

## The Skill

This story teaches PARTICIPATE — throwing yourself fully into what you're doing without watching yourself from outside. When you're fully in the game, the drawing, the song — that's participating.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: PARTICIPATE (Core Mindfulness — 'What' Skills). Participating means entering fully into an activity without self-consciousness. It's the opposite of dissociation or anxious self-monitoring. Children do this naturally in play — the skill is recognizing and protecting that state. Practice: Notice when your child is in flow (lost in play, drawing, building). Don't interrupt. After, you can say: 'You were really IN that. That's a superpower called participating.'

## Try This

Try drawing with your eyes closed for one minute. Don't judge the picture — just feel the crayon move. That feeling of not-watching-yourself? That's participating.

## Reflection

When was the last time you were so INTO something that you forgot about time? What were you doing?
