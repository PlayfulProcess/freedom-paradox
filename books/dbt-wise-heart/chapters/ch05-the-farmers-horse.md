# The Farmer's Horse

*Mindfulness*

## The Story

A farmer's horse ran away.

'How terrible!' said the neighbors.

The farmer said: 'Maybe.'

The next day the horse came back, bringing three wild horses.

'How wonderful!' said the neighbors.

'Maybe,' said the farmer.

The farmer's son tried to ride one of the wild horses and broke his leg.

'How terrible!' said the neighbors.

'Maybe,' said the farmer.

The next week, soldiers came to take all the young men to war. But the farmer's son couldn't go because of his broken leg.

'How wonderful!' said the neighbors.

'Maybe,' said the farmer.

The farmer wasn't cold or unkind. The farmer just knew something: when you rush to label things GOOD or BAD, you miss the whole story. Life is bigger than your labels.

The neighbors kept judging.
The farmer kept watching.

And life kept being bigger than either of them expected.

## The Song

Good or bad? I just don't know —
Let me watch and let it flow.

## The Skill

This story teaches NON-JUDGMENTAL STANCE — seeing things as they are, without rushing to label them good or bad. The farmer doesn't judge — he just watches. And because he watches, he sees more.

## For Parents

DBT Skill: NON-JUDGMENTALLY (Core Mindfulness — 'How' Skills). Non-judgmental means observing without evaluating. Not 'this is bad' or 'this is good' but 'this is what happened.' This doesn't mean having no values — it means separating observation from evaluation. Practice: When something happens (spilled milk, a grade, a friend situation), try saying 'That happened' before saying whether it's good or bad. Model curiosity instead of judgment.

## Try This

The Maybe Game: Next time something happens that seems good or bad, say 'Maybe!' instead. Wait and see what happens next. Keep a 'Maybe Journal' for a week.

## Reflection

Can you think of a time something seemed really bad but turned out to be okay — or even good? What about the reverse?
