# The Wise Heart: DBT Skills Through World Myths

## Stories That Teach Children to Feel

**By [PlayfulProcess](https://playfulprocess.com) with Claude**

---

**Working thesis:** Every child needs emotional skills, and the world's oldest stories teach them better than lectures. This book teaches all 29 core DBT skills through cross-cultural myths and folktales for ages 4-8.

**Structure:** 4 modules, 29 story-skill pairs + 5 meta items:

### Module 1: The Quiet Eye (Mindfulness) — 7 skills
Wise Mind, Observe, Describe, Participate, Non-Judgmentally, One-Mindfully, Effectively

### Module 2: The Dark Wood (Distress Tolerance) — 10 skills
Radical Acceptance, Turning the Mind, Self-Soothe, TIPP, Pros/Cons, Willingness, STOP, ACCEPTS, IMPROVE, Half-Smile

### Module 3: The Feeling Compass (Emotion Regulation) — 8 skills
Check Facts, Opposite Action, Build Mastery, PLEASE, Cope Ahead, Accumulate Positives, Mindfulness of Emotions, Problem-Solving

### Module 4: The Talking Circle (Interpersonal Effectiveness) — 5 skills
DEAR MAN, GIVE, FAST, Validation, Middle Path

**Design principles:**
- No shame — characters feel big feelings
- The myth does the teaching — don't explain, let the story land
- Mantras must be singable, repeatable, retrievable under stress
- Every tradition is honored — no tradition is decoration

**Each story includes:** The Story, The Song (2-line mantra), The Skill, For Parents (clinical context), Try This (exercise), Reflection (for age 8+)

**Traditions drawn from:** Indian, German (Grimm), Balinese, Chinese, Japanese (Zen), West African (Ashanti), Sumerian, Greek, Hebrew Bible, Danish (Andersen), Czech, Persian/Arabic, Russian, Inuit, European oral

**Connection to other books:** Proof of concept #2 after Campfire Stories. If Campfire Stories proved stories carry adaptive wisdom, Wise Heart proves they can teach specific clinical skills. Extends Working Architecture's practices into the child's own emotional toolkit. Uses the Linehan filter throughout — because it's Linehan's own framework.

**Source material:** `research/grammar.json` (original recursive.eco grammar)
