# The Intimate Scale: Re-Pairing with Each Other

*Emergence*

## About

The intimate scale is where repair begins — between two nervous systems learning to find each other in the dark. Gottman showed us that most problems never get solved. Bowlby showed us why it hurts so much. Sue Johnson showed us the exit: not solving, but reaching. The AA tradition showed us that facing what we've done is its own kind of medicine. And Linehan showed us how to stay alive while all of this is happening.

This isn't the 'small' scale. There's nothing small about saying 'I need you' to someone who might not say it back. The intimate is the training ground for everything that follows. If you can't repair with one person, you can't repair with a culture. If you can't sit with your own fire, you can't sit with the world's.

The tarot arc: The Lovers (choosing to stay) → The Moon (the unconscious cry) → The Star (naked vulnerability) → Judgement (facing what you've done) → Temperance (the balance that holds you through it all).

## For Readers

Start here if you're in a relationship that feels stuck. Start here if you're in recovery. Start here if you're a therapist. Start here if you're a human being who has ever needed someone and didn't know how to say it.

The practices in these chapters are the most personal: repair attempts with your partner, naming the cry beneath your fights, writing amends letters, splashing cold water on your face when emotions flood you. These aren't metaphors. These are Tuesday night at 11pm.

Read Chapter 1 (Gottman) and Chapter 3 (EFT) together — they're the diagnosis and the treatment. Read Chapter 4 (AA) if you've harmed someone and don't know how to begin. Read Chapter 5 (Linehan) if you're overwhelmed and need to survive before you can repair.

## Recursive Practice

Pull three cards from any tarot deck. Lay them in a line. Read them as: The Problem That Won't Be Solved (left) → The Cry Beneath (center) → The Reach (right). This is your intimate-scale reading. What's the perpetual problem? What's the attachment cry? What vulnerability request wants to emerge?
