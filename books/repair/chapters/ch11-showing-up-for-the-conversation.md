# Showing Up for the Conversation

*Major*

## Research

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy's core mechanism is cognitive restructuring: you notice an automatic thought ('nobody cares about me'), examine the evidence, and generate alternative perspectives. Tarot does this by accident. You draw The Emperor when you're feeling powerless and suddenly you're considering: what if I have more authority here than I think? The card doesn't predict. It reframes.

James Pennebaker's expressive writing research (1997) showed that writing about emotional experiences for 15-20 minutes a day significantly improves immune function, reduces doctor visits, and decreases anxiety and depression. The mechanism: coherence. When you write about chaos, you make it narratable. Tarot + journaling is Pennebaker on steroids — the card gives you an image, a metaphor, a starting point that your conscious mind didn't choose. You're writing from a place deeper than your usual story.

Art therapy research (Malchiodi, 2011) confirms: creating images activates different neural pathways than verbal processing. Drawing a card and then DRAWING your response to it — or designing your own card — engages the right hemisphere, the body, the pre-verbal. This is why children heal through art before they heal through talk. The image holds what words can't.

Jung spent years on his Red Book — a journal of active imagination, filled with painted images and dialogues with archetypal figures. He didn't publish it in his lifetime. It was the practice, not the product. Every tarot pull is a micro Red Book: an encounter with an image that speaks from the unconscious.

## Clinical

Let's be precise about the clinical mechanisms. Tarot, when used reflectively, activates at least five therapeutic modalities simultaneously:

1. CBT (COGNITIVE RESTRUCTURING): The card presents a perspective you didn't choose. Your automatic thought says 'I'm stuck.' The card says 'Wheel of Fortune.' Now you're considering: what if things are already turning? Not because the card is magic — because your brain can't help but make meaning. The reframe happens whether or not you believe in divination.

2. DEPTH PSYCHOLOGY (ARCHETYPAL DIALOGUE): Every Major Arcana card is an archetype. When you draw The Hermit, you're not getting advice from a picture — you're entering a dialogue with the part of you that knows how to withdraw wisely. Jung called this 'active imagination.' The card is a face for something inside you that doesn't have words.

3. NARRATIVE THERAPY (RE-AUTHORING): Michael White and David Epston showed that people suffer from 'dominant stories' — thin descriptions of who they are. Tarot generates alternative stories. The person who 'always fails' draws The Star and has to sit with an image of hope. The image doesn't override the story. It cracks it open, just enough.

4. ART THERAPY (IMAGE AS CONTAINER): When you design your own card — draw it, collage it, write on it — you externalize an internal state. The thing that was overwhelming becomes an object. You can look at it. Hold it. Put it down. Pick it up another day. The card becomes what Winnicott called a 'transitional object' — something between inner and outer, between fantasy and reality.

5. WHAT WE MIGHT CALL 'AKOMOLAFEAN' PRACTICE (INVITING THE CRACK): This is the new one. Bayo Akomolafe's philosophy suggests that the most important things happen in the cracks — the places where our certainty breaks. A tarot pull is a deliberate crack in your plan. You had a question. You knew what you thought the answer was. Then you drew Death. Now you don't know. That not-knowing IS the therapeutic intervention. Not the answer the card gives — the fact that your certainty got interrupted. The crack is where the new comes through.

None of these require belief in prediction. None require the supernatural. They require showing up — which is the same thing every therapy requires.

## Spiritual

The Oracle at Delphi didn't serve the individual. The Pythia — the priestess — inhaled vapors, entered trance, and spoke in riddles. The priests interpreted. The seekers applied. But the consultation wasn't self-help. It was civic. Kings consulted before wars. Cities consulted before founding colonies. The oracle was infrastructure — relational infrastructure between humans and the more-than-human.

In Yoruba tradition, Ifá divination is community medicine. The babalawo (diviner) doesn't read for personal curiosity. Ifá is consulted at births, marriages, crises, and collective decisions. The 256 Odù (configurations) contain the accumulated wisdom of ancestors. When you consult Ifá, you're not asking 'what should I do?' You're asking: 'what do the ancestors, the land, and the orixás see that I can't?'

The I Ching operates the same way: the hexagram isn't YOUR answer. It's the situation's answer. The I Ching reads the pattern of the moment — what's arising, what's declining, what's about to turn. You're consulting the intelligence of change itself.

Here's the radical proposal: what if oracular practice is how life talks back?

Parker Palmer says 'let your life speak.' But how? Life doesn't speak English. It doesn't speak Portuguese. It speaks in synchronicities, symptoms, dreams, seasons, the fox that crosses your path, the card that shows up three times in a row. It speaks in whatever language you've agreed to listen in.

That's the key word: agreed. Oracular practice is an agreement. You agree to show up. You agree to the language (tarot, I Ching, runes, bones, cards you drew yourself). You agree to listen even when the answer makes no sense. Life agrees to speak. The relationship forms through consistency — the same way any relationship forms.

Astrology as relational animism: not 'Saturn is going to ruin your March.' Rather: 'Saturn and I have a relationship. When Saturn transits my Moon, old grief surfaces. I can prepare for that the way I'd prepare for a difficult conversation with a family member — because Saturn IS a family member in this cosmology.'

Prayer, revisited: 'Our Father' or 'Great Mother' or 'Ancestors' or 'Dear Unknown' — prayer is an oracular practice when you expect an answer. Not a voice from the sky. An answer in the language you've agreed to listen in. The card you pull after praying. The dream that night. The stranger who says exactly what you needed to hear. These might be coincidence. They might be the conversation working.



## Practices

TAROT AS CBT PRACTICE:
When you notice an automatic negative thought (I'm failing, nobody cares, nothing will change), draw a single card. Don't ask 'what should I do?' Ask: 'what perspective am I not seeing?' The card isn't an answer. It's a cognitive interruption. A reframe you didn't generate yourself. Write for 5 minutes about how the card relates to your situation. That's Pennebaker + CBT in one practice.

TAROT AS DEPTH PSYCHOLOGY:
Draw a card. Look at the figure in the image. Talk to them. Out loud or in writing. 'Hermit, what are you doing alone on that mountain? What can you see from up there that I can't see from down here?' Let the figure answer. This is active imagination. It sounds silly. It works because the silliness bypasses the ego's defenses.

TAROT AS AKOMOLAFEAN PRACTICE:
Before you draw, write down what you THINK is going to happen. Your plan. Your prediction. Your certainty. Then draw. Let the card crack your certainty. Write: 'The card I drew doesn't match my plan. What is the crack showing me? What wants to come through that my plan was blocking?'

DESIGN YOUR OWN CARD:
Take a blank card (or paper, or your screen). Draw, collage, or design the card that represents where you are RIGHT NOW. Not who you want to be. Where you are. Give it a name. Give it a number. What suit is it? What element? Now: would you want to draw this card? What would it mean if someone else drew it? You've just made your inner state into an object. That's art therapy.

I CHING AS TERRITORIAL PRACTICE:
Throw the I Ching not for yourself but for your place. Ask: 'What is the land I live on going through right now?' Read the hexagram as the earth's situation, not yours. You might be surprised how much sense it makes — because you're part of the land's story whether you know it or not.

SPREAD: THE CONVERSATION WITH LIFE
- Card 1: What is life saying to me right now?
- Card 2: What am I saying back? (What is my habitual response?)
- Card 3: What language are we not sharing? (Where is the communication gap?)
- Card 4: What practice would help us understand each other?
- Card 5 (The Crack): What am I not ready to hear?

ASTROLOGY AS RELATIONAL CHECK-IN:
Once a month, look at the current transits. Not to predict. To check in. 'How is Mars doing? What's Venus up to? Where is Saturn?' Talk to them like relatives. 'Saturn, you're in my 7th house. I know you're going to make my relationships feel heavy. I'm here. I'm showing up. What are you teaching me?' This is relational animism. The planets aren't forces acting ON you. They're beings you're IN RELATIONSHIP WITH. The relationship requires showing up.

## Rituals

THE DAILY PULL:
Every morning, draw one card. Write three sentences about it. Not interpretation — conversation. 'Good morning, Seven of Swords. What are you doing here? What am I stealing from myself today?' Over time, this becomes a relationship with a symbolic language. Like learning any language: you show up daily. You fumble. Slowly, fluency comes.

THE WEEKLY JOURNAL SPREAD:
Once a week, do a three-card pull: Past Week / Present Moment / Coming Week. Write for 10 minutes. Not 'what the cards mean' — what they make you FEEL. What images arise. What memories. What fears. What hopes. Date each entry. After three months, read them all in sequence. You'll see your own patterns more clearly than any therapist could show you.

THE COMMUNITY READING:
Gather friends. Not for party tricks. For collective inquiry. Choose a question that matters to the group: 'What does our community need right now?' Each person draws one card. Go around the circle. Each person speaks their card's message as if the card is speaking through them. Nobody interprets anyone else's card. The reading is the tapestry of all the voices together. This is the oracular tradition restored: not individual fortune-telling but collective listening.

THE NEW CARD CEREMONY:
When something big happens in your life — a birth, a death, a move, a heartbreak, a vocation heard — create a card for it. Draw it, name it, place it in your deck. Now your deck contains your life. The generic becomes personal. The archetypal meets the biographical. Your deck is your myth.

## Kids Version

Do you know how you talk to your dog or your cat or your goldfish? You say 'good morning!' and they don't say 'good morning' back — but something happens, right? The dog wags its tail. The cat blinks slowly. The goldfish... well, the goldfish just swims, but you FEEL like it heard you.

That's what it's like to talk to life. Life doesn't speak words. It speaks in other ways: a cool breeze when you're hot. A funny coincidence. A picture that makes you feel something you can't explain.

Some people use special cards to have this conversation. The cards have beautiful pictures on them — a tower, a star, the moon, a person dancing in a circle. You ask a question, mix up the cards, and pick one. The picture doesn't TELL you the answer. It helps you THINK about the answer in a new way.

It's like when you're stuck on a math problem and your teacher says 'try drawing a picture.' The picture doesn't solve the math. But somehow, after drawing it, YOUR brain solves it.

You can even make your own cards! Draw pictures of things that matter to you. Your family. Your favorite place. Your biggest fear. Your silliest dream. Put them in a pile. Pick one when you need a new idea. That's your own oracle. Nobody else's cards will work the same as yours.

And here's the coolest part: you're not the only one having this conversation. Everyone is. Your mom, your teacher, the person on the bus. The wind is talking to ALL of us. We just need to agree on a language. Your cards. My cards. Someone else's silence. It's all conversation.

## Sci-Fi

THE LANGUAGE PROBLEM (2108)

The territorial consciousness hypothesis was confirmed in 2087: ideas don't originate in individual brains. They emerge in fields — geographic, ecological, relational fields. This explained why calculus was invented simultaneously by Newton and Leibniz. Why Darwin and Wallace arrived at natural selection at the same time. Why the same dreams appear in different cultures. The field thinks. Humans receive.

But receiving requires a receiver. A tuning fork. A language.

The communities in the rewilding zones had rediscovered this. Some used cards. Some used bones. Some used the I Ching. Some sat in silence and let the land speak through their bodies. The method didn't matter. The consistency did. You had to show up, in the same language, repeatedly, until the signal clarified — the way a radio tunes to a frequency through patient adjustment.

The AI in the rewilding zones was useful here: it could detect pattern correlations between readings and ecological events. When three communities in different watersheds all drew Tower cards in the same week, the AI flagged it: 'Tectonic activity detected in regional substrate. The readings preceded seismograph data by 72 hours.'

Nobody called it prediction. They called it conversation.

In Antarctica, the dome-dwellers had no oracular practice. Every decision was data-driven. Optimal. Rational. The AI made all significant choices based on probability models. There was no mystery. No crack. No question that didn't have a computable answer.

The dome's chief psychologist — one of the last to hold that role before it was automated — wrote in her final journal entry: 'We have eliminated uncertainty. We have also eliminated meaning. I believe these are the same thing.'

Bayo on the oracular: 'The trickster doesn't give you answers. The trickster rearranges the furniture of your questions. You walk into a room you thought you knew and everything is in a different place. Now you have to navigate by feel, not by map. That's not confusion — that's aliveness.'

The rewilding communities understood: the oracle is not a tool for the individual. It's an interface between the human and the more-than-human. When you draw a card, you're not reading YOUR future. You're reading the field's present. You're a sensor — a nerve ending of the territory. The personal reading and the territorial reading are the same reading, just as your pain and your ecosystem's pain are the same pain, arriving through different nerves.

This is why coregulation works. This is why ideas are born in different geographies at the same time. This is why a mother wakes before the baby cries. We are not separate agents consulting separate oracles. We are the oracle, reading itself.

## Cracks

AGNOSTOS THEOS:
- We framed tarot as 'not requiring belief in prediction.' But some practitioners DO believe in prediction. Dismissing that belief to make tarot palatable to secular-clinical audiences is its own kind of extraction — taking the practice and leaving the cosmology.
- The 'territorial consciousness hypothesis' in the Sci-Fi section is speculative. There's no peer-reviewed evidence that ideas emerge from geographic fields. The simultaneous invention argument (Newton/Leibniz) has simpler explanations: shared knowledge base, shared problems, shared tools. Maybe the oracular isn't territorial. Maybe it's just personal. That would still be enough.
- 'Life speaks in synchronicities' — or life doesn't speak at all, and we're pattern-matching animals seeing meaning in noise. Confirmation bias IS the mechanism. The question is: does it matter? If pulling The Star makes you consider hope, does it matter whether 'life' sent the card or your shuffle did?
- We said 'show up for the conversation the way you'd show up for any relationship.' But oracular relationships are asymmetric. You ask, you interpret, you decide what the answer means. That's not a conversation — that's a Rorschach test. (Unless it isn't. Unless the card really does come alive in the dialogue. We don't know. The Unknown God.)
- The biggest crack: recommending oracular practice as therapy risks enabling avoidance. 'I'll draw a card instead of calling my therapist.' 'The I Ching said to wait, so I won't address this crisis.' Oracular practice should SUPPLEMENT clinical work, not replace it. The cards are companions, not clinicians.
- Pamela Colman Smith died in poverty. Her images are everywhere. This chapter recommends using them. The crack: can you build a therapeutic practice on an artist's unrecompensed labor and call it healing? (You can. But name it. Name the crack.)
- We might be vehicles for ideas, as the Sci-Fi section suggests. But vehicles don't have agency. If the field thinks through us, do we choose? The oracular practice assumes both: you're receiving AND you're interpreting. The tension between vehicle and agent is unresolvable. That's the crack. It's also the practice.
