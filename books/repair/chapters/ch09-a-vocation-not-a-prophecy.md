# A Vocation, Not a Prophecy

*Major*

## Research

Parker Palmer, in 'Let Your Life Speak' (2000), redefines vocation: it's not what you decide to do with your life. It's what life decides to do with you. 'Vocation does not come from a voice out there calling me to become something I am not. It comes from a voice in here calling me to be the person I already am.'

This maps onto what psychologists call 'self-determination theory' (Deci & Ryan, 1985): intrinsic motivation — doing something because it matters, not because it pays — is the strongest predictor of sustained engagement and wellbeing.

Bayo Akomolafe: 'The times are urgent; let us slow down.' Not passivity — a different kind of action. The action of not-knowing. Of not-fixing. Of staying with the trouble (Donna Haraway's phrase). The West says: see a problem, solve it. Bayo says: the urgency to solve might be the problem.

Andreotti on 'hospicing modernity': not killing it, not saving it. Sitting with it as it transforms. Like a hospice worker — present, caring, not pretending the patient will recover, not hastening death. Just: here. With you. In this.

## Clinical

Strength (XI) in tarot: a woman gently opening a lion's mouth. Not domination — relationship. The strength to be gentle with something that could destroy you.

In clinical terms: ego strength isn't the ability to control your impulses. It's the ability to be present with them without being consumed. Linehan's radical acceptance meets Palmer's vocation: I accept what I am AND I hear what I'm called to become.

The clinical frame for vocation: what if the question isn't 'what should I do with my life?' but 'what is my life already doing?' Parker Palmer had a breakdown. Linehan was institutionalized. Bayo grew up in Nigeria and followed a path that didn't make career sense. Their vocations weren't chosen — they were heard.

Think of yourself as a bus (a silly but useful image). You're the bus. On the bus are all your passengers: your anxiety, your creativity, your mother's voice, your childhood wounds, your weird obsession with birds, your longing for justice. You don't get to kick passengers off. You drive the bus. Vocation is: which direction does this particular busload of passengers need to go?

ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) uses exactly this metaphor. Values aren't goals. They're directions. You never arrive at 'compassion.' You drive toward it. Every day. With all your weird passengers.

## Spiritual

Strength is the card of the vow that doesn't come from willpower but from love. The woman doesn't wrestle the lion. She opens its mouth with tenderness. The lion doesn't fight her because — why would you fight someone who touches you like that?

Vocation in monastic traditions: the Benedictine vow of 'stability' — you stay. Not because it's easy. Because staying IS the practice. You don't leave when the community annoys you. You don't leave when God is silent. You stay.

Bayo Akomolafe on the sacred: 'The sacred is not the exceptional. It is the crack in the ordinary.' The burning bush wasn't special because it was a bush or because it burned. It was special because Moses stopped. He turned aside to look. Vocation starts with: I turned aside.

Prayer as vocation: not asking God for instructions. Listening. The Quaker practice of 'expectant waiting' — sitting in silence until something rises. Not your plan. Not your ambition. Something deeper. The life that wants to live through you.

Astrology as vocation: your natal chart isn't a map of what will happen. It's a map of what you're carrying. Saturn in your chart isn't a curse — it's your assignment. The planet you struggle with most is the teacher you didn't choose. What if vocation is: saying yes to the assignment you didn't choose?



## Practices

PARKER PALMER'S PRACTICE — Clearness Committee:
Gather 4-6 trusted people. One person sits in the center with a question about their life direction. The others ask ONLY open, honest questions — no advice, no suggestions, no 'have you tried...?' Only: 'What did you feel when ___?' 'What are you most afraid of?' The person in the center hears their own truth reflected back. 90 minutes. Life-changing.

RECURSIVE.ECO PRACTICE:
Draw Strength (XI). Sit with the image. Ask: 'What is the lion in my life right now? Am I wrestling it or opening its mouth with gentleness? What would gentleness look like here?'

I CHING: Hexagram 1 — 乾 (The Creative, Heaven). Pure yang. The creative force. But the commentary says: 'The dragon appearing in the field — it is good to see the great person.' The creative doesn't act alone. It needs the field (Hexagram 2, The Receptive). Vocation is creative force meeting receptive ground. You plant; the earth does the rest.

BODY PRACTICE:
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Knees slightly bent. Hands at your sides. Close your eyes. Ask your body: 'What direction do you want to face?' Don't decide with your mind. Let your body turn. This is silly. Try it anyway. The body knows things the mind argues about.

## Rituals

THE VOCATION QUESTION:
Once a year (birthday, solstice, new year — pick one), sit in silence for 20 minutes and then write the answer to one question: 'What is my life asking of me right now?' Not what you want. Not what you should. What is being asked. Keep these answers. Read them across years. Your vocation reveals itself in the pattern.

THE HONOR WALK:
This is the 'I prefer to die with honor' practice. Walk for 30 minutes — no phone, no destination. As you walk, say silently: 'I am walking in the direction of what I believe, even if I can't see where it goes.' This isn't productive. This isn't optimized. This is a body moving in alignment with something it can't name. That's enough. That's vocation.

## Kids Version

Have you ever done something not because someone told you to, but because it just felt RIGHT? Like picking up a worm from the sidewalk and putting it in the grass. Nobody asked you to. You just... had to.

That feeling — the 'I just have to' feeling — grown-ups call it a 'calling.' Not like a phone call. More like... the way a flower turns toward the sun. The flower doesn't DECIDE to turn. It just does. Because that's what flowers do.

Some people feel called to be doctors. Some feel called to make music. Some feel called to be really really good at being kind. Some feel called to pick up worms.

The trick is: you don't pick your calling. Your calling picks you. Your job is to listen.

How do you listen? Get quiet. Get still. Pay attention to the things that make you feel MORE like yourself, not less. That's the direction.

(And if you don't hear anything yet — that's okay. You're still growing. Even flowers take time to learn which way the sun is.)

## Sci-Fi

THE LISTENERS (2121)

After the Great Dysregulation and the Turning, two approaches emerged.

The Fixers (mostly in the domes) said: 'We have the technology. We can geo-engineer the atmosphere, desalinate the oceans, synthesize food, colonize Mars. The problem is technical. The solution is technical.'

The Listeners (mostly in the rewilding zones) said: 'We don't know what to do. So we're going to get quiet and listen.'

The Fixers built. Brilliantly. Each solution generated three new problems, which they solved, generating nine newer problems. They called this 'innovation.'

The Listeners gardened. Sang. Held circles. Practiced. Slowly, patterns emerged — not solutions, but directions. Like a body turning toward the sun.

Bayo would say: the Fixers were heroic. The Listeners were foolish. The trickster lives in the gap between heroism and foolishness, and the future grows there.

The AI in the domes optimized for fixing. The AI in the rewilding zones optimized for listening. The dome AI produced reports. The rewilding AI produced silence — strategic pauses in the noise that let humans hear each other. The dome called this 'inefficiency.' The rewilding zones called it 'Monday.'

Neither won. That's the point. Vocation isn't about winning. It's about: which direction does your particular busload of passengers need to go? The dome passengers needed to build. The rewilding passengers needed to listen. Both are true. Both are needed. The dialectic doesn't resolve.

## Open Source

PUBLIC DOMAIN:
- Rainer Maria Rilke, 'Letters to a Young Poet' (1929): 'Be patient toward all that is unsolved in your heart and try to love the questions themselves.' The original vocation text.
- Henry David Thoreau, 'Walden' (1854): 'I went to the woods because I wished to live deliberately.' Not to escape — to listen. Thoreau's year was a Clearness Committee with the trees.
- Lao Tzu, 'Tao Te Ching' (6th century BCE): 'The Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao.' Vocation can't be spoken — only lived. The Tao as the direction the bus faces when the driver stops steering.
- Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 3: 'It is better to do your own dharma imperfectly than another's dharma perfectly.' Your calling, however clumsy, is yours.

MUSEUM:
- Hildegard of Bingen, illuminated manuscripts (12th century): A mystic, composer, scientist, and healer who said: 'I am the breeze that nurtures all things green.' Her vocation was everything. She didn't choose — she overflowed.
- Zen calligraphy (ensō circle): One brushstroke. Complete. Imperfect. The practice IS the product.

## Cracks

AGNOSTOS THEOS:
- 'Let your life speak' assumes your life is free enough to speak. For people in survival mode — refugees, the homeless, the trafficked — vocation is a luxury concept. The cruelest thing you can say to someone who can't eat is 'follow your calling.'
- Palmer, Akomolafe, and Haraway are all academics. They slow down from positions of security. What does 'slow down' mean to a factory worker? A gig driver? The urgency isn't philosophical for everyone.
- We said 'not prophecy — vocation.' But isn't vocation its own kind of prediction? 'This is what I'm called to do' assumes a future in which doing it matters. If the future collapses, does vocation hold? (Maybe yes. The vocation isn't in the result. It's in the direction. You walk toward what you believe even if you never arrive.)
- The Lion and the Woman (Strength XI) is traditionally read as feminine gentleness taming masculine wildness. That's a gendered reading that might reinforce the idea that women's job is to gentle-fy men. The card might also mean: the lion IS you. The woman IS you. Both. The dialectic is internal.
- Bayo says 'slow down.' But there's a fire. People are burning right now. Slowing down when others are on fire is either wisdom or privilege. Probably both.
