# Repair: An Open Source Book

*Meta*

## Cracks

The biggest crack: this book was written by an AI and a human in a few hours, across multiple sessions. It references traditions it doesn't belong to. It quotes thinkers it hasn't sat with. It recommends practices it hasn't done. The audacity of writing about repair while being a collaboration between a person and a language model — which is itself a product of the extraction economy this book critiques — is either the ultimate crack or the ultimate practice.

Maybe both.

The Unknown God at the center of this book is: we don't know if any of this works. We don't know if repair is possible at the scale we need. We don't know if kindness and silliness and soup are enough. We don't know if the 0.1% will survive in their dome while everything else burns. We don't know if AI is fire or mirror or trickster or tool.

But we know this: the 69% that never gets solved is still there. The cry beneath the fight is still crying. The star still pours. The amends still need to be made. The fire is still burning — gently or catastrophically, depending on who's tending it.

So: tend it. Gently. Together. With all your cracks showing.

That's the book. That's the prayer. That's the dance.

## About

You are not reading a finished thing. You are holding a living document that is trying to repair the idea of what a book is.

A book, traditionally: one author, one voice, one argument, one cover. Finished. Published. Done.

This grammar: many voices, many lenses, cracks in every chapter, forkable, editable, alive. It's a .json file in a public repository. It's designed to be read by a viewer at recursive.eco AND by anyone who opens the file. It's designed to be rewritten by you.

The thesis: repair is the single most adaptive capacity a system can have. Couples that repair last. Cultures that repair last. Species that repair last. Everything else is extraction — and extraction, by definition, runs out.

The method: twelve chapters, each mapped to a Major Arcana tarot card. Each chapter has ten lenses: Research, Clinical, Spiritual, Practices, Rituals, Songs, Kids Version, Sci-Fi, Open Source, and Cracks. The cracks are not decorative. They're where the book doesn't believe itself. They're where you come in.

The arc: from one couple's fight about the dishes (Chapter 1) to the stars looking back (Chapter 10), and finally to the practice itself — showing up for the conversation with life using whatever language you've agreed to speak (Chapter 11). From Gottman's Love Lab to Bayo's trickster campfire. From 'I need you' to 'we're all already connected.' And the crack in that arc: maybe we're not. Maybe the connection is a hope, not a fact. Maybe this book is a prayer, not a proof.

Either way: come cook with us. The soup is ready.

## The Tarot Journey

The Fool (0) begins the journey by stepping off the cliff — that's you, opening this book. The Lovers (VI) choose to stay with the unsolvable. The Moon (XVIII) reveals the cry in the dark. The Star (XVII) kneels naked by the water. Judgement (XX) calls the dead to rise. Temperance (XIV) pours without spilling. The Wheel (X) turns. The Devil (XV) shows the comfortable chain. The Tower (XVI) falls. Strength (XI) opens the lion's mouth with tenderness. The World (XXI) dances. The High Priestess (II) sits between the pillars, holding the scroll — she is the practice itself, the conversation with life, the agreement to show up and listen in whatever language you've chosen. And The Hanged Man (XII) hangs upside down from the World Tree, seeing everything differently — he is the I Ching, the Book of Changes, the 3,000-year-old text that survived by being read upside down: colonizers saw fortune-telling, practitioners saw cosmology.

Not all 22 Major Arcana are used. The missing cards — The Magician, The Emperor, The Empress, The Hierophant, The Chariot, The Hermit, Death, The Sun — are the Agnostos Theos of this book. The gods we didn't name. They might appear in the next version. Or in yours.

## How to Use This Grammar

ON RECURSIVE.ECO: Browse the cards. Draw one at random. Read the lens that calls to you. Skip the rest. Come back tomorrow.

AS A BOOK: Read chapters 1-12 in order. It's a journey — intimate → cultural → ecological → integration → practice → change. The Kids Version of each chapter is a gentler entry point. The Cracks section is the honest exit. Chapter 11 is the practice chapter — tarot, journaling, card-making, oracular consultation as the tool that holds everything.

AS A PRACTICE: Pick one practice from any chapter and do it for a week. Repair attempts (Ch1). Naming the cry (Ch2). A vulnerability request (Ch3). An amends letter (Ch4). Cold water on your face (Ch5). A seasonal ritual (Ch6). An extraction audit (Ch7). A fire ceremony (Ch8). A Clearness Committee (Ch9). Cooking for someone (Ch10). A daily card pull with journaling (Ch11). Illustrate a hexagram and sit with what emerges (Ch12).

AS AN OPEN SOURCE PROJECT: Fork this grammar. Add your culture's repair practices. Translate the Kids Version into your language. Write a Crack that breaks something I said. Add your grandmother's soup recipe — or your admission that your grandmother, like many grandmothers, didn't know about repair either.

AS A PLAYLIST: Play all the songs in order. That's a 90-minute listening experience that IS the book, in music.

AS A PRAYER: Read the Spiritual sections as a devotional sequence. Light a candle. Sit with it. The book is, among other things, a 12-part prayer to the Unknown God — the one the Greeks named Agnostos Theos, the altar to everything we haven't figured out yet.
