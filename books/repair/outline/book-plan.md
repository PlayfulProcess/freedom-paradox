# The Repair Deck: A Major Arcana for Mending

## An Open-Source Book About Repair at Every Scale

**By [PlayfulProcess](https://playfulprocess.com) with Claude**

---

**Working thesis:** 69% of conflicts never resolve (Gottman). The question becomes repair capacity, not problem-solving. Cultures with high repair infrastructure survive centuries; those optimized for extraction collapse within 400 years. This book maps 22 archetypal repair patterns — a Major Arcana for mending — across intimate, communal, and civilizational scales.

**Structure:** 22 chapters (cards), each examined through 10 lenses:
1. Research — scholarly foundation with citations
2. Clinical — therapeutic/practical application
3. Spiritual — contemplative/prayer dimensions
4. Practices — actionable exercises
5. Rituals — ceremonial applications
6. Songs & Playlists — cultural touchstones
7. Kids Version — accessible reframing
8. Sci-Fi — speculative extrapolation
9. Open Source — public domain references
10. Cracks — decolonial critique (acknowledging limitations)

**Current status:** 6 of 22 chapters drafted in grammar.json format:
- Ch 1: The 69% That Never Gets Solved (Gottman, perpetual problems)
- Ch 2: The Cry Beneath the Fight (Bowlby, attachment theory)
- Ch 3: Hold Me Tight (Sue Johnson, EFT)
- Ch 4: Making Amends (AA's 12-step model)
- Ch 5: Staying Alive in the Fire (Linehan, DBT)
- Ch 6: What Survives the Turning (ecological/systemic sustainability)

**Connection to other books:** Extends Working Architecture (practical containers → repair when containers break). Uses the Linehan filter (useful, fits the data, compassionate) from Grammars. The repair grammar is what happens when the responsibility structures from the theoretical books meet actual human mess.

**Source material:** `research/grammar.json` (original recursive.eco grammar), `research/grammar-inner-triangle.json` (CBT/DBT/EFT relational cards)
