## Task 5: Singer Contemplative Dyads Primary Source

**Status: FOUND — figure needs correction (50% → 51%)**

### Primary Paper
- **Engert V, Kok BE, Papassotiriou I, Chrousos GP, Singer T. (2017)** "Specific reduction in cortisol stress reactivity after social but not attention-based mental training." *Science Advances* 3(10):e1700495. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.1700495. Published October 4, 2017. Cited 205 times.
- Part of Tania Singer's **ReSource Project** at Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences, Leipzig.

### Key Finding
- Participants who completed the socio-affective (Affect Module) or socio-cognitive (Perspective Module) training — which included **Contemplative Dyad** exercises — released **up to 51% less cortisol** in their saliva when exposed to the Trier Social Stress Test (a standardized psychosocial stressor: mock job interview + mental arithmetic).
- The purely attention-based module (Presence Module, mindfulness only) did **not** reduce cortisol stress reactivity.
- This is an *acute* stress response measure (salivary cortisol under Trier Social Stress Test), not a chronic/long-term measure.

### Correction Needed
- Ch05 says "reduces cortisol stress levels by fifty percent in *both* participants" — should be **fifty-one percent** (51%)
- The "in both participants" phrasing is somewhat misleading. The study measured individual cortisol responses of trained participants vs. controls, not cortisol in dyad pairs simultaneously. The dyadic exercises trained the capacity, but the cortisol was measured in individual stress tests.
- Suggested revision for the comment: replace the comment with the full citation.

### Companion Paper (Long-Term Cortisol)
- **Puhlmann LMC et al. (2021)** "Contemplative Mental Training Reduces Hair Glucocorticoid Levels in a Randomized Clinical Trial." *Psychosomatic Medicine* 83(8):894-905. PMC8505163. Same ReSource Project, measuring long-term (hair) cortisol. Also found reductions, confirming both acute and chronic stress markers improve.

### Action Needed
- Replace `<!-- Singer contemplative dyads 50% cortisol ... -->` comment with proper citation
- Correct "fifty percent" to "fifty-one percent"
- Clarify that cortisol reduction was measured in individual stress tests after dyadic training, not simultaneously in both dyad members
- Full citation: Engert et al., *Science Advances* 3(10):e1700495, 2017
