# The "Linehan Wager" Does Not Exist

**Summary:** The "three-filter test" (useful, fits data, compassionate) appears nowhere in Linehan's published works. The term "Linehan wager" exists in zero discoverable sources.

## What actually exists:

Linehan & Wilks (2015) in American Journal of Psychotherapy:
"Such a model would have to be capable of guiding effective therapy, nonpejorative for the client, and compatible with current research data."

These were design criteria for the biosocial model of BPD — not a repeatable epistemological test.

## The semantic leaps:
- "Capable of guiding effective therapy" → "Is it useful?" (reasonable)
- "Compatible with current research data" → "Does it fit the data?" (close)
- "Nonpejorative for the client" → "Is it compassionate?" (SIGNIFICANT LEAP — nonpejorative means non-blaming, narrower than compassion)

## What the book must do:

The "Linehan wager" is used as the book's epistemological method throughout (~10 chapters). Options:

1. RENAME IT: Call it "the book's three-filter test" or "the adaptive fitness test" and credit the inspiration to Linehan's design criteria without claiming she proposed it as a general test
2. ADD ATTRIBUTION: "Inspired by Linehan's design criteria for the biosocial model (Linehan & Wilks, 2015), this book applies three questions..."
3. ACKNOWLEDGE: "The term 'Linehan wager' is this book's coinage, not Linehan's."

Source: Linehan & Wilks 2015 American Journal of Psychotherapy; Linehan 2020 Building a Life Worth Living
