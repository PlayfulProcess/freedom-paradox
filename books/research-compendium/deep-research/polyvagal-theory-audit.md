# Polyvagal Theory: A Theory in Crisis

**Summary:** 39 experts in autonomic physiology have declared PVT "untenable," yet it remains widely adopted in trauma therapy.

## What the book should do:

The book already has a disclaimer in Ch14: "The polyvagal framework is a Western scientific correlate, not the foundation of relating."

This disclaimer is INSUFFICIENT given the research. The book needs to:

1. Stop using "ventral vagal" and "dorsal vagal" as if they map cleanly onto social engagement vs shutdown — they don't
2. Use the EMPIRICAL OBSERVATIONS (autonomic state influences social behavior; safety facilitates connection) without depending on PVT's specific mechanisms
3. Add: "Polyvagal theory's clinical utility as a therapeutic heuristic does not establish the accuracy of its neuroanatomical and evolutionary claims. Thirty-nine experts in autonomic physiology have declared the core biological premises untenable (Grossman et al., 2026). The observations this chapter draws on — that the nervous system is an open circuit, that co-regulation is biological, that safety enables social engagement — stand on independent evidence and do not require PVT to be correct."

## Key findings:
- Myelinated cardiac vagal fibers are NOT uniquely mammalian (found in lungfish, sharks, reptiles)
- Virtually all cardiac vagal control comes from NAmb, not the dorsal/ventral split PVT proposes
- The three-tier phylogenetic hierarchy has no empirical support
- The "social engagement system" is not a demonstrated anatomical circuit
- RSA is NOT a reliable index of vagal tone (confounded by respiratory parameters)

## What IS accepted:
- Vagal tonic inhibition of heart rate
- Association between reduced vagal tone and psychopathology
- Therapeutic value of safety-oriented body-based approaches for trauma
- These stand WITHOUT PVT

Source: Grossman 2023 Biological Psychology; Grossman et al. 2026 Clinical Neuropsychiatry (39 signatories); Taylor et al. 2022; Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022; Monteiro et al. 2018 Science Advances
