# Campbell Never Simply Said "We Need New Myths"

**Summary:** The popular attribution captures roughly one-quarter of his actual position. His real argument was paradoxical: we need planetary mythology AND myths cannot be invented.

## What he actually said:
- "The only mythology that is valid today is the mythology of the planet — and we don't have such a mythology" (Power of Myth, p. 28)
- "Myths are not invented as stories are. Myths are inspired. They come from the same realm that dream comes from" (1981 Moyers interview)
- "We can't have a mythology for a long, long time to come. Things are changing too fast to become mythologized" (Power of Myth)
- "Mythologies are not invented; they are found" (Pathways to Bliss)

## The paradox:
1. Traditional mythologies are failing
2. A new mythology must be planetary
3. But myths cannot be deliberately invented
4. Artists keep myth alive through creative expression, not construction
5. In the interregnum, individuals work out personal myths from fragments
