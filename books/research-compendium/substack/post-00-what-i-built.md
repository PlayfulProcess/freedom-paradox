# What I Built and Why I'm Reading It Slowly

---

I promised to build the tools. I built them.

Over the past year I wrote six books without realizing they were one book. A book about open source and AI. A book about nervous systems and bedtime stories. A book about Kashmir Shaivism and the invisible world. A book about what to do Monday morning with a child, a community, a commons. A book about food. A book about whether inherent value is truth or axiom — and why the answer might not matter.

They turned out to be one argument: **the species that got fire before it had the grammars to hold it.**

---

## The Dilemmas

The argument has a shape. Book One is called *The Dilemmas* — the sixty-nine percent that never resolves. Gottman found that couples who stay together and couples who divorce have the same percentage of unsolvable problems. The difference is repair capacity. I think civilizations work the same way. Open or closed. Safety or freedom. AI as gift or weapon. These don't resolve. They are perpetual problems. The question is whether we can live inside them without destroying each other.

## Relational Responsibilities

Book Two is called *Relational Responsibilities* — what to do when you stop trying to solve the unsolvable and start practicing instead. A bedtime story. A tarot card on your phone that interrupts the scroll. A grammar — a finite set of symbols that generates infinite meaning when practiced in community. A locked-down YouTube player where your child watches the same story over and over, because repetition is how nervous systems learn and the algorithm is designed to prevent it.

---

## The Tools

I built the tools because I believe the argument.

[recursive.eco](https://recursive.eco) is infrastructure for adaptive meaning-making through make-believe. Tarot decks, ancient texts, kids' stories, wellness tools — created by people, not algorithms. Free and open.

[flow.recursive.eco](https://flow.recursive.eco) delivers grammars to your phone — using the device against its own logic. The card interrupts the scroll. Five seconds of looking at an image you didn't choose is a parasympathetic micro-shift. That's the practice.

[kids.recursive.eco](https://kids.recursive.eco) is a COPPA-compliant viewing space where the parent builds the playlist and the child chooses within it — agency inside a relational container, not agency inside an extractive one.

I am not innocent in any of this. My platform casts light and shadow with every design choice. Donation-based: light, no extraction; shadow, may not scale. Open interpretation: light, you make meaning together; shadow, symbols collected without being sat with. Co-creating with AI: light, a mirror for reflection; shadow, AI can flatten nuance. Every feature carries both. I name them because the trap of solving is invisible precisely when you believe you've escaped it.

## What I Learned This Year

I sat in an anti-oppressive social work course — a course designed to dismantle colonial patterns in the helping professions. In week two, an Implicit Association Test showed me my bias lives in my shoulders before it reaches my thoughts. The body relaxed into the biased pairing. The cognitive ease was palpable.

Vanessa Andreotti calls the alternative to resolution *analeptic thinking* — holding contradictions without synthesis. I have bias AND I'm committed to justice AND awareness won't eliminate what lives in the nervous system AND the frameworks I'm using may themselves be inadequate. No synthesis. Just ongoing tension.

This is the sixty-nine percent applied to the self.

## The Practice

I've turned the entire book — all forty-two chapters, both appendices, every grammar deck — into a single grammar on recursive.eco. One hundred and eighty-four cards. Each one has a summary, a key insight, and a question.

Every month, I will cast a story from my own grammar. The question will be the same each time: **should I finish my MSW?**

I don't know the answer. I left equity research because I wanted to build gateways to mental health access. I discovered the profession was structured by the colonial patterns it claimed to dismantle. I built the gateways anyway — outside the institution, on my own terms. Whether I need the credential is an open question. The uncertainty itself may be the honest position.

The grammar will cast a card. I will sit with it. I will interpret it — sometimes with AI, sometimes alone, sometimes with whoever shows up. I will write what I find. And I will post it here, alongside the original chapter or card that was drawn, so you can read the source material and make your own meaning.

Joseph Campbell said we need new mythologies. I don't have one. I have threads on a loom. Whether a fabric emerges depends on who else shows up to weave.

## The Song

I'm writing this the night before a Tantra Illuminated retreat. Tomorrow I sit with the tradition whose circularity this book examines — the one that says: begin by accepting your wholeness in order to realize your wholeness. The practice presupposes the recognition it claims to produce. Tillich would call this courage. Wallis calls it recognition. A five-year-old hearing Shel Silverstein would laugh.

I wrote a song for the occasion. It's called "The Tending Is the Fire." Because the practice doesn't tend something separate from itself. The bedtime story doesn't serve the child's development — the bedtime story IS the development. The grammar doesn't constrain power from outside — the grammar IS the power, shaped. Non-dual. The tending and the fire are not two things.
