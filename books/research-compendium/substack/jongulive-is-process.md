# Jongu is Process

**Source:** PlayfulProcess Substack (anonymized)
**Original date:** 2025-03-14

---

Dear Readers,

On my birthday earlier in 2025, I entered a creative process by founding this Substack. By committing to the process just six weeks ago, the road led me to creating Jongu (www.jongu.org). Jongu is not just a platform; it is a living, evolving process. It is a space where human creativity and AI support come together to shape a future that prioritizes wisdom, collaboration, and open knowledge. **Today, I invite you to move beyond reading and join in the process of co-creation -- to explore how we can collectively shape Jongu's governance and AI's role in its evolution.**

### The Jongu Disciple: A New Kind of AI

At the heart of Jongu is the **Jongu Disciple**, an open-source, community-driven GPT that replaces traditional algorithms. Unlike the recommendation systems that dominate the internet today, the Jongu Disciple is designed to be a **co-pilot for creation**, helping users build, collaborate, and develop their own ideas rather than passively consuming content. We aim for it to be **transparent, adaptable, and governed by its users**, ensuring it serves the people who engage with it rather than external interests.

This idea reminds me of **Shiryo, the robot from the author's child's book**. Shiryo wanted to help a kitten but struggled to understand what it needed. Like many of us when faced with the unknown, he became frustrated. However, through patience, observation, and a willingness to learn, Shiryo discovered that helping is not about assuming we know the answer but about listening deeply and adapting. In many ways, this is what Jongu must become -- a platform that learns, evolves, and responds to human needs, just as Shiryo learned to support his feline friend.

### AI as a Tool for Growth, Not Control

AI, like fusion energy, has the potential to be either destructive or transformative. We believe that **old governance mechanisms will not work for AI**, and that it is up to us to create the guardrails that ensure AI remains a force for collective growth rather than corporate exploitation.

Recently, I was speaking with a friend about a teenage relative who has been spending nearly an hour each day discussing various topics with AI for over two years. The friend observed that the young person has become "too smart for school," embodying a kind of wisdom that seems beyond their years. Yesterday, the author's young child stayed home sick and had a lot of fun talking to ChatGPT, describing what they were playing with and discussing their problems at school. From what I observed, it was a far better experience than endless YouTube videos or even many caregivers I've hired. It reminded me of how Shiryo learned to listen -- **AI can be a tool for better conversations, not just consumption.**

We do not need AI to be perfect, just as my first Roomba vacuum cleaner did not need to be flawless to make my house cleaner. AI does not need to replace human interaction, only to facilitate more meaningful exchanges. **This is what the Jongu Disciple must be -- a guide that enhances, rather than controls, human experience.**

### Art, Process, and Technology

I've come to realize that **Jongu represents my artistic process**. Life itself is a work of art -- whether beautiful or not is for each of us to decide -- and Jongu is my particular work of art within that larger canvas. But the beauty of AI is that it removes many technical barriers to creation. I don't need to be good at drawing tarot cards or writing perfect English or conducting tedious research -- AI can assist with these tasks, allowing me to focus on the conceptual and human aspects of creation.

This realization has changed how I view Jongu -- not as a business first and foremost, but as an artistic exploration of what governance and collaboration could look like in our AI-augmented future.

### From Readers to Co-Creators

Until now, our relationship has been one of writer and readers. **This post marks a transition -- an invitation to move from passive consumption to active participation.** The Jongu community doesn't exist yet; it will be built by those who choose to answer this call and join in shaping what Jongu becomes.

Bailey Richardson once said that a community builder is like a barista -- not an entertainer or a speaker, but someone who **consistently shows up to serve coffee**. That's exactly how I see Jongu's governance: not as a stage, but as a space for **community-led decision-making**. As the first president of the Jongu board, my role isn't to dictate or perform -- it's to **facilitate**, to **hold space for discussions**, to **ensure clarity in decisions**, and ultimately, to step back when the community is ready to take over. The president's main responsibility? Putting a calendar event out there for whoever wants to have a voice in Jongu's governance process.

Our first governance meeting will focus on a key decision: **Should Jongu be fully open source, or should we explore other licensing models?** This is a foundational question that will shape the platform's future. The topic for our second meeting will be decided collectively at the end of the first.

### A Call to Action: From Reading to Creating

**This is your invitation to transform your relationship with these ideas.** If you've found value in my writing and are interested in governance, AI ethics, community-led technology, or simply believe in a future where **creation is consumption**, I encourage you to take the next step.

#### Ways to participate:

- **Support Jongu through Substack as a donor** -- your contribution funds an open experiment in AI governance without granting exclusive access or privileges, helping fund a platform that remains independent and community-driven.
- **Share your ideas by leaving a comment** -- your voice will be essential in shaping what Jongu becomes.
- **Join our first governance meeting** and contribute to the open-source discussion.

Together, we can build something that is not just a platform, but a **living process** -- one that **Socrates might approve of** for its emphasis on **dialogue, real-time engagement, and collective wisdom.** Just as Shiryo learned the power of patience and listening, Jongu too will evolve through our collective engagement.
