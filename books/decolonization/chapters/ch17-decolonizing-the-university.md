# Decolonizing the University

*Mind*

## The Pattern

Universities are colonial institutions. Not metaphorically — literally. Built on stolen land, funded by colonial wealth (slave trade profits, extractive industries, land grants on indigenous territory), organized around European disciplines, staffed predominantly by white scholars, producing knowledge that serves colonial economies. The Rhodes Must Fall movement asked not just about a statue but about the entire knowledge structure that the statue represented.

## What It Costs

Indigenous and Global South knowledge reduced to 'area studies' — an appendix to the real curriculum. Scholars from colonized communities expected to produce knowledge in colonial frameworks to be taken seriously. The publish-or-perish system that values journal articles over community knowledge. The university that studies indigenous people but doesn't learn from them. Billions in endowments built on colonial extraction, managed to grow more wealth rather than repair the damage.

## What Persists

Indigenous universities and tribal colleges. The Intercultural University movement in Latin America. Rhodes Must Fall and its global echoes. Scholars like Linda Tuhiwai Smith whose 'Decolonizing Methodologies' changed how research relates to indigenous communities. The growing insistence on community-based participatory research. The question that won't go away: whose knowledge is this university for?

## The Invitation

If you were educated in a Western university, your thinking was shaped by an institution that was never designed for everyone's knowledge. This doesn't make your education worthless. It makes it partial. What did your education teach you to see? What did it teach you not to see?
