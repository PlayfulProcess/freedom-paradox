# Conservation Colonialism

*Planet*

## The Pattern

Fortress conservation — the model of protecting nature by removing people — is colonialism wearing green. National parks created by dispossessing indigenous communities. Conservation NGOs buying land and evicting residents. The assumption that humans and nature are separate and that nature requires protection from humans (rather than from particular economic systems). This model of conservation reproduces the colonial myth of wilderness: pristine nature exists only where indigenous people have been removed.

## What It Costs

An estimated 14 million indigenous and traditional people displaced by conservation projects worldwide. Maasai evicted from the Serengeti. Batwa expelled from Bwindi Impenetrable Forest. San people removed from the Central Kalahari Game Reserve. The cruel irony: the communities displaced were often the ones whose practices maintained the biodiversity that conservation now seeks to protect. Remove the people, and the ecosystem degrades.

## What Persists

Indigenous and Community Conserved Areas (ICCAs) — conservation led by communities rather than imposed on them. The recognition that indigenous territories are the most biodiverse precisely because of, not despite, indigenous management. The shift from fortress conservation to community-based conservation. The 30x30 target (protecting 30% of earth by 2030) increasingly recognizing that indigenous-led conservation is not an alternative to 'real' conservation — it is the most effective conservation.

## The Invitation

Who was removed so that your favorite national park could exist? This is not an argument against parks. It's an argument for a different kind of conservation — one that includes the people who know the land best.
