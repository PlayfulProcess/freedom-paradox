# The Spirit: Who Told You How to Pray?

*Domains*

## About

The colonization of spirit may be the oldest colonial project. Before borders, before schools, before plantations, there was the mission. The insistence that one relationship with the sacred is universal and all others are primitive, demonic, or imaginary. Colonial spirituality severs the human from the land, the ancestors from the living, the sacred from the everyday. It replaces a thousand ways of praying with one God who, conveniently, speaks the colonizer's language.

## The Invitation

What is your relationship with the sacred? Not what you believe — what you practice. What you turn to in grief, in wonder, in darkness? And where did that practice come from? Was it chosen, inherited, imposed, or recovered?

## The Colonial Logic

One God. One book. One way. The conversion mandate — the assumption that indigenous spiritual practices are not just different but wrong, not just wrong but dangerous. The secular version is equally colonial: the assumption that spiritual practice itself is pre-rational, that enlightened people outgrow it, that the disenchantment of the world is progress rather than amputation.
