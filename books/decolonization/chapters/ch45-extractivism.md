# Extractivism

*Planet*

## The Pattern

Extractivism is the logic of colonialism distilled to its essence: take, transform, discard. Take silver from Potosí, sugar from the Caribbean, rubber from the Congo, oil from the Niger Delta, lithium from Bolivia, coltan from the DRC. The pattern is five centuries old and accelerating. The extractive frontier moves but the structure stays: resources flow from colonized lands to metropolitan markets, profits accumulate in imperial centers, waste and devastation remain where the resources were.

## What It Costs

Potosí: 8 million indigenous and enslaved African people died in the silver mines. The Congo: King Leopold's rubber terror killed an estimated 10 million Congolese. The Niger Delta: half a century of oil extraction has destroyed the ecosystem and poisoned the water. Bolivia: lithium mining for 'green' technology threatens indigenous water sources. The cost is always borne by the people living on top of what the market wants below.

## What Persists

Communities that say no. The Ogoni people's resistance to Shell in Nigeria. The Waorani nation's legal victory against oil drilling in Ecuador. Water protectors at Standing Rock. Anti-mining movements across Latin America. The concept of 'leaving it in the ground' — the recognition that not all resources should be extracted, that some wealth is worth more underground, as the earth's body rather than the market's commodity.

## The Invitation

Everything you own was extracted from somewhere. This is not guilt — it's physics. But physics without ethics is extractivism. What would an economy look like that took only what the earth offered rather than what the market demanded?
