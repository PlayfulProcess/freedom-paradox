# Body Sovereignty and Bodily Autonomy

*Body*

## The Pattern

Colonial power has always operated through bodies — controlling who can move freely, who can reproduce, who gets experimented on, who is considered fully human. Enslaved bodies as property. Indigenous bodies as specimens. Women's bodies as territory. Disabled bodies as defective. Trans bodies as impossible. The colonial body is a body that belongs to the state, the church, the market — never to the person living in it.

## What It Costs

Forced sterilization programs across the Americas, from indigenous women in the US and Canada to Black and Puerto Rican women. Medical experimentation — Tuskegee, Henrietta Lacks, residential school nutritional experiments. Body policing that continues in stop-and-frisk, dress codes, bathroom bills. The internalized sense that your body needs permission to exist as it is.

## What Persists

Reproductive justice movements led by women of color who insist that bodily autonomy includes the right to have children, not have children, and parent children in safe environments. Disability justice that refuses the medical model and insists disabled bodies are not broken. Trans and Two-Spirit people reclaiming identities that predate colonization. The simple, radical act of existing in your body without apology.

## The Invitation

Where in your body do you carry the sense that you need permission? Permission to rest, to take up space, to look the way you look, to feel what you feel? That sense has a history. It was put there.
