# Education as Colonization

*Mind*

## The Pattern

'Kill the Indian, save the man.' Captain Richard Henry Pratt's motto for the Carlisle Indian Industrial School (1879) was not an aberration — it was colonial education's honest mission statement. From residential schools in Canada and the US to missionary schools across Africa and Asia, colonial education had one purpose: to replace indigenous knowledge, language, spirituality, and identity with European ones. The method was children. The weapon was school.

## What It Costs

In Canada alone, over 150,000 indigenous children were taken from their families and placed in residential schools. Thousands died. Survivors carry trauma that echoes across generations — loss of language, loss of parenting models, loss of cultural knowledge, loss of trust. Unmarked graves continue to be found. In Australia, the Stolen Generations. In the US, boarding schools. In Africa, the colonial school that taught you to despise your own culture in the language of the colonizer.

## What Persists

Indigenous-run schools. Tribal colleges. Land-based education. Language nests. The growing recognition that education is never neutral — it always teaches you whose knowledge matters and whose doesn't. Decolonial pedagogy that centers community knowledge, elder teaching, land as teacher, story as curriculum. Paulo Freire's insistence that education is either domestication or liberation.

## The Invitation

What did school teach you to forget? Not just content — but ways of learning. Did you learn that knowledge comes from books or from land? From experts or from elders? From competition or from relationship? The answers reveal the colonial architecture of your mind.
