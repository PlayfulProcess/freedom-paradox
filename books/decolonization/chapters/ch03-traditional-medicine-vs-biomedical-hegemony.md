# Traditional Medicine vs. Biomedical Hegemony

*Body*

## The Pattern

Colonial medicine operates through a simple equation: knowledge that can be patented is medicine; knowledge that can't be patented is superstition. Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, curanderismo, indigenous plant knowledge — these are either dismissed as pre-scientific or strip-mined for active compounds, patented, and sold back at markup. The healer becomes the patient. The grandmother becomes the clinical trial subject.

## What It Costs

Generations of indigenous healers criminalized, sometimes killed. Plant medicines classified as controlled substances while their synthetic derivatives become blockbuster drugs. Communities dependent on pharmaceutical supply chains for conditions their ancestors managed with local plants. The loss of holistic frameworks — medicine as relationship with land, spirit, community — replaced by medicine as chemical intervention in a biological machine.

## What Persists

Curanderas still practice. Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine serve billions. Indigenous plant knowledge is increasingly recognized — not by being validated by Western science, but by communities who never stopped using it. Ethnobotanists and indigenous knowledge keepers collaborate on protocols that protect intellectual sovereignty. Some hospitals now integrate traditional healing. The WHO recognizes traditional medicine, however imperfectly.

## The Invitation

Notice which healing practices you consider legitimate and which you consider alternative. Who taught you that hierarchy? What would medicine look like if the question wasn't 'does it work by our standards?' but 'what does this community know about being well?'
