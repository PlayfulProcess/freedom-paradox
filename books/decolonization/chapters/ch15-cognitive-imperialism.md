# Cognitive Imperialism

*Mind*

## The Pattern

Marie Battiste coined the term: cognitive imperialism is the imposition of one worldview as the only valid framework for understanding reality. It operates not through force but through the quiet assumption that Western categories are universal. Subject/object. Mind/body. Human/nature. Civilized/primitive. These binaries feel like reality itself — but they're a particular cultural operating system installed so successfully that it became invisible.

## What It Costs

The inability to think outside Western categories even when trying to. Decolonial thinkers working in colonial languages, publishing in colonial journals, seeking legitimacy from colonial institutions. The recursive trap: you can't think your way out of cognitive imperialism using the thinking cognitive imperialism installed. This is what Audre Lorde meant: the master's tools will never dismantle the master's house.

## What Persists

Indigenous languages that structure reality differently. Relational ontologies that refuse the subject/object split. Process philosophies that prioritize becoming over being. Ubuntu — I am because we are. The Lakota phrase 'Mitakuye Oyasin' (all my relatives) that includes stones, rivers, and stars. Every time someone thinks in a way that colonial categories cannot contain, cognitive imperialism loses a little ground.

## The Invitation

The hardest decolonization is the decolonization of your own thinking. Not replacing one set of conclusions with another, but noticing the categories themselves. What binary are you trapped in right now? What if both sides of that binary are colonial?
