# Epistemic Violence: The Killing of Knowledge Systems

*Mind*

## The Pattern

Boaventura de Sousa Santos calls it epistemicide — the systematic destruction of knowledge systems that don't conform to Western scientific rationalism. It's not that indigenous people had no astronomy, medicine, ecology, psychology, philosophy. It's that these knowledge systems were actively classified as myth, folklore, superstition — words that mean 'not real knowledge.' The violence is not ignorance. It's reclassification.

## What It Costs

Incalculable. We don't know what we've lost because the framework for knowing it was destroyed along with the knowledge itself. Entire ecological management systems. Medicinal knowledge. Mathematical traditions. Philosophical frameworks. Navigation systems. The Library of Alexandria burns every day in slow motion — not in one fire but in a thousand small acts of dismissal, defunding, and disregard.

## What Persists

Knowledge systems that survived through practice rather than publication. Aboriginal Australian songlines — the oldest continuously maintained knowledge system on earth. Polynesian navigation. Amazonian ecological knowledge. Andean mathematics. These persist not because they were preserved in archives but because communities kept living them, often in secret, often at great cost.

## The Invitation

What do you know that you don't consider knowledge? What does your body know, your family know, your land know, that you've been taught to discount? Epistemic justice begins with the recognition that knowledge has always been plural.
