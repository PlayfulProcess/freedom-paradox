# The Collective: Who Told You How to Organize?

*Domains*

## About

Every human community organizes itself — for decision-making, resource sharing, conflict resolution, care of the vulnerable, celebration of the sacred. Colonialism replaced this diversity of organizing with a single model: the nation-state, market capitalism, representative democracy, and the NGO. These are presented as the pinnacle of human social evolution. They are, in fact, one culture's answers to universal questions — answers that have been imposed globally through force, debt, and the quiet violence of 'development.'

## The Invitation

What forms of collective life did your ancestors practice before the state told them how to organize? What forms of mutual care exist in your community that have no name in political science?

## The Colonial Logic

The state is the only legitimate political form. The market is the only legitimate economic form. Democracy (representative, Western-style) is the only legitimate governance form. Everything else — consensus, commons, gift economy, council of elders, matrilineal authority — is pre-modern. To develop is to adopt Western political and economic forms. To resist is to be backward.
