# Colonial Infrastructure: Borders, Passports, Debt

*Collective*

## The Pattern

Colonial power operates through infrastructure — physical and administrative systems that organize life according to colonial logic long after formal colonialism ends. Borders drawn by colonial powers. Passport systems that determine who moves freely and who is detained. National debt incurred by colonial or post-colonial regimes and enforced on populations who never consented. Railways built to extract resources from interior to port. Languages of administration. Legal systems. Currency. These are not neutral tools. They are colonial architectures that continue to function as designed.

## What It Costs

Haiti paid France reparations for its own liberation from slavery — from 1825 to 1947 — a debt that crippled the world's first Black republic. African nations still pay colonial debts. CFA franc zones in West and Central Africa maintain French monetary control over former colonies. Border violence kills thousands annually. Passport privilege determines whose life is mobile and whose is caged. The infrastructure of colonialism is the water we swim in — so pervasive it seems like reality itself.

## What Persists

Debt cancellation movements — Jubilee campaigns that echo the ancient tradition of periodic debt forgiveness. The African Union's push for continental free movement. Alternative currencies and community banking. The Zapatistas' autonomous infrastructure — their own schools, hospitals, governance, built outside and against the colonial state. Every community that builds its own infrastructure rather than waiting for the state to provide it (and attach conditions).

## The Invitation

What infrastructure shapes your daily life? Roads, laws, currencies, borders, languages — who built them, for whose benefit, and at whose cost? Infrastructure is political. It just doesn't look political because it's been there longer than you have.
