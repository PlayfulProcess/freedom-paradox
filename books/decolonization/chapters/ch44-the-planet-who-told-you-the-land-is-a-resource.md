# The Planet: Who Told You the Land Is a Resource?

*Domains*

## About

The ecological crisis is a colonial crisis. Not because colonialism caused pollution (though it did) but because colonialism installed the ontology that makes ecological destruction logical. If the earth is a resource, you extract it. If the forest is timber, you cut it. If the river is water supply, you dam it. If the mountain is ore, you mine it. Decolonizing our relationship with the planet means recognizing that the ecological emergency is not a failure of management but the success of a worldview that treats the living earth as dead matter.

## The Invitation

The planet doesn't need saving. It needs to be listened to. What is the land where you live telling you? Not the data — the land itself. What is dying? What is being born? What does it ask of you?

## The Colonial Logic

Nature as standing reserve. The earth as raw material awaiting human purpose. Progress measured in GDP — the metric that counts the cutting of a forest as growth and the standing forest as nothing. The carbon economy as the planetary-scale expression of enclosure: privatize the atmosphere, commodify the climate, trade the right to pollute. The ultimate colonial extraction: not just taking from the land but changing the chemical composition of the sky.
