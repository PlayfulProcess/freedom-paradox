# Linear Time vs. Cyclical Time

*Mind*

## The Pattern

Colonial time is linear: progress marches forward, the past is behind, the future is ahead, and development is the only direction. This time is clock time, factory time, quarterly-earnings time. It replaced cyclical time — seasonal, ceremonial, ecological, ancestral — not because linear time is truer but because it serves industrial capitalism. You can't exploit a people who measure time in ceremonies and harvests. You need them on the clock.

## What It Costs

The colonization of time is the colonization of meaning. In linear time, indigenous peoples are 'behind.' Traditional practices are 'outdated.' Ancient knowledge is 'superseded.' The very grammar of progress makes the colonized permanently past-tense. Meanwhile, living in clock time disconnects everyone — colonizer and colonized alike — from seasonal rhythms, bodily rhythms, the rhythms of grief and celebration that don't fit in calendar slots.

## What Persists

Ceremonial time. Dreamtime. The Andean concept of nayra — where the past is in front of you (because you can see it) and the future is behind (because you can't). Hindu and Buddhist concepts of cyclical time. The Jewish Sabbath that breaks the week's productivity. The land itself, which operates in cycles regardless of what the clock says. Ecological time — the time of seasons, tides, migration, decay, renewal — which is reasserting itself through climate crisis.

## The Invitation

What time are you living in? Clock time? Deadline time? Career-arc time? What would it mean to live in seasonal time, or ceremonial time, or the time of your body rather than your calendar? Linear time is not wrong. But it is not the only time.
