# The Development Myth

*Collective*

## The Pattern

In 1949, Harry Truman inaugurated the age of 'development' by dividing the world into developed and underdeveloped nations. With one speech, two-thirds of the world's peoples were reclassified as lacking — not colonized, not exploited, but 'underdeveloped.' The solution: become like us. Development replaces the explicit racism of colonialism with the soft paternalism of expertise. The World Bank and IMF replaced colonial administrators. Structural adjustment replaced direct rule. The result is the same: Global South resources flow north, and the explanation is that the south hasn't developed enough yet.

## What It Costs

Structural adjustment programs that gutted public health and education across Africa and Latin America to service debts incurred by dictators the Global North installed. Cash crops replacing food crops. Privatization of water. The destruction of local economies to integrate them into global markets controlled from elsewhere. The psychological cost: entire peoples defined by what they lack rather than what they have. The category 'developing nation' is not a description. It's a judgment.

## What Persists

Post-development thought — Arturo Escobar, Gustavo Esteva, Vandana Shiva — that rejects the development framework entirely. Buen vivir (good living) from Andean traditions as an alternative to GDP growth. Degrowth movements in the Global North. Bhutan's Gross National Happiness. Ubuntu economics. The insistence that a community that feeds itself, governs itself, and lives in balance with its land is not underdeveloped — it's a community that refused enclosure.

## The Invitation

What does a good life look like? Not GDP per capita — a good life. Does your answer require the poverty of others? If not, you may already be thinking beyond development.
