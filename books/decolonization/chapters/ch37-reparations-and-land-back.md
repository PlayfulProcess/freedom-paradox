# Reparations and Land Back

*Collective*

## The Pattern

Colonial wealth didn't appear from nowhere. It was extracted — through enslaved labor, through stolen land, through resource extraction, through unequal trade enforced by military power. The wealth of the Global North is the poverty of the Global South, converted through centuries of organized theft. Reparations and Land Back are not demands for charity. They are demands for restitution — the return of what was taken.

## What It Costs

The refusal of reparations is the continuation of theft by other means. The US promised formerly enslaved people '40 acres and a mule' and never delivered. The racial wealth gap — white families holding roughly ten times the wealth of Black families — is the arithmetic of unpaid labor. Indigenous peoples hold a fraction of their original territories while their land generates wealth for others. The cost of not paying reparations is paid every day in poverty, incarceration, health disparities, and early death.

## What Persists

The Movement for Black Lives and its comprehensive reparations platform. Land Back as both metaphor and literal demand. The Landless Workers Movement (MST) in Brazil, occupying unused land for farming. Caribbean nations demanding reparations from former colonial powers. Germany's reparations to Holocaust survivors as precedent (however imperfect). Georgetown University's reckoning with its slave-sale history. The mathematics of justice: what was taken can be returned.

## The Invitation

Reparations are not about guilt. They're about math. Wealth was taken. It can be returned. Land was stolen. It can be returned. The question is not whether reparations are justified but whether you're willing to live in a world that acknowledges its debts.
