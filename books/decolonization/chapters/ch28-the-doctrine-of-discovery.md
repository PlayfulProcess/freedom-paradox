# The Doctrine of Discovery

*Spirit*

## The Pattern

In 1493, Pope Alexander VI issued the papal bull Inter Caetera, granting Spain sovereignty over lands 'discovered' in the Americas. This Doctrine of Discovery — the theological claim that Christian nations had the right to claim lands inhabited by non-Christians — became the legal foundation for European colonialism worldwide. It was cited by the US Supreme Court as recently as 2005 (City of Sherrill v. Oneida Indian Nation). Colonial law rests on colonial theology.

## What It Costs

The entire legal framework of settler-colonial land ownership traces back to a papal decree declaring that non-Christian peoples had no sovereignty. Treaties, constitutions, property law — all built on the theological assumption that some peoples are fully human and others are not. The Vatican formally repudiated the Doctrine of Discovery in 2023, five centuries too late and without material reparation. The legal structures it created remain fully operational.

## What Persists

Indigenous legal scholars and activists who name the Doctrine of Discovery as the root of dispossession and demand its formal repudiation in law, not just theology. The UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues. The movement to replace discovery doctrine with indigenous sovereignty. The growing recognition that secular law is not as secular as it claims — that theological assumptions are embedded in the legal foundations of colonial states.

## The Invitation

If you own property in the Americas, Australia, New Zealand, or any settler-colonial state, your title traces back — through however many transactions — to a claim based on the Doctrine of Discovery. This is not guilt. It's geography. What do you do with this knowledge?
