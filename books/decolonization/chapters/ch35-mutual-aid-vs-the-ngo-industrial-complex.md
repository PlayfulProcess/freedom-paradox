# Mutual Aid vs. the NGO Industrial Complex

*Collective*

## The Pattern

Colonial charity flows downward: from those who have to those who lack, with conditions attached. The NGO industrial complex professionalizes this flow — creating careers, bureaucracies, and metrics around the management of poverty. Mutual aid flows sideways: from those who have this to those who need this, and back again when needs shift. The colonial pattern is help. The decolonial pattern is solidarity. The difference is directionality, reciprocity, and who defines the problem.

## What It Costs

Communities made dependent on external aid that comes with external conditions. The professionalization of care — you need a degree to feed people, a nonprofit to house people, a grant to love people. The extraction of community capacity: when an NGO enters, it often replaces rather than strengthens existing mutual aid networks. The savior narrative that positions the helper as subject and the helped as object. Billions spent on development that maintains the structures producing poverty.

## What Persists

Mutual aid networks — from Black mutual aid societies in the 19th century to pandemic mutual aid in 2020. The Zapatistas' autonomous governance. Kurdish cooperatives. Landless Workers Movement (MST) in Brazil. Rotating savings circles (tandas, susus, chit funds) across the Global South. The understanding that the community already has what it needs — the task is removing obstacles, not delivering solutions.

## The Invitation

Do you help or do you show up? Helping creates hierarchy: you have, they lack. Showing up creates relationship: we're in this together. What mutual aid networks exist around you? What would you offer? What would you receive? Can you receive?
