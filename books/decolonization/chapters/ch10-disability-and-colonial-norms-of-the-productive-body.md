# Disability and Colonial Norms of the 'Productive' Body

*Body*

## The Pattern

The colonial body is the productive body. Disability — the body that doesn't conform to industrial productivity standards — becomes deficiency, tragedy, burden. Colonial institutions warehoused disabled people. Eugenic programs sterilized and killed them. The 'normal' body is a colonial invention — a statistical average produced in the 19th century to sort bodies into useful and useless, fit and unfit, worthy and disposable.

## What It Costs

Institutions, asylums, and mass incarceration of disabled people across centuries. Eugenic sterilization programs in the US, Canada, Scandinavia, and Nazi Germany — all drawing from the same colonial logic of human improvement. The ongoing violence of inaccessibility — not as oversight but as architecture that declares which bodies belong in public space. Internalized ableism: the sense that your worth is your productivity.

## What Persists

Disability justice — not disability rights (a legal framework) but disability justice (an intersectional, decolonial framework). Sins Invalid, Mia Mingus, Leah Lakshmi Piepzna-Samarasinha and the insistence that disabled, queer, brown and Black bodies are not broken. Interdependence as value rather than independence. Crip wisdom: the knowledge that comes from navigating a world not built for you. Many indigenous cultures had no concept of disability as deficiency.

## The Invitation

What if the problem was never the body? What if it was always the world that was built too narrow? What forms of intelligence, creativity, and wisdom emerge from bodies that colonial modernity calls broken?
