# The Myth of Objectivity

*Mind*

## The Pattern

Objectivity is the view from nowhere — or rather, the view from a very specific somewhere (European, male, propertied) that declared itself nowhere. The objective observer, the neutral scientist, the impartial judge — these figures are not universal. They are cultural products of a tradition that believed it could step outside culture altogether. Donna Haraway calls it the 'god trick': seeing everything from nowhere.

## What It Costs

Every knowledge tradition that centers relationship, positionality, and accountability gets dismissed as 'biased.' Indigenous knowledge is subjective. Women's knowledge is emotional. Black knowledge is political. Only Western scientific knowledge gets to be 'just knowledge.' The myth of objectivity doesn't eliminate perspective — it hides the dominant perspective and calls it universal.

## What Persists

Feminist standpoint theory. Indigenous research methodologies that begin with relationship and accountability rather than detachment. Sandra Harding's 'strong objectivity' that includes the observer's position. The growing recognition in physics itself (observer effect, quantum entanglement) that the observer cannot be separated from the observed. Every knowledge tradition that says 'I will tell you where I stand before I tell you what I see.'

## The Invitation

Where are you standing when you think you're being objective? Who taught you that standing there was the same as standing nowhere? The invitation is not to abandon rigor but to practice situated knowledge — to always name where you see from.
