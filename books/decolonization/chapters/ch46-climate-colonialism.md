# Climate Colonialism

*Planet*

## The Pattern

The nations most responsible for climate change are least affected by it. The nations least responsible are most affected. This is not irony — it's structure. The Global North industrialized using fossil fuels, accumulated wealth through carbon-intensive development, and now the Global South drowns, burns, and starves from the consequences. Carbon colonialism adds insult: carbon offset schemes where Northern polluters pay Southern communities to plant trees or not develop — essentially paying the colonized to absorb the colonizer's pollution.

## What It Costs

Small island nations disappearing. Bangladesh flooding. Sub-Saharan drought. Climate refugees who will never be called refugees. The Loss and Damage framework, fought for by the Global South at COP negotiations, perpetually resisted by the Global North. The obscenity of climate adaptation: asking the poorest nations to adapt to a crisis created by the richest. Climate change is not an equal-opportunity disaster. It is colonialism's atmosphere.

## What Persists

Climate justice movements that center the most affected. The Alliance of Small Island States (AOSIS) demanding accountability. Indigenous climate leadership — indigenous peoples protect 80% of the world's remaining biodiversity while comprising 5% of the population. The Loss and Damage fund finally established at COP27 (2022), however inadequately funded. The framing shift from 'climate change' to 'climate justice' — insisting that the crisis cannot be addressed without addressing its colonial structure.

## The Invitation

What is your carbon footprint? Now ask: what is your nation's historical carbon debt? The personal and the structural are not the same question, and answering only the personal is a way of avoiding the structural. Climate justice is not about your individual choices. It's about the system those choices exist within.
