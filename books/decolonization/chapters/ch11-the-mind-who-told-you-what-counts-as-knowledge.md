# The Mind: Who Told You What Counts as Knowledge?

*Domains*

## About

Colonization of the mind is the most durable form of colonization. Long after the soldiers leave, the categories remain. What counts as rational, what counts as evidence, what counts as a legitimate question, what counts as a real discipline — these are not neutral determinations. They are the residue of a specific European intellectual tradition that universalized itself by declaring all other ways of knowing primitive, pre-scientific, or simply nonexistent.

## The Invitation

Epistemicide — the killing of knowledge systems — may be colonialism's deepest violence. You can rebuild a burned building. How do you rebuild a way of knowing?

## The Colonial Logic

One knowledge system to rule them all. The Enlightenment as gift to the world rather than a particular cultural product with particular blind spots. Reason vs. superstition. Written vs. oral. Published vs. lived. Peer-reviewed vs. grandmother-tested. The university as temple of universal knowledge, built on stolen land and funded by colonial wealth.
