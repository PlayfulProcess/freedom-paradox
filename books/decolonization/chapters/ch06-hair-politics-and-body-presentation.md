# Hair Politics and Body Presentation

*Body*

## The Pattern

Hair is one of the most intimate sites of colonial control. Boarding schools cut indigenous children's hair as a first act of 'civilization.' Enslaved Africans were denied care for their hair. Professional dress codes encode Euro-centric hair standards as 'neat' and 'appropriate.' The natural hair of Black people — locs, braids, afros — has been deemed unprofessional, unkempt, threatening. Hair carries prayer, identity, mourning, status, beauty — which is exactly why it was targeted.

## What It Costs

Children's hair cut on their first day at residential schools, often while they cried, understanding the violation even if they couldn't name it. Generations of Black women and men chemically straightening hair to access employment and safety. The psychic tax of managing your body's natural expression to make others comfortable. The CROWN Act was needed in 2019 because in 2019 people were still being sent home from school and work for their hair.

## What Persists

Natural hair movements. Loc culture. Braiding circles that pass technique and story across generations. The CROWN Act. Indigenous hair as prayer — the understanding that hair connects you to spirit, to ancestors, to the earth. The growing refusal to alter your body's truth for colonial comfort.

## The Invitation

Hair seems small until you realize empires took it seriously enough to legislate. What does your hair mean to you? What would it mean if you'd been told your whole life that it was wrong?
