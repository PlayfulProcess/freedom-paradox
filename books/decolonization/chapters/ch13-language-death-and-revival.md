# Language Death and Revival

*Mind*

## The Pattern

A language dies every two weeks. Of the roughly 7,000 languages spoken today, half may be gone by century's end. This is not natural attrition. It's the result of policies — residential schools where children were beaten for speaking their language, national education systems that teach only in colonial languages, economic systems that reward English/French/Spanish/Portuguese and punish everything else. Language death is not the language failing. It's the language being killed.

## What It Costs

Each language is a complete cognitive framework — a way of perceiving time, relationship, causality, the sacred. Hopi has no tenses for past and future, structuring reality as manifest and manifesting. Lakota grammar embeds relationship into every sentence. Australian Aboriginal languages encode cardinal directions into spatial awareness. When a language dies, it's not just words that are lost — it's an entire way of organizing reality.

## What Persists

Hebrew was revived from a liturgical language to a living one — proof that language death is not always final. Hawaiian immersion schools. Māori language nests (kōhanga reo). Welsh medium education. Indigenous language apps. Master-apprentice programs where the last elderly speakers pass language to young learners one relationship at a time. The insistence that your language is not a hobby but a homeland.

## The Invitation

If you are monolingual in a colonial language, you are living in a house built by empire and thinking it's the whole world. What language did your ancestors dream in?
