# Decolonization: Body, Mind, Spirit, Collective, Planet

## A Grammar for Unlearning

**By [PlayfulProcess](https://playfulprocess.com) with Claude**

---

**Working thesis:** Modernity is dying and needs hospicing — not with despair or triumph, but wisdom (Andreotti). Decolonization is not a destination but an ongoing practice of noticing colonial patterns, naming their costs, honoring what persists, and sitting with complexity rather than seeking quick resolution.

**Structure:** Two source grammars merged into one book:

### Part I: The Tree of Coloniality (from "Gestures Towards a Balanced Future")
The botanical metaphor:
- **Roots:** Six separations (from land, body, ancestors, each other, mystery, death)
- **Trunk:** Five addictive patterns (intellect's authority, progress, helper's innocence, comfort entitlement, fantasy of clean breaks)
- **Canopy:** Five domains blocking light
- **Rotten fruits:** Burnout, loneliness, climate grief, performative allyship, unnamed wrongness
- **Seeds:** Reciprocity, grief-as-medicine, enough-ness, entanglement, not-knowing, slow time, playful irreverence

### Part II: Five Domains of Decolonization (from "Decolonization: Five Domains")
- **Body** (9 items): food, medicine, sovereignty, dance, hair, rest, labor, birth/death, disability
- **Mind** (10 items): epistemic violence, language, education, cognitive imperialism, oral tradition, universities, objectivity, time, research, unlearning
- **Spirit** (10 items): sacred land, appropriation, animism, ancestors, ceremony, Doctrine of Discovery, conversion, grief, plant medicine, nonhuman relations
- **Collective** (10 items): commons, mutual aid, nation-states, reparations, justice, development, infrastructure, solidarity, memory, indigenous governance
- **Planet** (8 items): extractivism, climate colonialism, rights of nature, monoculture, indigenous land management, water, sacrifice zones, conservation

### Supplementary: Decolonizing Childhood (from separate grammar)
- Thresholds, Learning, Sexual Education, Somatic/DBT — through Umbanda spirit voices

**Connection to other books:** The decolonial companion to Grammars of the Living World. Where Grammars diagnoses the colonial reflex abstractly (the five-move pattern), this book applies it concretely across every domain of life. Extends the separability thesis into embodied practice.
