# 69/31 Reframe Audit

## The Frame

**Book One: The 69%** — perpetual problems that don't resolve. Diagnosis. The discipline of learning to stop trying to solve the unsolvable.

**Book Two: The 31%** — what you can actually do. Action. Practice. Build. Fix. The discipline of acting where action works.

**The wisdom:** discernment between the two. Without a practice, everything looks perpetual. With one, you begin to see what moves.

## Key Insight: The Andreotti/Akomolafe Blind Spot

The complete rejection of othering can become avoidance of action. Some problems SHOULD be othered and solved. A tumor is not wholeness. A fudged benchmark is not a dilemma. When you refuse to distinguish between the solvable and the unsolvable, you enable perpetuation of solvable harm.

## Decision: No Chapter Moves Needed

The audit recommended moving Ch27-31 and Ch38 to Book One. **We chose not to** because:

1. Ch27-31: Committing to a tradition IS action (31%). The philosophical debate is perpetual (69%). The chapters hold both — which is the book's point.
2. Ch38: Ends with the Samudra Manthan — contain the poison. That's 31%.
3. Moving chapters would require massive renumbering, cross-reference updates, and SUMMARY changes.

Instead: **reframe in the transitions** (Ch26 ending, Ch41 opening) to name the 69/31 distinction explicitly.
