## TIER 1: HIGH-IMPACT INTEGRATIONS (change the argument)

These strengthen load-bearing claims or fill gaps that weaken credibility.

### 1.1 — Ant Death Spiral → Ch09 (The Safety Argument)
**Source:** Akomolafe appearances
**What:** Army ants follow pheromone trails. When a trail loops back on itself, the colony walks in a death spiral until exhaustion. Each ant is following the rules perfectly. The system kills them.
**Why:** Ch09 argues safety alignment erodes under competitive pressure. The death spiral is the visceral image the chapter lacks — a system where every individual actor is behaving rationally and the collective outcome is lethal.
**Insert near:** The RSP revision discussion. One paragraph, ~100 words.
**Risk:** Low. Additive, doesn't change existing argument.

### 1.2 — Bittersweet Thesis → Ch17 (The Darkness Is the Medicine)
**Source:** Susan Cain appearances
**What:** Sorrow and longing are sources of creativity, connection, and transcendence — not disorders to treat. Minor-key music activates pain AND reward circuits simultaneously. "Disenfranchised grief" = losses society doesn't recognize (paths not taken, developmental stages passing, the gap between world-as-is and world-as-could-be).
**Why:** Ch17's argument that darkness is medicine needs this empirical and emotional grounding. Cain provides the neuroscience (minor-key music), the taxonomy (disenfranchised grief), and the cultural diagnosis (positivity optimization as hearth-degradation mechanism). Currently the chapter makes the claim philosophically but lacks the research backbone.
**Insert:** Two passages. (a) Minor-key music neuroscience near the "graduated dosing" discussion. (b) Disenfranchised grief near the "what was lost when stories were sanitized" passage.
**Risk:** Low. Deepens existing argument.

### 1.3 — Hayes Attention-as-Labor → Ch08 (Value Creation: For Whom)
**Source:** Ezra Klein full transcripts
**What:** Attention has been commodified like labor in the Industrial Revolution — pooled, it's immensely valuable; individually, it's worthless. "Attention fracking" extracts from previously untouched reserves (sleep, childhood, grief). Political attention now outweighs money as currency.
**Why:** Ch08 asks "value creation: for whom?" but relies mostly on Zuckerberg's open-source motivation. Hayes provides the structural economic analysis — WHY attention became the commodity, not just that it did. The labor-commodification parallel gives the chapter historical depth.
**Insert near:** The platform economics discussion. One paragraph with the labor parallel, ~120 words.
**Risk:** Low. Additive.

### 1.4 — Hwang Business Model Path Dependence → Ch05/Ch08
**Source:** Ezra Klein full transcripts
**What:** Programmatic advertising is financialization, not continuation. Nanosecond auctions for demographic profiles. VCs refused subscription experiments because ad-supported scaling proved more attractive. Ad-supported media needs millions of readers; subscription needs thousands — structural incentive toward scale over depth.
**Why:** Explains WHY the open-source ecosystem got structured the way it did. The business model isn't accidental — it's path-dependent. This is the missing link between "platforms extract attention" (ch04-05) and "open source gets captured" (ch08).
**Insert near:** The discussion of how open-source infrastructure gets monetized.
**Risk:** Low. Fills explanatory gap.

### 1.5 — MacIntyre's Moral Collapse Genealogy → Ch24 (The Species That Got Fire)
**Source:** Philosophy/Naturalism research
**What:** MacIntyre's *After Virtue* argument: Enlightenment destroyed teleology → moral language became emotivist (expressing preferences, not describing reality) → we inherited moral fragments without the framework that gave them meaning. The "Linehan filter" posture (useful/fits data/compassionate) is either a recovery of teleological thinking or sophisticated emotivism.
**Why:** Ch24 already has the Kant section. MacIntyre provides the genealogy — WHY Kant's formal ethics ended up thin. This is the intellectual history behind "they built freedom without building the responsibility structures to hold it." It also stress-tests the three-filter test: is adaptive framing just emotivism with better marketing?
**Insert near:** The Enlightenment's Gift and Gap section. One paragraph framing MacIntyre, one paragraph acknowledging the emotivism challenge.
**Risk:** Medium. Must engage the stress test honestly without undermining the book's own method.

### 1.6 — Liming Loneliness Data → Ch18 (When Stories Lose Their Homes)
**Source:** Ezra Klein full transcripts
**What:** Americans' close friends declined 25 points 1990-2021. 79% young adults vs 41% seniors report loneliness. Nuclear family is 15-year aberration. Loneliness is an outcome, not a malady — structural production of isolation. "Hanging out" as social musculature that atrophies.
**Why:** Ch18 argues stories lose their homes but relies mostly on cultural analysis. Liming provides the hard data and the structural argument — loneliness isn't a feeling problem, it's an infrastructure problem.
**Insert near:** The section on what was lost when communal storytelling spaces disappeared.
**Risk:** Low. Empirical grounding for existing claim.

## TIER 3: CONFLICT FLAGS (require careful handling, not just insertion)

These aren't simple additions. They stress-test the book's framework and require honest engagement.

### 3.1 — Akomolafe's 69/31 Challenge
**The tension:** Akomolafe never provides criteria for distinguishing solvable from unsolvable problems. His position is that the distinction itself IS the trap. The book's 69/31 frame is a direct departure from Akomolafe.
**Resolution:** Acknowledge in Ch39 (The Grammar We Need). One paragraph: "Akomolafe would say the 69/31 distinction reproduces the solving reflex at a meta-level. He may be right. This book makes the distinction anyway — not because it has resolved the recursion but because the alternative (treating everything as perpetual) enables the perpetuation of solvable harm."
**Chapter:** Ch39, near the trap-of-solving discussion.

### 3.2 — Akomolafe on Technology
**The tension:** Akomolafe discusses technology only as sites of concern, never allies. His position: technology is already captured by whiteness-as-order and cannot be trusted for decolonial outcomes. This directly challenges the book's commitment to open-source technology as responsibility infrastructure.
**Resolution:** Already partially addressed in Ch39's light/shadow framing of recursive.eco. Could add one sentence acknowledging: "Akomolafe would say even naming the shadows reproduces the colonial reflex of management. The book proceeds anyway, shadows named, recursion unresolved."
**Chapter:** Ch39 light/shadow passage.

### 3.3 — Harris's Sufficiency-of-Reason Challenge
**The tension:** Harris genuinely believes rational, evidence-based inquiry can determine values WITHOUT communal grammars. This is the most important secular challenge to the book's thesis. Harris's own Waking Up app is precisely a container/grammar while arguing theoretically that grammars are unnecessary. He's built exactly what the book describes while denying its necessity.
**Resolution:** Acknowledge in Ch24 or Ch38 (What AI Actually Is). "Harris models a path from rational inquiry to contemplative practice that appears to require no communal grammar. But the Waking Up app — with its curated teachers, its structured curriculum, its community features — is a grammar by another name. The question is not whether individual practice works for individuals. It is whether individual practice scales to civilizational challenges without the coordination structures that grammars provide."
**Chapter:** Ch38, near the intelligence-vs-wisdom discussion.

### 3.4 — Andreotti's "Not Yet Ready to Build"
**The tension:** Andreotti says we're in the composting phase, not ready to build. The book builds anyway (recursive.eco, Working Architecture, etc.).
**Resolution:** Already addressed via Rowson's "this move is forced, even if it loses" in Ch39. No new text needed — but verify the Rowson passage survived all edits.

### 3.5 — Introvert Container Design (Cain)
**The tension:** If containers are framed as inherently communal, the book alienates a third to half of the population. Silence, solitude, and low-stimulation containers are equally valid forms.
**Resolution:** Add to Ch35 (The Family as Grammar) or Ch34 (Listening, Playing, Making): one paragraph acknowledging that not all containers are noisy or social. The Quaker meeting's silence, the solo walk in nature, the child reading alone in a room where someone else is present — these are containers too. The defining feature is not social density but relational availability.
**Chapter:** Ch34, near the practice boxes.

### 3.6 — Gottman Four Horsemen Qualification (Doherty)
**The tension:** Doherty: Gottman's four horsemen research comes from untreated couples. Contempt doesn't predict divorce for couples in therapy. The book uses the 69/31 finding and Gottman extensively.
**Resolution:** This is important but narrow. The book's use of Gottman is primarily the 69/31 finding and the masters-vs-disasters distinction, NOT the four horsemen predictive model. Verify which Gottman claims appear in the book and whether any need qualification. If only 69/31 and masters/disasters, no change needed — Doherty's critique doesn't touch those findings.
**Action:** Grep for "four horsemen" and "contempt" across chapters. Qualify if found.

### 3.7 — Polyvagal Theory (cross-file convergence)
**The tension:** Already addressed this session — Ch14 disclaimer strengthened, caveats added to ch15/ch34.
**Resolution:** DONE. No further action.

### 3.8 — Goldstein's "Mattering Instinct" vs. Inherent Value
**The tension:** For strict naturalists, mattering is a creature-feature (something brains do), not a reality-feature (something the universe contains). The book's stronger claim — that inherent value is real, not just useful — needs to acknowledge this.
**Resolution:** Brief note in Ch26 (The Axiom): "Rebecca Goldstein's 'mattering instinct' offers a naturalist-friendly bridge — the claim that the impulse to matter is evolved, real, and normative even if not metaphysically grounded. A strict naturalist stops here. This book goes further — but the further step is the axiom, not the argument."
**Chapter:** Ch26, near the Tillich-Wallis resolution.
