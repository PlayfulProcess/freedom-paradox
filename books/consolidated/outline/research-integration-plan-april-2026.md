## AUTHOR NOTES (incorporated from review)

- **Title:** Consider "Fire Before Responsibility" as title or subtitle
- **Core reframe:** The problem is the human condition, not modernity. We were animals in balance. We got intelligence. Now we must surpass it — grow our capacity for responsibility to match our capacity for power. Andreotti's framing of "modernity" as the problem implies pre-modern or post-modern solutions exist. They don't. The gap between capacity and responsibility is permanent and structural.
- **Near enemies (Wallis):** Drugification of connection is a near enemy — looks like the real thing, counterfeits it. Frame 2.1 through this lens.
- **Harris:** Would agree on importance of telling good stories and open society values. His objection is about stories becoming dogma — identifying make-believe as truth. The book mostly passes this test (Ch15, Ch24 methodology) but needs tightening in Ch30 (invisible world) and Ch40 (honest practitioner). See Harris structural audit below.
- **Akomolafe:** User sees immobilization as the practical failure of "don't solve." Change and the will to change ARE part of accepting what is. Fugitive hypothesis is Akomolafe's own solution — an incongruence in his thought. Lean Wallis + couples therapy research over his mythopoetic-as-truth direction.
- **Andreotti "not yet ready to build":** How can she know? She says "hospicing to give birth" — eventually you do give birth. Haidt went out and wrote an urgent book because it was useful. Change is happening. Waiting for the universe is not responsibility.
- **Introvert containers:** recursive.eco ecosystem is well-suited for introverts. Diversity of container types is itself the asset.

## HARRIS STRUCTURAL AUDIT (from analysis)

The book **passes** the Harris test in Ch15 (epistemological framing) and Ch24 (methodology note). Three weak spots:

1. **Ch24 lines 40-51:** Invasion ecology presented as literal fact ("the tannins are literal, not metaphorical") before the adaptive-frame disclaimer. Reverse the order — or add a bridge sentence making clear this is data interpreted through the adaptive lens.
2. **Ch30 lines 37-43:** Leaps from "we can't perceive 95% of reality" to "therefore traditional invisible relationships are warranted." Needs a bridge: the practices are warranted by their outcomes, not by the reality of the entities.
3. **Ch40 lines 131-132:** "The invisible is real" is an ontological claim the book hasn't earned empirically. Soften to: "The invisible may be real. The practices that relate to it demonstrably produce adaptive outcomes. The honest practitioner holds both."

**Action:** Fix all three as part of Batch 3. Small surgical edits, not rewrites.
