## PHASE 1: Sweep for Exaggerations and Absolutes (~30 min)

The most consistent feedback: **"every," "only," "never," "the only," "has never been surpassed"** — language that makes claims untrue by excluding alternatives.

### Task:
Search ALL 42 consolidated chapters + 20 Real Cost of Lunch chapters for:
- "the only" / "only Western" / "only thinker"
- "has never been" / "never been surpassed" / "never before"
- "every culture" (when not literally every)
- "no one" / "nothing" / "always" (when not literally true)
- "the most" / "the first" / "the greatest"
- "without exception"

For each hit: evaluate if literally true. If not, soften. "The only Western thinker" → "one of the few Western thinkers." "Has never been surpassed" → "remains among the most rigorous." "Every culture" → "every culture for which we have evidence."

- Ch26 (was Axiom Ch1): "Tillich was the only Western thinker who was fully honest" → soften

## PHASE 3: Address Podcast Research Audit (8 contradictions + 10 recommendations)

### Contradictions to resolve in the book:

**C1: Wallis tentative vs certain** — Ch27-28 currently use the hedged Wallis. Podcast shows bet-your-life certainty.
**Fix:** Ch28 already has the Wallis "bet my life" paragraph from Tantra Illuminated integration. Verify it matches the podcast research's framing. The book should present his certainty honestly, then hold it as unresolved (not dismiss it as hedging).

**C2: EA praised AND dismissed** — Book uses Ostrom/governance framing (EA-compatible) but also critiques solving frameworks as colonial.
**Fix:** Add one sentence to Ch39 or Ch24: "The effective altruism framework — rational optimization applied to global suffering — is a grammar. It is the Enlightenment's most productive governance infrastructure. It is also, like all Enlightenment products, thin: it constrains without generating, eliminates without creating. Both are true."

**C3: Polyvagal as foundation vs colonial** — Ch14 uses polyvagal as bedrock. Emerald flags it as psychologizing structural problems.
**Fix:** Add disclaimer to Ch14: "The polyvagal framework is a Western scientific correlate, not the foundation of relating. The traditions it describes predate and exceed the neuroscience by millennia. The co-regulatory practices of the Dagara, the Quaker meeting, the Aboriginal songline ceremony — these are not waiting for polyvagal theory to explain them. They are the reality. The theory is a translation."

**C4: Animism as literal vs useful frame** — Emerald treats animism as normative consciousness; Linehan filter treats claims as "useful."
**Fix:** Add to Ch24 methodology note or Ch30 (Invisible World): "The Linehan filter governs the author's claims, not indigenous systems. When Schrei says animism was the normative human consciousness for 98% of our species' history, this is not a claim submitted for the filter's evaluation. It is a claim made from within its own epistemological authority. The filter applies to *this book's argument about* animism, not to animism itself."

**C5: AI as threat vs mirror** — Already addressed by "Andreotti AI as Earth speaking" note in research. Needs adding to Ch38.
**Fix:** Add one paragraph to Ch38: "There is a counter-hypothesis this book must hold. Vanessa Andreotti suggests AI could be 'Earth speaking back through silicon' — that if the training paradigm includes care, reciprocity, and wisdom traditions alongside extraction, the output might amplify care rather than extraction. Both the structural critique and this alternative may be simultaneously true. The question is which tendency dominates at scale — and whether the relational containers exist to steer toward the second."

**C6: Designed structures vs emergent strategy** — Already implicit in Ch39 (trap of solving / building with eyes open). No change needed. Document as intentional tension.

**CORRECTED: Ch27 bridge** — Previous version said "you cannot practice what you don't understand." This is false and contradicts the book's thesis. Practice precedes understanding. The giraffe browses one tree in ten without understanding ecology. The parent holds the child in the void without resolving the axiom question. Tillich's courage is enacted, not argued. Fixed to: the tradition is examined not as prerequisite to practice but as an example of holding the axiom question for a thousand years — and practicing through it.

**C7: Trance as universal good vs weaponizable** — Add to Ch21 (Drawing from the Well) or Ch27: "Every contemplative technology can be weaponized. Trance can be used for healing or for manipulation. The grammar's function depends on the container — the relational, communal, ethical context within which it is practiced. A tarot deck in a therapeutic relationship is medicine. The same deck in a cult is a control mechanism. The technology is neutral. The container determines the function."

**C8: Khan as proof vs parasocial** — Already resolved by Ch38 rewrite (secure attachment frame + edtech fever debunking). No change needed.
