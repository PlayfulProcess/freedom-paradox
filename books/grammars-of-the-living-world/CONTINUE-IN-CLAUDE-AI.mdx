## The Book

**Title:** Grammars of the Living World
**License:** CC BY-SA 4.0
**Working thesis:** We are living through a crisis of mythic belonging — we have inherited stories calibrated for a world that no longer exists and media systems that structurally prevent us from creating shared stories for the world we live in. The impulse to solve this crisis may itself be part of the crisis. What remains possible is not a solution but a gesture — toward commons, toward practice, toward more adaptive futures.

## Architecture (9 chapters, 4 parts)

### PART I: THE CRISIS
- **Ch 1: When Stories Lose Their Homes** — Context is constitutive of meaning. Hansel & Gretel (1812 biological mother → 1857 stepmother), the gingerbread house paradox (starvation trap then, child's Tuesday now), Winnie the Pooh (Milne's PTSD → stuffed animals behind glass at NYPL), Benjamin's aura (cult value → exhibition value), Hicks on Benin Bronzes, mythic displacement ≠ cultural appropriation. **RESEARCH COMPLETE. READY TO DRAFT.**
- **Ch 2: The Degradation of the Hearth** — Fire → television → personal screen. Each step preserved some functions, destroyed others. Mr. Rogers as proof of concept (the medium is not the problem; the design is). Christakis on joint attention. Harris/Zuboff on attention economy. **RESEARCH COMPLETE. READY TO DRAFT.**
- **Ch 3: The Polarization of the Polis** — Fragmentation of family narrative mirrors political fragmentation. Anderson, Putnam, Gottman's 69%, Haidt, Talmudic chavruta. **NEEDS ONE MORE RESEARCH SESSION — vivid opening case + deeper Habermas/Warner.**

### PART II: THE GRAMMARS
- **Ch 4: Machines for Constructing Stories** — I Ching, Ifá, Tarot, Kabbalah as "grammars" — finite symbolic vocabularies generating infinite meaning. Five properties. Calvino. Bohm. Convergence across civilizations. **EXISTING DRAFT — needs adaptation.**
- **Ch 5: Survival** — Qin burning (I Ching survived as "divination manual"), Ifá oral transmission across Middle Passage, tarot's structural openness, Maya codices as counterexample. **EXISTING DRAFT — minimal changes.**
- **Ch 6: Tarot's Double Life** — Colonial capture (Court de Gébelin, Lévi, Golden Dawn, Blavatsky, Smith's erasure) AND liberation (Marmolejo, Road, Dore, Pollack, HCI scholarship). Openness as both vulnerability and emancipation. **EXISTING DRAFT — merge two old chapters.**
- **Ch 7: The Living Cosmos** — Grammars are alive, not dead symbol systems. Jyotish (Grahas as living beings, Nakshatras, Dashamahavidya), Wallis's nondual Shaiva Tantra (Cit-Shakti, pratyabhijña), phenomenology of attending to Saturn. Design implication: contemplative mirrors, not predictive instruments. **EXISTING DRAFT — needs vivid personal opening.**

### PART III: THE HINGE
- **Ch 8: The Trap of Solving** — Modernity's reflex: problem → solution. Andreotti (hospicing modernity), Akomolafe (psychology creates reality), but lead with Tillich (God above God), Wallis (svātantrya-śakti), Palmer (functional atheism). Kriya vs. karma. Cosmic topology metaphor. A gesture, not a fix. **HEAVY RESTRUCTURE from existing material.**

### PART IV: THE GESTURE
- **Ch 9: Grammars of the Living World** — Kelty's recursive public modulated for contemplative practice. Linehan's DBT framing (effective, fits evidence, compassionate). The specific architecture: no ads, no algorithm, no tracking. Fred Rogers as design philosopher. "If we succeed, we will disappear." Ends with invitation to practice, not answers. **EXISTING DRAFT — moderate restructure.**

## Writing Style
- Philosophical memoir grounded in scholarship. A writer thinking in public, not an expert delivering conclusions.
- The Mr. Rogers emotional register: warm, serious, respectful, unwilling to simplify.
- Analytical but accessible. Concrete examples over abstractions. Short paragraphs. No academic throat-clearing.
- Each chapter closes with unanswered questions (except Ch 9, which ends with practice).
- The equity analyst instinct: who benefits from this narrative?
- Respect CARE Principles for Indigenous Data when discussing Ifá, Indigenous systems.
