# Research Session: The Container Is Disappearing (Species Ch 5)

## Context
This is research for Chapter 5 of *The Species That Tells Stories*. The chapter documents how the five containment conditions from Chapter 1 are being systematically removed — not by anyone's plan, but as the emergent consequence of optimizing for engagement rather than development.

## What I Need

### The Three Great Transitions
- **Fire → Television → Personal screen**: each preserved content while degrading relational infrastructure
- The fire had the storyteller's body, the community gathered, the stars overhead
- Television had the family gathered but the storyteller absent — research on family TV co-viewing patterns (1950s-2000s)
- The personal screen has neither — when did average age of first smartphone drop below 10?
- Screen time data: average daily screen time for children by age group (current data)

### The Algorithmic Feed as Structural Inversion of the Bedtime Story
- Optimization for engagement instead of development — how recommendation algorithms work
- Infinite scroll instead of bounded narrative — who invented infinite scroll? When?
- Solitary consumption instead of co-regulatory presence — percentage of children's screen time that is solo
- Escalation instead of graduated dosing — how algorithms escalate arousal for engagement
- The business model: attention extraction requires removing containment features

### Pornography Exposure Data (held carefully, without moral panic)
- 93% of boys, 63% of girls before 18 — source and date of this finding
- Average first exposure age 12 — source
- 58% unintentional — source
- Mori et al. 2023 meta-analysis: association with problematic sexual behaviors (16,200 participants, 27 studies) — exact findings
- 57% of children who encounter pornography tell no one — source
- Frame as containment failure, not content problem: the content has always existed, what's new is it arrives without elder, community, graduation, co-present regulated nervous system

### The Micro-Dosing Effect
- Algorithmic content normalizing radical material in small entertaining doses
- The manosphere targeting boys who feel isolated — specific pipeline research
- 2023 Science Advances study: extremist content reaches primarily users who already seek it (important nuance — algorithms rarely radicalize innocents)
- The structural exploitation of developmental vulnerability: adolescent identity formation, need for belonging
- Andrew Tate case study: reach metrics, demographic of audience, how algorithmic amplification worked

### Exposure Without Containment
- In the exposure therapy framework: exposure without a therapist is not therapy
- The five containment conditions mapped to each delivery system:
  - Bedtime story: preserves all 5
  - Family movie: preserves most
  - Solo screen with bounded content: preserves some
  - Algorithmic feed: preserves none
- Research on co-viewing effects: does parental presence during screen time change outcomes?

### Resilience
- Resilience is real — but resilience itself is a product of prior containment
- Research on protective factors: which children are most vulnerable to uncontained digital exposure?
- The algorithm exploits the gap love left — children who already lack relational containment are most vulnerable
- This is not "screens are bad" — it is a precise analysis of which containment features each system preserves

## Tone
Compassionate, not alarmist. Parents are overwhelmed, not negligent. The system is designed to exploit attention. The chapter should leave parents feeling informed and empowered, not shamed.
