# Research Session: What the New Stories Carry (Species Ch 4)

## Context
This is research for Chapter 4 of *The Species That Tells Stories*. The chapter evaluates modern children's stories through the three-filter test — not as a critic but as a parent asking "what is this story doing to my child's nervous system?"

## What I Need

### Frozen (2013, 2019)
- The full narrative arc vs. the cultural extraction of "Let It Go"
- Evidence that suppression fails, isolated self-expression fails, love/sacrifice heals
- Anna's sacrifice as the turning point — co-regulation science in animated form
- The cultural reception inverting the meaning: "Let It Go" became an anthem of individual liberation, but the story says the opposite
- The merchandising of the crisis moment (ice palace on every lunchbox, not Anna's sacrifice)
- Box office, streaming, merchandise revenue data
- Frozen 2: Elsa discovers indigenous Northuldra people, colonial wrong (the dam). Does it address what Frozen 1 ignored?

### Encanto (2021)
- Family systems theory made accessible to billions of children
- Intergenerational trauma: Abuela's displacement as origin of her rigidity
- "We Don't Talk About Bruno" becoming the most-streamed Disney song in history — the culture choosing a song about family silencing and the return of the repressed
- Nobody is the villain. The system needs healing, not the individual
- The house cracking as a metaphor for family dysfunction
- Scholarly or therapeutic analysis of Encanto's family systems accuracy

### Moana (2016)
- Te Ka is Te Fiti wounded — the destroyer is the creator in pain
- Recognition rather than violence as the healing mechanism
- The Oceanic Story Trust — genuine cultural collaboration AND structural asymmetry. How it worked. Who was on it. What they changed. What they couldn't change
- Ecological responsibility encoded as sacred obligation
- Box office, cultural impact in Pacific Island communities

### Miyazaki / Studio Ghibli
- Films optimized for the child's developmental experience, not franchise value
- My Neighbor Totoro, Spirited Away, Princess Mononoke — specific analysis
- Ecological embeddedness without moralizing
- Cultural specificity as the vehicle of universality
- Slower pacing, self-contained narratives, limited merchandising
- Miyazaki's philosophy on children, darkness, and what stories owe their audience
- Contrast with Disney's franchise model: Ghibli's deliberate refusal to sequel/merchandise

### Disney's Economic Infrastructure
- The $94.4B operation, Walt's 1957 synergy map, Iger's $86.5B in acquisitions
- How franchise logic (merchandise, sequels, theme parks) systematically extracts crisis moments and suppresses resolutions
- The structural tension between adaptive storytelling and profit optimization
- Disney+ streaming data and how algorithmic delivery changes the containment conditions

### Fred Rogers & Daniel Tiger
- Rogers' philosophy: "Love is at the root of everything — all learning, all parenting, all relationships"
- The no-merchandise model and why it worked
- Angela Santomero's Daniel Tiger — prosocial effects contingent on parental co-viewing
- The research: Daniel Tiger's effects disappear when children watch alone (the love IS the mechanism)
- CPB funding dissolved in 2026 — what this means for public media storytelling

### The Evolution
- Disney moving from romantic rescue (Snow White, 1937) to relational repair (Frozen/Encanto/Moana, 2013-2021)
- Evidence this is a genuine adaptive shift in mass mythology
- What it means: the species CAN create better stories at scale
- What it doesn't mean: the commercial apparatus still undermines the adaptive message

## Three-Filter Test Application
For each story: Is it useful (builds adaptive capacity)? Does it fit the data (world it describes corresponds to child's world)? Is it compassionate (reduces suffering without generating new suffering)?

## Tone
Appreciative, not dismissive. These films have brought joy and genuine insight to billions of children. The critique is structural (commercial apparatus undermines adaptive message), not artistic (the stories themselves are remarkably well-crafted).
