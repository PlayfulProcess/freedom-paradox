# Research Session: What We Protect and Why (Species Ch 6)

## Context
This is research for Chapter 6 of *The Species That Tells Stories*. The chapter examines the biology of taboo — not as moral authority but as empirical discernment. Which protections track real biology? Which are cultural constructs? And where does a genuine biological signal get culturally inflated into a program of shame?

## What I Need

### The Westermarck Effect
- Shepher's kibbutzim study: children raised together in communal settings almost never married each other, even when there was no biological relation
- Wolf's shim-pua data: 15,000 couples in Taiwan where girls were adopted into future husband's family as infants — dramatically higher divorce rates, fewer children
- Lieberman's dual-cue kin-detection model: co-residence duration + maternal perinatal association
- Shor & Simchai's 2009 revision — what did they update?
- Key point: incest avoidance is not culturally constructed. It has biological substrate. The Westermarck effect operates across cultures without requiring explicit teaching.

### HPA Axis Vulnerability in Children
- Evidence that sexual activation of prepubescent nervous systems causes measurable HPA axis dysregulation
- Cross-cultural consistency of this finding (not a Western cultural artifact)
- The developmental window: what changes at puberty that makes sexual content processable?
- Research on cortisol, stress response in sexually abused children

### Comprehensive Sex Education vs. Abstinence-Only
- Stanger-Hall 2011: abstinence-only education positively correlated with teen pregnancy rates
- Kohler 2008: comprehensive sex education reduces teen pregnancy by 60%
- The three-filter test: comprehensive sex ed passes all three (useful, fits data, compassionate). Abstinence-only fails all three.
- International comparison: Netherlands model vs. US model — outcomes data

### The Three-Layer Model
- **Layer 1: Biological substrate** — Westermarck, HPA vulnerability. Cross-culturally consistent, adaptive
- **Layer 2: Cultural constructs** — food taboos, nudity norms, same-sex contact prohibitions. Variable, serving cultural rather than biological functions
- **Layer 3: Biological signal culturally inflated** — the Puritan move. Child-protection mechanism expanded into comprehensive bodily shame
- Donald Brown's human universals (~400 societies, hundreds of universals) — which taboos are universal?
- Haidt's moral foundations — the purity/disgust mechanism as biological substrate culturally elaborated

### Freedom of Speech as Paradoxical Adaptive Resource
- Short-term harm (harmful stories circulate) vs. long-term adaptation (new voices enter the culture)
- Monocultures collapse, whether ecological or mythic
- The evolutionary argument for narrative diversity: we need more stories, not fewer
- But communities need capacity to curate their own narrative ecosystems
- Not state censorship. Not algorithmic free-for-all. Community self-governance — the way every culture did it for 60,000 years until the algorithm took over
- Ostrom's commons governance applied to narrative ecosystems

## Tone
Scientifically careful, not morally certain. "We don't fully know" applies here especially. The naturalistic fallacy (biology doesn't equal morality) must be explicitly acknowledged.
