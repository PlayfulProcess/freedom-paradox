# Research Session: The Stories We Might Tell (Species Ch 7)

## Context
This is research for Chapter 7 of *The Species That Tells Stories*. The final full chapter — not a prescription but a set of structural features drawn from everything the research revealed. What would adaptive stories look like? Can you design a myth, or must myths emerge from lived practice?

## What I Need

### Fred Rogers as North Star
- Full philosophy: "Love is at the root of everything — all learning, all parenting, all relationships"
- The sweater ritual as transitional space (Winnicott connection)
- Direct address, slow pace, emotional naming — what made his approach work developmentally?
- Angela Santomero's research on Daniel Tiger: prosocial effects contingent on parental co-viewing
- The mechanism: the love relationship IS the active ingredient, not the content alone
- Rogers' deliberate refusal of merchandising — the economic structure that protected the adaptive function
- CPB dissolution in 2026 — what happened and what it means for publicly funded children's storytelling

### Sesame Workshop's Global Localization Model
- 150+ countries with locally adapted versions
- How the educational curriculum is adapted to local contexts (nutrition in some countries, conflict resolution in others)
- The "mirror/window" framework for representation
- Sesame Street in refugee camps — specific programs, outcomes data
- Is this the best existing model of adaptive storytelling at scale?

### Can You Design a Myth?
- The honest tension: myths emerge from lived communal practice, not from design
- But conditions can be created: communities empowered to curate their own narrative ecosystems
- Ostrom's 8 design principles for commons governance — applied to narrative
- Tools that support intergenerational storytelling
- Design that optimizes for development rather than engagement

### The Seven Structural Features of Adaptive Stories
Evidence for each:
1. **Relational mediation** — shared between people, not consumed alone (all the co-viewing research)
2. **Developmental optimization** — serves child's growth, not platform metrics (Waldorf staging, Vygotsky's ZPD)
3. **Bounded structure** — beginning, middle, end, resolution (narrative arc research)
4. **Ecological embeddedness** — human as participant, not master (Miyazaki, Dreamtime, Kimmerer)
5. **Relational repair over individual heroism** — Frozen/Encanto model over old Disney model
6. **Cultural specificity as vehicle of universality** — Ghibli's Japaneseness, Encanto's Colombianness
7. **Honest darkness** — the scary parts, the unfair parts (exposure therapy framework from Ch 2)

### Recursive.eco as Gesture
- Not as solution but as gesture: "We don't know what stories your community needs. But we can give you tools to find out together — and we promise not to optimize for your attention while you do."
- Design principles: no notifications, no autoplay, no engagement optimization
- Community curation rather than algorithmic recommendation
- Contemplation-designed rather than engagement-designed

### The Economics of Mythic Infrastructure
- Disney's $94.4B: what does it cost to produce adaptive mythology at scale?
- Rogers' no-merchandise model — economically viable only with public funding
- Daniel Tiger's necessary commercial adaptation (PBS + merchandising)
- Homer funded by the choregoi system (state-sponsored civic therapy)
- The Grimms funded by scholarly publishing
- Every story system that lasted had a funding mechanism
- The question: "does the monetization distort or sustain the adaptive function?"

### Three Structural Protections
1. Mission-locking — purpose encoded in structure, not dependent on leaders
2. Editorial-commercial separation — content decisions separate from revenue decisions
3. Diversified funding — no single revenue stream can capture editorial function

## Tone
Generative, hopeful, humble. "We don't fully know what stories our future ancestors need. But we know that love is the container, darkness is the medicine, relationship is the mechanism, and community is the author."
