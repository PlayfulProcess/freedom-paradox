# Chapter 8: What Holds

---

This chapter is the book's filter — the methodology for distinguishing cooperative strategies from extractive ones, applied to civilization's major moral claims. The filter is the Linehan test, reframed through game theory.

**Useful** means: produces cooperative outcomes that sustain the system over multiple iterations.

**Fits the data** means: matches what we observe in systems that persist. Cooperative systems maintain diversity. Extractive systems simplify and collapse.

**Compassionate** means: maintains the conditions for future adaptation — including the survival of alternative strategies. Because monoculture is fragile and diversity is resilient. Compassion in this frame is not sentimental. It is strategic. Preserving cultural diversity is the equivalent of maintaining a seed bank. You do it not because every seed is beautiful but because you do not know which one you will need next.

---

## The stress tests

Each of civilization's major moral claims, evaluated not as good or bad but as cooperative or extractive in practice.

**"The world is broken and needs repair."** Cooperative dimension: generates collective action, motivates sacrifice, creates solidarity. Extractive dimension: authorizes savior classes who extract moral authority from the brokenness of others. The brokenness premise is the root of white saviorism because it creates a permanent asymmetry between the repairers (who are whole) and the broken (who need fixing). The game-theoretic critique: a strategy that requires others to be deficient in order for you to have a role is parasitic, not cooperative. **Verdict:** useful as a motivating story, dangerous as an identity.

**"Act according to universalizable maxims."** Cooperative dimension: Kant's formal principle is a genuine cooperative constraint — don't free-ride, don't deceive, don't treat others as instruments. These are anti-defection rules. They work. O'Neill and Korsgaard are right that the principle is cross-cultural. Extractive dimension: the model of moral agency (individual reasoning in isolation) is an extractive topology. It displaces communal moral reasoning — Ifá, Quaker meeting, elder council — and concentrates authority in the philosopher. The universal logic is the Trojan horse. Inside it is a particular model of who counts as a moral reasoner. **Verdict:** the constraint is genuine. The model of agency is the payload. You need both: the constraint (don't defect) and the communal grammar (how to cooperate). The categorical imperative is a constraint. Grammars are the content.

**"Maximize well-being for the greatest number."** Cooperative dimension: useful for policy coordination, resource allocation, cost-benefit analysis. Extractive dimension: can justify sacrificing minorities for majority benefit — treating people as resources to be optimized. The utilitarian calculus has no structural protection for the minority whose suffering is "outweighed." **Verdict:** a cooperative tool that becomes extractive without structural constraints protecting those who are counted as costs rather than benefits.

**"All men are created equal."** Cooperative dimension: a powerful cooperative aspiration that has expanded rights to previously excluded groups across three centuries. Extractive dimension: "freedom" as implemented has meant freedom to extract. "Equality" meant equality among property-owning European men. The cooperative language concealed extractive practice. Mignolo: there is no modernity without coloniality. **Verdict:** the aspiration is genuine. Its implementation has been the extractive strategy in cooperative clothing.

**"Everything is interconnected."** Cooperative dimension: the most empirically supported cooperative framework. Biology confirms it. Ecology confirms it. Neuroscience confirms it. Extractive dimension: can enable spiritual bypassing — using the cooperative language to avoid accountability for specific extractive behaviors. "We're all one" can mean "your suffering is not my problem because duality is illusion." **Verdict:** true and dangerous — true because interconnection is empirically confirmed, dangerous because the truth can be weaponized as an excuse for inaction.

**"Bodies in parasympathetic states relate better."** This book's hypothesis. Cooperative dimension: builds infrastructure for cooperation at the nervous system level. Everything in the first chapter. Extractive dimension: "we know what regulated looks like" becomes a new form of authority capture. The therapist who diagnoses dysregulation in the client is performing Move 2 of the colonial reflex — universalizing a particular model of what health looks like. **Verdict:** this book must be honest about this. The three-filter test is itself a particular strategy. It emerged from a specific clinical context. It privileges empiricism in ways that may marginalize other knowledge systems. The filter is a tool, not a truth. It is held with open hands.

## What survives the filter

Not any single system but a pattern — observable across every system that persists.

The direction of life: toward more connection, more exchange, more mutual constraint, more complexity. Mycorrhizal networks do it. Coral reefs do it. Old-growth forests do it. The mitochondrion that was once a parasite became the engine of every complex cell on earth — not by conquering the host but by entering a cooperative arrangement so deep that neither can live without the other.

Lynn Margulis: "Life did not take over the globe by combat, but by networking."

This is not a moral claim. It is an empirical observation about which strategies produce durability. The cooperative strategy — power matched by responsibility, extraction balanced by reciprocity, the shadow of the future long enough that today's defection carries tomorrow's consequences — is the strategy that builds complexity. The extractive strategy — power without constraint, profit without reciprocity, the future discounted to zero — is the strategy that simplifies until it collapses.

The evidence has been accumulating for 3.8 billion years. The cooperative direction of life is not an aspiration. It is a pattern. The question is whether the species that got fire can recognize the pattern and align with it before the consequences of thirteen thousand years of extraction foreclose the possibility.
