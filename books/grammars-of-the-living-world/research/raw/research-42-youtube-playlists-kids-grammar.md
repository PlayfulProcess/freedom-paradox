## Relevance to Grammars of the Living World

**Primary chapters:** Ch 4 (Degradation of Hearth), Ch 1 (Biological Hypothesis)

**Key insight:** "The best screen time is shared screen time." This grammar enacts the co-regulation thesis in practice. Every playlist description invites the parent to watch *with* the child — "Adults are encouraged to join in," "parents might secretly enjoy too," "Watch, sing, and reflect together." The grammar doesn't just curate content; it structures the relational practice of co-viewing.

**Connection to research-31 (Co-regulation Science):** The 2024 JAMA Pediatrics meta-analysis found that co-use and educational programming buffered developmental risks while solo use exacerbated them. The critical variable: presence of a co-regulating human. This grammar is designed to produce exactly that condition.

**Connection to Disney Decolonial Tree:** Item 12 ("The Hidden Aspects of Ballet") raises the composting question from inside a practice the parent loves — not cancellation but honest inquiry: "I love Ballet. My daughter does too... but sometimes I wonder if it is an instrument of colonization and patriarchy." This is the adaptive frame applied to screen time itself.

**Connection to Ch 10 (Grammars of the Living World):** This grammar demonstrates Layer 2 of the platform — shared symbolic structures created by people, not algorithms. A parent curating playlists for their family is a grammar-maker. The platform provides infrastructure for that practice.
