## Methodology

### The Experiment

This book does NOT start from astrology and find confirming history. It starts from history and tests whether the astrological framework maps onto it. The methodology:

1. **Select** a historical moment from the colonial/decolonial arc
2. **Describe** the event in its own terms (political, economic, cultural)
3. **Hypothesize** what planetary transits *should* be present based on astrological theory
4. **Check** the actual ephemeris for that date
5. **Report** the result — match, partial match, or miss

Misses are as important as hits. A book that only shows confirmations is astrology marketing. A book that reports honestly is a grammar in action — attending to the sky and seeing what responds.

## Part IV: The Living Experiment

### What We're Testing

The design experiment tests a specific claim from Grammars Ch 7: "when you attend to Saturn — in your chart, in the sky, in your experience of limitation and discipline and time — something responds."

If the transits consistently map onto the colonial/decolonial arc, that is evidence (not proof) that the grammar has descriptive power. If they don't, that is equally important — it reveals where the grammar's vocabulary fails.

### What Constitutes a "Match"

- **Strong match:** The hypothesized cycle is present AND the sign placement adds meaning (e.g., Saturn-Pluto in Capricorn for institutional restructuring)
- **Partial match:** The hypothesized cycle is present but the sign doesn't add obvious meaning, OR a different cycle is present that fits better
- **Miss:** No relevant outer planet aspect is active. The sky is "quiet" during a major historical event.
- **Surprise:** An aspect is present that was NOT hypothesized but fits better than what was expected

Misses and surprises are the most valuable findings. They prevent the project from becoming confirmation bias with charts.
