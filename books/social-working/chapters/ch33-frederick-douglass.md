# Frederick Douglass

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What does it mean to make yourself when the world unmakes you?

## Source

Chapman & Withers: Douglass represents the erasure of Black leadership from 'social work history.' His organizing, mutual aid networks, and community building were 'social working' before the term existed.

## Context

Self-liberated, self-educated, self-made. Escaped slavery, became leading abolitionist orator. Supported women's suffrage—rare among men of his era. His autobiography was act of resistance.

## Key Insight

Douglass's work—speaking tours, newspapers, mutual aid—was community organizing and social change work. But because it wasn't in white settlement houses, it's not called 'social work.'
