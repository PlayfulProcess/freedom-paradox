# Mary Ann Shadd Cary

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What hope sustains when the journey has no guaranteed end?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (Chapter 2): Documents how Black women's media activism—community organizing through journalism—was a form of 'social working' that predated and paralleled white settlement houses.

## Context

First Black woman newspaper editor in North America. Published Provincial Freeman in Canada, advocating for fugitive slaves and integration. Lawyer, educator, abolitionist.

## Key Insight

Shadd Cary's journalism was mutual aid through media—creating community, sharing resources, building networks for fugitives. This is social work history that textbooks erase.
