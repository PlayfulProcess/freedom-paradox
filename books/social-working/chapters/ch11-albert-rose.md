# Albert Rose

*Standard_Account*

## Reflection

What dies when experts decide communities must be 'renewed'?

## Source

Rose claimed Africville residents were 'ready and to some extent eager to negotiate a settlement.' Chapman & Withers note: 'It wasn't true that people welcomed the relocation; many were adamantly opposed to it' (70). Rose received the Canadian Centennial Medal in 1967, 'during the time of active destruction' (70).

## Context

Director of University of Toronto School of Social Work; president of Canadian Association of Social Workers. 'The driving force behind the construction of Canada's first major low-rent rehousing project, Regent Park' (62). In 1963, 'visited Africville for two hours' and wrote a report justifying the relocation (69).
