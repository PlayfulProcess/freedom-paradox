# Booker T. Washington

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

When is patience wisdom? When is it collaboration?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (48): Washington's approach represented one pole of Black anti-racist strategy—work within the system, build institutions, accommodate to survive. Du Bois and Wells represented another.

## Context

Accommodation politics. Advocated 'assimilative measures, aimed at integrating poor people into ruling class values and mores' (Chapman & Withers, 48). Critiqued by W.E.B. Du Bois and Ida B. Wells.

## Key Insight

The Washington-Du Bois debate isn't just historical—it's the perennial question: work within broken systems or build alternatives? Both strategies have costs; both have been used by those in power to divide movements.
