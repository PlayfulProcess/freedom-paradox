# Henry Bibb

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What would you risk everything to return to?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (Chapter 2): Bibb's work with fugitive settlements represents 'newcomer settlement social work'—mutual aid among the displaced—that predates white settlement houses but carries 'colonizer contradiction' in its land claims.

## Context

Escaped slavery multiple times trying to free his family. Founded Voice of the Fugitive newspaper in Canada. Helped build fugitive settlements—mutual aid communities for escaped slaves.

## Key Insight

Fugitive settlements were doing 'settlement work' before Jane Addams—but this complicates the narrative because they also displaced Indigenous peoples. All are complicit.
