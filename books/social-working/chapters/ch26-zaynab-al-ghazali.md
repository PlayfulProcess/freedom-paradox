# Zaynab al-Ghazali

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What becomes visible only from a position of surrender?

## Source

Chapman & Withers: The 'standard account' centers Anglo-American secular social work. Al-Ghazali represents a tradition of Islamic community care that predates and parallels Western models but is invisible in textbooks.

## Context

Founded Society of Muslim Ladies in Egypt (1936). Imprisoned and tortured by Nasser regime for organizing. Represents global, non-secular tradition of community care that the 'standard account' ignores.

## Key Insight

Global community care traditions exist outside the Anglo-American secular frame—but are erased from 'social work history.'

## Archetypal Resonances

The Hanged Man, Strength, The High Priestess
