# Oscar McCulloch

*Standard_Account*

## Reflection

What cycles of harm continue from research done with good intentions?

## Source

McCulloch compared a poor family to 'a crustacean which has left the free, independent life common to its family, and is living as a parasite, or pauper' (35). His answer to 'What can we do?': 'First, we must close up official out-door relief. Second, we must check private and indiscriminate benevolence… Third, we must get hold of the children' (35).

## Context

Founder and leader of the Indianapolis COS. Published 'The Tribe of Ishmael: A Study in Social Degradation' at the 1888 National Conference of Charities and Corrections (35). 'One of the earliest to instill fear in the general public about both indiscriminate relief and the eugenic threat that poor, disabled, and racialized people posed' (35).
