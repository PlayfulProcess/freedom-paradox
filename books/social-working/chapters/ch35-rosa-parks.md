# Rosa Parks

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What gets remembered as spontaneous that was actually organized?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (74-75): 'Like all social justice movements, the Montgomery bus boycott was built on painstaking organizing efforts and the infrastructure developed through these efforts by generations of people struggling for social justice. The bus boycott wasn't a spontaneous explosion.'

## Context

Trained NAACP organizer who attended Highlander Folk School. Not 'just tired'—strategically chosen to re-stage what Claudette Colvin had done nine months earlier. Movement leaders decided Colvin 'was not the right person to symbolically galvanize a mass movement' (Chapman & Withers, 74).

## Key Insight

Angela Davis: 'We shouldn't remember these histories through the lens of individualism' (74). The movement needed both the infrastructure AND the symbolic spark.
