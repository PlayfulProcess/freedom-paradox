# George Edmund Haynes

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

Can justice be built within unjust institutions?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (Chapter 2): Haynes built Black social work education while white institutions excluded Black students. The 'standard account' credits white founders while erasing those who trained generations of Black practitioners.

## Context

First Black person to earn PhD from Columbia (1912). Co-founded National Urban League. Created the first training program for Black social workers at Fisk University.

## Key Insight

Haynes founded institutions to train Black social workers because white schools wouldn't. This parallel infrastructure is invisible in 'social work history.'

## Archetypal Resonances

Justice, The Hierophant, The Magician
