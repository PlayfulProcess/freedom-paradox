# Maggie L. Walker

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What resources do you have that systems refuse to recognize?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (Chapter 2, 74-75): Walker and the Independent Order of St. Luke 'led a streetcar boycott against racial segregation in 1903'—one of many acts of resistance that built the infrastructure for later movements.

## Context

First woman bank president in America (1903). Founded St. Luke Penny Savings Bank for Black community financial autonomy. Also led a streetcar boycott against racial segregation in Richmond, Virginia.

## Key Insight

Walker's bank and boycotts were 'newcomer settlement social work'—but routinely left out of credit union and social work history. Financial autonomy as resistance.
