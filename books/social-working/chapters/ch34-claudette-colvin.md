# Claudette Colvin

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

Whose determination is remembered? Whose is erased?

## Source

Angela Davis reminds us 'we shouldn't remember these histories through the lens of individualism. Bus boycotts could have been mobilized around Colvin's arrest' (74). The movement was 'built on painstaking organizing efforts and the infrastructure' of collective action (75).

## Context

'Nine months before Rosa Parks made her famous stand... Claudette Colvin... refused to move from her seat on a bus' (73-74). Chapman & Withers discuss how movement leaders decided 'she was not the right person to symbolically galvanize a mass movement' (74).
