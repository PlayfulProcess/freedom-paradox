# Jeffery Brackett

*Standard_Account*

## Reflection

What is named into existence? What is named out?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (43): 'According to this origin story, Brackett came up with the term to imply that the new profession was "orderly, responsible, and disciplined"—exactly what liberals normatively say of the innovations of early social work.'

## Context

Credited with coining the term 'social work' in the early 1900s. 'He added the word work to differentiate professional practice from what he considered the often misguided and self-serving philanthropic activity of wealthy volunteers' (Chapman & Withers, 43).

## Key Insight

The naming itself was an act of power—separating 'professional' (legitimate) from 'volunteer' (misguided). Who gets to define what counts as real work?
