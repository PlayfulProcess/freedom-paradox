# Simon Patten

*Standard_Account*

## Reflection

Who teaches whom how to live?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (43-44): Unlike Brackett's COS-aligned version, Patten's term 'describes both COS and settlement workers, and is coined by someone who prefers the more politicized work of the settlement movement.'

## Context

Alternative origin story: credited with coining 'social worker.' Advocated consumption education for poor people. 'Organised leisure time would facilitate access for the poor to the "right" activities including daily consumption' (Chapman & Withers, 48).

## Key Insight

Two origin stories, two visions: Brackett's 'orderly, disciplined' professionalism vs. Patten's 'politicized' settlement work. The term's meaning was contested from birth.
