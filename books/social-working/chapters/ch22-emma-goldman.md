## Reflection

## Source

The book argues this complicates the idea that anyone can be 'always and only aligned with justice and the oppressed' (29). Goldman should be 'celebrated for [her] important work... But celebrating [her] work isn't best facilitated by positioning [her] as always and only on the side of justice' (28-29).

## Context

Anarchist revolutionary. Chapman & Withers note that 'a number of socialist and anarchist social justice activists, including Emma Goldman, were eugenicists' (28). Part of their argument that 'liberals and radicals alike, whether Marxist, feminist, anarchist, or other, tended to work for eugenic aims a century ago' (38).
