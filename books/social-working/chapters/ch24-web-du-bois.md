# W.E.B. Du Bois

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What does it mean to work within institutions you're critiquing?

## Source

Chapman & Withers (48): Du Bois and Wells represented a radical alternative to Washington's accommodation—but were 'political allies of Addams' despite her own contradictions.

## Context

NAACP co-founder. Along with Ida B. Wells, critiqued Booker T. Washington's 'assimilative measures, aimed at integrating poor people into ruling class values and mores' (Chapman & Withers, 48).

## Key Insight

The Du Bois-Washington debate prefigured later tensions: accommodation vs. confrontation, working within systems vs. building alternatives. Both were 'erased' from mainstream social work history.
