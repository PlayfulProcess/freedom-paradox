# Bayard Rustin

*Erased_Voices*

## Reflection

What work happens behind the scenes? Who gets credited?

## Source

Chapman & Withers' framework of 'interlocking oppressions' applies: Rustin was erased not for lack of contribution but because homophobia intersected with anti-Black racism. He did the work; others got the credit.

## Context

Chief organizer of the 1963 March on Washington. Deliberately marginalized within the movement for being openly gay. Pacifist who trained MLK in nonviolent tactics.

## Key Insight

Rustin organized the most famous civil rights event in history—but was hidden because of who he loved. This is 'interlocking oppression' in the movement itself.
