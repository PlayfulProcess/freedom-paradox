# Social Working: An Analeptic Deck

## The Verb, Not the Profession

**By [PlayfulProcess](https://playfulprocess.com) with Claude**

---

**Working thesis:** "The mainstream has never run clean." Social work as a profession has been complicit in the colonial project from its founding. But social working — the verb, the practice of communities caring for each other — predates and exceeds the profession. This book recovers the erased voices, names the complicity, and expands the circle to include more-than-human social working.

**Structure:** 76 cards across 8 suits:

1. **The Standard Account** — Canonical figures (Addams, Richmond, Pratt)
2. **Erased Voices** — Marginalized practitioners (Ida B. Wells, Du Bois, Maggie Walker)
3. **Sanitized Icons** — Figures stripped of complexity (MLK, Gandhi, Malcolm X, Che)
4. **The Cracks** — Indigenous/spiritual/everyday roles (grandmother, doula, preto velho)
5. **Contemporary Witnesses** — Alternative practitioners (Akomolafe, Andreotti)
6. **The Wicked History** — Oz canon deconstructed (Baum through Schwartz)
7. **The Rest of the Worlds** — More-than-human social working (animals, plants, fungi, minerals, elements, spirit/matter, epistemologies)
8. **Places of Care** — Historical sites of mutual aid (Quilombo Palmares, Toynbee Hall, Hull House, Highlander, terreiros, maroon communities, Africville)

**Core principle:** Neither demonize nor romanticize. Every figure is complicit. Decolonization requires recognizing interlocking systems of oppression even in liberatory movements.

**Connection to other books:** The MSW professional companion. Where Working Architecture is the practical manual for parents/teachers, Social Working is the critical examination of the professional field. Links to the colonial reflex analysis in Grammars and the separability thesis throughout.
